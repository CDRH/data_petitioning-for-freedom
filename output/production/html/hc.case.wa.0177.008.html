EXHIBIT E.

UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION.

In the Matter of the Application of Tonka Bajlo for the Writ of Habeas Corpus. )

2459 No. 1328

AFFIDAVIT OF FANY MADISON.

STATE OF WASHINGTON COUNTY OF KING ) SS.

Fany Madison residing at 810 Charles Street, Seattle, Washington, on oath deposes and says: That she is a resident of Seattle, King County, Washington, residing at 810 Charles Street, and has known Tonka Bajlo for the past nine months; that she lived across the street from her as a neighbor for the past three months; that during said time she was employed as housekeeper and cook at 815 Charles Street for some four or five Austrian laborers; that during the time I have known her I have never heard anything ill of her in any manner whatsoever; that I am a married woman and live with my husband at 810 Charles Street, Seattle, Washington; that I know it is customary for Austrian women and women from the old country among the class of people that I was raised with to accept employment as cooks and housekeepers for me who are employed as construction laborers on the streets of the cities and in the camps of railroad and logging companies; that employment of that character is looked upon by our people as honorable and one who accepts such employment is not looked upon with suspicion or subjected to criticism by her fellow countrymen; that when I first became acquainted with her my husband employed her to act as cook when we were married; that I know she has been ill while living at 815 Charles Street and that she was unable to do much work; that ever since I have known her she has been acting   as cook and housekeeper for Austrians who kept boarding houses, or rather boarded themselves; That I have never heard anything wrong about her connection with Paska Bilin, John Jurin, or any other man; that I only know her as a hard working woman earning her own living; that she has complained to me that her husband beat her and that she could not live with him because he beat her so often; that Tonka Bajlo's husband has visited my home at 810 Charles Street; that I run a boarding house and he is acquainted with boarders living in my house; that I heard him say he had beat his wife several times, but would not beat her any more if she would live with him; that my husband is a molder by trade and works for on 4th Avenue, Seattle, Washington; that I have been over to the home of Tonka Bajlo, 815 Charles Street, Seattle, Washington, several times and have been in the room she occupied and know that she had a room of her own; that there are two outside entrances to the house, one on one side of the house facing the alley, and another on the other side of the house which leads into the kitchen from the street; that Tonka Bajlo's room was the front room facing on the street, and the entrance to said room was through the next room to it, and from there on to the alley; that there are three bedrooms in the house and the kitchen; that the house is a small house in a regrade district and has been raised up.

Fany Madison.

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 15th day of April, 1913.

Chas. H. Ennis.
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
