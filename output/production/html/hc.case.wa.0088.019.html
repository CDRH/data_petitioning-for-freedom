Mr.. FARREN a witness called on behalf of the Respondent, after being duly sworn, testified as follows:

Q. [Gilman] Where do you reside? A. Tacoma.

Q. What is your business A. Policeman.

Q. How long have you been on the police force? A. I have been on the police force since the 25th of last May.

Q. Do you know the petitioner in this case? A. Yes.

Q. How long have you known her? A. I have known her something a little over six years, going on seven years.

Q. Do you know what her character and habits are with reference to sobriety?

A. Well, I have seen her drunk several times.

Q. Where have you seen her drunk? A. Tacoma.

Q. In what part of the streets, or in the house, or where? A. In the street.

Q. Did you ever have occasion to arrest her for drunkenness? A. Yes sir.

Q. When? A. Along in June. I helped arrest her.

Q. What was she doing at that time, how conducting herself? A. She was drunk and disorderly.

Q. Have you at any other time seen her drunk on the public streets in Tacoma?

A. I have I think seen her twice before that.

Q. Within what time? A. Within the last seven years.

Q. And this was within the last year that you arrested her for drunkenness?

A. Sometime last June, I could not exactly say.

Q. Do you know where she is living? A. Yes, I know the.

Q. Do you know who she is living with? A. I know the house where she is living.

Q. Who lives in the same house? A. Antone Patros, I believe is his name -- that he goes by.

Q. What kind of a looking man is he? A. He is heavy set man, a man I should judge would weigh 185 pounds.

Q. Have you seen him in the Court room this morning? A. I have.

Q. Is he such a man as Mr. Hopkins described in his testimony?

Mr. RONALD: We object.

The COURT: Describe him yourself Mr. witness.

A. He is a man I suppose -- I dont know that he is taller than I am, but he is as tall as I am and considerably heavier, dark curly hair, moustache.

Q. He is black, is he not? A. Yes sir.

Q. They are occupying the same house? A. They are living under the same roof.

Q. What is Antone's business, do you know? A. I have known him seven years and I never knew him to do anything but gamble. He did run some fish nets for a while. He never worked at them.

CROSS-EXAMINATION:

Q. [Ronald] You are a policeman now? A. Yes sir.

Q. Did you ever know Antone to do anything but gamble? A. That is all I ever knew him to do.

Q. Did you ever arrest him for it? A. No sir.

Q. Why did you not? A. Never got orders to arrest him.

Q. But you would arrest this poor woman, would you? A. That is what I am on the police force for.

Q. You are not on the police force to arrest gamblers? A. I arrest them whenever the chief orders me to.

Q. The Chief ordered you to arrest this woman? A. No sir.

Q. How came you to arrest her? without orders? A. I have orders to arrest all drunken and disorderly.

Q. But gamblers you have orders to let alone? A. No orders for gamblers at all.

Q. You have orders to enforce the ordinances have you? A. I have.

Q. Is not that against the city ordinances? A. I think it is.

Q. Why dont you enforce it? A. They have given no orders.

Q. Did you take an oath when you went on the police force? A. I did.

Q. An oath to enforce the ordinances of the city? A. Yes sir.

Q. Well sir you have not lived up to your oath, have you? A. I have lived up to it the best I knew how.

Q. You have deliberately allowed gamblers to go without arresting them have you? A. We had no orders.

Q. You deliberately allowed them to do it? A. Yes sir.

Q. In the face of the oath you have taken, do you consider sir that you have done your duty? A. I consider I have.

Q. You consider that you are perfectly an honest man after all that? A. I consider I am.

Q. You never gamble yourself? A. No sir.
