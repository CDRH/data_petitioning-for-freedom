  • Summary of Proceedings: Samuel Carr petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus to retrieve his two young sons, Harry and Herbert Carr, from William and Nina Helms, which was granted. A few years earlier, Samuel had placed the boys in the Home for the Friendless in Lincoln, Nebraska, from which institution the Helmses obtained custody of the two. The Helmses did not formally adopt Harry and Herbert, whom they put to work in their traveling minstrel show. After hearing the evidence, Harry and Herbert were released back into the custody of the Home for the Friendless. The judge ordered that Samuel could retrieve his children once he demonstrated that he could properly care for them and after he paid off his debt to the state. For background on this case, see the 15 and 16 November 1906 issues of the Hastings Daily Republican.
