







In the District Court of the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding
Terms at Seattle for the County of King.

In the matter of the application of Mary Ellen Lyts for a writ of Habeas Corpus.

To the Honorable Thomas Burke, Chief Justice of said District Court:

Now comes W. D. Lyts, the defendant in the writ granted herein, and for return thereto
respectfully submits to your honor:

I.

That he is the father of said Francis Walter Lyts

II.

That the said Francis Walter Lyts is five years of age

III.

That Mary Ellen Lyts the mother of said Francis Walter Lyts is not a fit person to
be given the care custody and education of said child

IV.

That the said Mary Ellen Lyts has not sufficient means to support maintain and educate
the said Francis Walter Lyts

V.

That on or about the first day of June 1888 the said Mary Ellen Lyts has abandoned



and deserted this defendant and refuses to return to the said defendant and took said
child with her hereby refuses to all of defendant to visit such child and is now notoriously
living in the home and house of one Fred Brooks

VI.

That the said Francis Walter Lyts is not restrained of his liberty, but is detained
by the said W. D. Lyts for the purpose of securing to the said Francis Walter Lyts
proper protection, maintenance, support and education

Meekester Lewis and Gillman
