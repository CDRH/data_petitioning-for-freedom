Exhibit "A"

In Justice Court of Columbia County Washington Territory before W H Kuhn Justice of the Peace

The Territory of Washington
vs
S.M. McFarland

To the Sheriff or any constable and the keeper of the County Jail [?] County

Whereas at a Justice Court held at my office in said County for the examination of S.M McFarland the above named defendant upon the written complaint under oath of M Cross charging that the said defendant SM McFarland on the 24th day of April 1888 within Columbia County aforesaid unlawfully did remove from the County of Columbia in said Territory certain personal property towit One brown mule about seven years old One brown mule about seven years old One set of team harness and one 3 1/2 inch Steel Skein Barn Wagon which said personal property the said SM McFarland had therefore towit On May 15th 1886 duly mortgaged by certain chattel mortgage bearing date on that day to M Cross to secure the payment by a certain promissory note therein set forth which said personal property was at the date of said mortgage situate in Columbia County aforesaid and that said property was so removed as aforesaid without the said mortgage being duly released and without the consent in writing or otherwise of said mortgage M Cross and after hearing the evidence adduced and the arguments of counsel it appears to the satisfaction of the Court that an offense against the Laws   of said Territory has been committed and that there is probable cause for charging the defendant with said offense and whereas the said SM McFarland has failed to give bail in the sum of Three hundred dollars for his appearance to answer at the next term of the District Court for said County as required by me

Therefore in the name of the United States you the said Sheriff or said Constable are commanded forthwith to convey and deliver the said SM McFarland to the said keeper and you the said keeper are commanded to receive the him the said SM McFarland into your custody in said jail and him there safely keep until here discharged by due course of law

Given under my hand May 9th 1888

[?] W H Kuhn
Justice of the Peace

Filed
May 12 1888
JW Jessel Clerk
By U.J. Ellis Deputy
