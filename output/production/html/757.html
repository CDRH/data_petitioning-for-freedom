  • Summary of Proceedings: George Rigby was arrested for vagrancy by Sheriff E. F. Brailey and brought to the Police Court. Under the testimony of Detective Savage, who claimed Rigby to be a thief with a bad record, Rigby was sentenced to 90 days in jail. On November 2, Rigby and attorneys, M. L. Beckwith and John M. Macfarland, petitioned for habeas. Rigby claimed that there was no evidence to support the charge, and that he was at home with money during the time of his arrest. His petition was granted by Judge Lee S. Estelle, but Rigby's fate is unknown.
