  • Summary of Proceedings: In 1911, the Yamhill County Court found William Gardner to be insane and committed him to the Oregon State Hospital. In 1915, James Walton, Jr., counsel for Gardner, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on Gardner's behalf, which was granted (another party had petitioned for habeas on behalf of Gardner in 1913). Records from both the insanity and habeas hearings reveal that Gardner believed his wife and daughter were unfaithful in their marriages and that he had threatened violence against all parties and himself. The cause was ultimately dismissed--the fate of Gardner unclear.
