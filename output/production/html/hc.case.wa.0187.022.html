UNITED STATES OF AMERICA. DISTRICT COURT FOR THE WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON. NORTHERN DIVISION.

In the matter of the petition of Marie Robina Grayson, for a Writ of Habeas Corpus. PETITIONERS BOND ON APPEAL BY THE GOVERNMENT. 2780

Iron

KNOW ALL MEN BY THESE PRESENTS That we Marie Robina Grayson, as principal, and J. L. Lyen and B. P. Castner, as sureties are held and firmly bound unto the United States of America, in the penal sum of $1000.00,for the payment whereof well and truly to be made in good and lawful money of the United States of America, we do bind ourselves, and each of our heirs, executors, administrators and assigns jointly and severally firmly by these presents.

Sealed with our seals and dated this 24th day of August, 1914.

The condition of the above obligation is such that where heretofore the above named principal, Marie Robina Grayson, duly filed her petition for the allowance of a Writ of Habeas Corpus, and thereupon such Writ issued to Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration directed and thereupon the said Henry M. White, made full return to said petition whereupon a hearing upon the petition, return of the respondent and petitioners reply, to such return, was duly had in the above entitled Court, and thereafter and on the 20th day of July, 1914, the above entitled Court in the above cause did make and file its Order upon such Writ of Habeas Corpus, discharging the above named principal, Marie Robina Grayson and releasing her from the custody of the said Henry M. White, by whom theretofore she had been held, and thereafter the United States did appeal from such Order and Judgment of this Court, in due form of law, to the United States Circuit Court of Appeals, for the Ninth Judicial Circuit, and this Court did make its order allowing such appeal, and directing that pending the said appeal and hearing, and determination thereof, the petitioner and principal Marie Robina -1-   Grayson should be enlarged upon executing a recognizance to the United States of America for her appearance to answer any Judgment in said matter given by the United States Circuit Court of Appeals for the Ninth Judicial Circuit.

NOW THEREFORE if the said Marie Robina Grayson shall appear, answer to and abide and Judgment or Order made in said matter, by the United States Circuit Court of Appeals for the Ninth Judicial Circuit, and shall render herself up in obedience to any Judgment or Order in said Circuit Court of Appeals in said matter, then this obligation to be null and void otherwise to be and remain of full force and virtue.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF We have hereunto set our hands and seals the day and year first above written.

Mary Robina Grayson
J L Lyen
B P Caster

SIGNED SEALED AND DELIVERED IN THE PRESENCE OF

A. D. Stevenson
[?] WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON.SS.

J.L. LYEN AND B. P. CASTNER first being duly sworn on their oaths do severally say, each for himself, and not one for the other, that they are the sureties named in and by whom the foregoing instrument was executed, that they are residents of the County of Snohomish, in the District aforesaid; that they are severally worth the sum of $1000.00named in the within bond, above their just debts and liabilities, and exclusive of their property, exempt from execution, in separate property; that they are free holders of and in District aforesaid, and citizens thereof; that they are neither an attorney, sheriff, clerk of any court, or other officer of any Court.
