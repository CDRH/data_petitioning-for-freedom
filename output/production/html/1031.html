  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 9, 1918, John M. Burger made a complaint in the police court of Omaha, which charged Dick Kimball with the unlawful possession of intoxicating liquor. Kimball was charged with a misdemeanor, fined 100 or imprisonment for 30 days. On September 30, Kimball filed for habeas, arguing that such a charge was not a felony, making his punishment improper. The petition was granted October 1, but later dismissed.
