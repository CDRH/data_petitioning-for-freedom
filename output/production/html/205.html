  • Summary of Proceedings: Williams Alfredo petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus on May 18th, 1917, claiming that he was being unlawfully detained by Omaha police in the city jail. Alfredo's petition claimed that he was a stranger in the city and had not committed any crime. Judge Willis G. Sears granted his petition and issued a writ commanding Chief of Police Henry W. Dunn, Captain Heitfeldt, and Chief of Detectives Stephan Maloney to appear and show the time and cause of Alfredo's detention. Sheriff Michael L. Clark delivered the writ to Dunn; Heitfeldt and Maloney were not served. The outcome of the proceedings is unknown.
