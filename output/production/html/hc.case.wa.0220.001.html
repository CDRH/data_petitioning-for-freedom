IN THE DISTRICT COURT OF THE UNITED STATES FOR THE WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION.

IN THE MATTER OF THE APPLICATION OF ELSIE MILLER DICKINSON, ALIAS ELSIE MILLER, for a WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS.

NO. 2965 BOND.

KNOW ALL MEN BY THESE PRESENTS: That we, Elsie Miller Dickinson, alias Elsie Miller, as principal, and Samuel Fox and Max Esfeld, as sureties, are held and firmly bound unto the United States of America, in the full sum of one thousand dollars ($1000). lawful money of the United States, for the payment of which sum well and truly to be made, we hereby bind ourselves, our heirs, executors and administrators firmly by these presents.

DATED at Seattle, Washington, February 18, 1915.

THE CONDITION of this obligation is such that whereas the deportation of the principal above named was heretofore ordered by Secretary of the United States Department of Labor, and whereas, said principal, in the above entitled proceeding, petitioned for a Writ of Habeas Corpus in the above entitled court, and whereas, said court heretofore issued a Writ of Habeas Corpus directed to Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, commanding and enjoining him to produce before said court on the 24th day of February, 1915, the body of said principal,

NOW, THEREFORE, if the said Elsie Miller Dickinson, alias Elsie Miller, shall appear in said court upon the return date of said writ or at such other dates as the same may be continued to, and until the final order said court in the 1   premises, and shall obey all the orders of said court in the premises, and shall render herself into the custody of said Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, in event such Writ of Habeas Corpus is by said court discharged, then this obligation shall be null and void, otherwise to be and remain in full force and virtue.

Elesi Miller Dickinson
Samuel Fox
Sadie Fox
Max Esfeld.
Mrs. Esfeld. O.K. Allen US Atty 2  

UNITED STATES OF AMERICA WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION : SS

SAMUEL FOX, a surety on the annexed bond, being first duly sworn, upon oath deposes and says: That he is a surety on the foregoing obligation, which he executed as his free and voluntary act and deed for the uses and purposes therein mentioned; that he resides in Seattle, King County, Washington, and is worth in separate property the sum of two thousand dollars ($2000) over and above all his just debts and liabilities, and exclusive of property exempt by law from execution.

That his property consists of Lot fourteen (14), Block ten (10), Lakeside Addition to Seattle, of the reasonable value of one thousand five hundred dollars ($1500),

ALSO personal property consisting of stock in the Northwestern Junk Company of the reasonable value of ten thousand dollars ($10,000).

Samuel Fox

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 18th day of February, A. D. 1915.

Ed M Lakin
Deputy Clerk, U. S. Dist. Court, Western Dist. of Washington
