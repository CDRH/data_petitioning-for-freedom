  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 20, 1903, a subpoena was served upon Branch directing him to appear before Calvin C. Valentine, a notary public in Douglas county. Branch was the secretary for the Business Men’s Association and was subpoenaed to provide insight into the case of William Halsted, et al. vs. the Omaha Waiters Union. He appeared but he did not produce the books and papers called in for the subpoena because he alleged they were taken from his possession by T. J. Mahoney (attorney for the Business Men’s Association) to investigate for the trial that Branch was subpoenaed for. Valentine issued a mittimus for his arrest and Branch petitioned that he was unlawfully detained because the notary public Valentine had no authority to arrest him for such a reason. Branch was later discharged from prison.
