  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 1, 1918, Henry Slack was arrested in his home and tried for vagrancy and disorderly conduct. On December 2, Slack filed for habeas charging that his arrest came without warrant and that the complaint did not provide reason for such charges. The petition was granted, but the case was dismissed for want of prosecution on January 26, 1920.
