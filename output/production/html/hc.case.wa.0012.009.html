In the Probate Court of Whitman County W.T.
In the matter of the adoption of Daisy Geneva Departes a minor

Territory of Washington
County of Whitman } ss

N.P. Hateley being duly sworn on oath preys that he is acquainted with Amos Gustin and Elizabeth Departes Catherine Gustin that said Amos Gustin is the owner of a farm in Nez Perce County Idaho Territory and has been for the past three years or there about wishing thereon with his farm[?] and has been until the past week been resident of said Idaho Territory and affiant believes that said Amos Gustin above from Idaho Territory is but temporary, if at all, and not for the purpose of changing his residence to Colfax except for temproary purposes

Affiant further says that in the summer months of 1887, affiant was at the home of said Amos Gustin and Catherine Gustin in Nez Perce County Idaho and in a conversation had with said Catherine Gustin, she said that she did not entend to keep the child Daisy Geneva Departes   but would deliever her up to her mother at any time she should desire to take her away

N.P. Hately

Subscribed & sworn to before me this 25th day of January A.D. 1888

Wm A Fuman

Justice of the Peace
