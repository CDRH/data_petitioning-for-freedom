  • Summary of Proceedings: According to Albert Davidson, Lizzie B. Cruse pretended to be an unmarried woman and pretended to be a resident of Douglas county and made a complaint to Edgar S. Bradley (justice of the peace for Douglas County), that she was pregnant with a bastard child and that Davidson was the father of said child. Bradley issued a warrant and delivered the same to B. F. Ford, a constable in Douglas County, commanding the arrest of Davidson. Ford arrested Davidson in his home city of Wymore and brought him to Douglas County and left him in the custody of George A. Bennett, the sheriff, and Joe Miller, the jailor. Cruse never presented her own side of the story, but the writ is allowed. Yet, a week later, the case was dismissed.
