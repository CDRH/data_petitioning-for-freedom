  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 9, 1881, Dan Sullivan was convicted of petty larceny in front of justice of the peace V.D. Lay and sentenced to 60 days in jail. He filed a petition for a writ of habeus corpus against sheriff David Marsh on June 13, 1881, claiming that his confinement was illegal because the writ of certiori wasn't directed at the sheriff specifically, it didn't specify the county or jail that Sullivan was to be imprisoned in, and Lay had exceeded his jurisdiction in incarcerating Sullivan. The writ was returned and filed on June 13, 1881, but the outcome is unknown.
