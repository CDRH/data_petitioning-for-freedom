  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 30, 1922, Robert H. Johnson filed a second habeas petition against the sheriff of Douglas County. In his petition, Johnson explained that a complaint had been filed against him under the charge of vagrancy, was found guilty, filed an appeal - which was dismissed - and is now under a new warrant without complaint. Johnson further sought to challenge the vagrancy ordinance, Ordinance No. 10916, and alleged, in a supplemental petition, that his arrests were political. Johnson explained that he had campaigned against City Prosecutor Dineen during an election, and that his vagrancy case and 60 others are now being dismissed. A writ was issued, sustained, and Johnson was ordered to be released on January 24, 1922.
