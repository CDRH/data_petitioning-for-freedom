  • Summary of Proceedings: On March 17th, 1919, William Wernsen was arrested in Omaha and sentenced to pay a fine of $100 for being convicted of vagrancy. Wernsen filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus, arguing that because the State of Nebraska has set the limit for vagrancy fines at $50, his imprisonment is unlawful. His petition was allowed and Juedge Redick ordered the Douglas County Sheriff to produce Wernsen in court on March 25th, 1919. The outcome of the suit was not filed with the rest of the case documents, however, the appearance docket reveals that Wernsen's writ was dismissed for want of prosecution and he remained in custody.
