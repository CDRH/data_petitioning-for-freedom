  • Summary of Proceedings: On April 7th, 1888, Joseph Deveriske was sentenced to imprisonment for the crime of larceny by J. R. Snyder, Justice of the Peace. On April 19th, 1888, Deveriske's wife, Betty Deveriske filed a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of her husband. She claimed that Joseph Deveriske was being illegally imprisoned, and that J. R. Snyder did not have the jurisdiction to imprison him. The same day, Judge Nash ordered the chief of police, Joseph Warren, to bring Joseph Deveriske to court the next day. The outcome of the writ and the fate of the bound party is unknown.
