In the District Court of the United States, In and For the Western District of Washington, Northern Division.

In the Matter of the Application of Marie Miler for Writ of Habeas Corpus. } No. 5 2457 Petiton for Habeas Corpus.

To the Honorable Edward E. Cushman, Judge of the above entitled Court:

The petition of Marie Miler respectfully shows:

I.

That she, the said Marie Miler, is a citizen of the Empire of Austria, a duly organized Empire of Europe, with treatise of peace, commerce and intercourse with the United States of America.

II.

That on or about the 1st day of August, 1909, the petitioner came to the United States, where she has resided since. That during all of said time she has conducted herself as a good citizen should do.

III.

That on or about the 1st day of January 1913, more than three years after her arrival in the United States, she was complained against by the immigration service at Seattle, Washington, under the direction of the Hon. Ellis DeBruler, United States Commissioner of Immigration. That certain proceedings were had on or about the 18th day of March, 1913, the petitioner was ordered deported and sent from the United States of America, all of which was without legal authority and in total disregard of the petitioner's rights as a resident of the United States, and without any authority vested in the Department of Immigration to deport the petitioner.

IV.

That at the hearing the petitioner was not represented[?]   by counsel. That she had retained counsel before the hearing and had paid him a reasonable and substantial fee, that he had failed to appear and represent her at any time during the proceeding and has not seen her or consulted with her since receiving his retainer from her. That she does not understand the English language and had no opportunity of protecting or knowing what her legal rights were. That the evidence on which she was ordered deported was a false affidavit which the applicant had no opportunity to controvert, owing to the fact that counsel whom she had retained did nothing after receiving compensation from her toward protecting her rights. That the affidavit was made by a party with who she had been in partnership in the City of Seattle and against who she had an motion pending for a recievership and accounting and that the affidavit and the charges preferred by that party and other conspirators likely interested with him, was a conspiracy on their part to get rid of the petitioner and acquire her property.

V.

That the petitioner as a citizen of the Empire of Austria, an Empire having treatise with the United States, is entitled to a trial in the civil and criminal courts of the United States of America, and which she has not had, and, as aforesaid, is about to be deported without due process of law as well an without being given an opportunity to be heard and based on a fraudulent conspiracy to force her out of the country and deprive her of her rights, both financial and physical.

VI.

That your applicant and petitioner is about to be deported on April 16th, 1913, and unless a writ of Habeas Corpus issues she will be deported and forever barred from having a hearing as to her legal rights, with due respect to her property and liberty, and in futherance of the conspiracy of her enemies.

 

VII.

That at the present time, preparatory to having her deported, the Hon. Ellis DeBruler, United States Commissioner of Immigration, is unlawfully detaining, imprisoning, and confining your said petitioner in the Dentention Station at Seattle, Washington, and refuses to relase her from custody, all of which is against her will, wishes, desire and against her legal rights.

Wherefore your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted to the Hon. Ellis DeBruler, U.S. Commissioner of Immigration, at Seattle, Washington, as aforesaid, commanding him to have the body of your petitioner, Marie Miler, before your Honorable Court at a time and place therein to be specified, to do an recieve what shall then and there be considered by your Honor concerning your said petitioner, Marie Miler, together with the time and cause of her detention and the said writ; and that she, the said Marie Miler, your petitioner, may be restored to her liberty.

Mari Miller
Petitoner. Elias A. Wright
Attorney for Petitioner.

United States of America,
District of Washington. } ss.

Marie Miler being first duly sworn on her oath deposes and says: That she is the petitioner above named; that she has read the foregoing petition, knows the contents thereof, and that the same is true.

Mari Miller

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 15th dat of April, 1913.

Elias A. Wright Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
