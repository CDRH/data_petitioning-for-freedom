In the District Court King County W.T.

In the matter of the application of Ah Young for writ of Habeas Corpus.

To the above named Court.

Comes now Ah Young your petitioner and respectfully represents.

I

That he your petitioner is restrained of his liberty by John H. McGraw Sheriff and Jailor of King County Washington Ty in the Jail of said County situate in the City of Seattle.

II

That the cause or pretense of said restraint is by reason of a pretended trial and conviction and sentence of your petitioner by one W. J. Shine[?] a Justice of the Peace for Kent precinct said County & territory of the alleged offense of "visiting and smoking and inhaling opium: by reason of which pretended   trial and conviction your petitioner was sentenced to pay a fine of fifty dollars by said Justice of the Peace and the costs of said pretended trial of $17.50 and was by order of said Justice ordered committed to the Jail of said County until said fine and costs were paid.

III

That said restraint and said trial conviction sentence and commitment are illegal in that said Justice of the Peace had no jurisdiction to try said def your petitioner for said offense.

Wherefore your petitioner prays that a writ of Habes Corpus issue that he may be brought before the Court and have and receive such as may be ordered concerning him

IV

That he makes this application in his own behalf

Ah Young his [?] mark
