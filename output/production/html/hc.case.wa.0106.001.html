In the District Court of the Second Judicial District of Washington Territory holding Terms at Olympia for the Counties of Cowlitz and Wahkiakum

John Seibert
Plaintiff
vs.
Sarah E. Seibert
Defendant} No 71 Complaint

John Seibert the above named Plaintiff complains of Sarah E. Seibert and for Cause of Action Alleges

That Plaintiff and defendant are husband and wife, that they were[?] married in San Francisco State of California on the 25th day of October A.D. 1876, and ever since same were and now are husband and wife.

That plaintiff has been a resident of Kalama, in the 2nd Judicial District of Washington Territory, for more than one year immediately presiding the Commencement of this action and that he now is a resident of   the District aforesaid

That on the [] day of December 1877 at the regular December term of the District Court of Washington Territory, holding terms at Olympia for the Counties of Chehalis, Mason Lewis and Thurston, and before this action was commenced, the said Defendant, Sarah E. Seibert was duly convicted of the Crime of Polygamy, and duly Sentenced by the said Court to Confinement in the penitentiary for the Term of Eighteen months and in pursuance of said Sentence the defendant is now Confined in the County Jail of Thurston County there being no Territorial penitentiary in the Territory of Washington.

Wherefore plaintiff prays that the bonds of matrimony between plaintiff and defendant be dissolved, and plaintiff have such other and further relief as may be equitable and just
