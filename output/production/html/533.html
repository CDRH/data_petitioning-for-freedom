  • Summary of Proceedings: Frank D. Soverign claimed that he was unlawfully jailed by Constable Robert Mitchell and petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. Justice of the Peace R. W. Taylor had ordered for Soverign to be jailed on the charge of "maliciously killing a wild bird" (a hawk). After hearing evidence, Judge Pound remanded Soverign to the custody of the jailer. The appearance docket records a Supreme Court mandate on July 22, 1878, but the outcome of that mandate is unknown.
