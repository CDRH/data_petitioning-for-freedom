  • Summary of Proceedings: In October 1890, M. O. Ricketts petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of a young man named Till, which was granted. Ricketts claimed that Till was being "held as a slave" in Fillmore County, Nebraska by a farmer named Milton Willerford. Willerford denied this, maintaining that he had not purchased Till and that he did not detain him against his will. Rather, Willeford asserted that Till, whose history the case file does not elucidate, had chosen to live with his family the past seven years. The cause was eventually dismissed by the mutual agreement of both parties. Till's fate is unknown.
