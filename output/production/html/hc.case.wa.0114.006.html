In the US District Court holding terms at Olympia- In the matter of the application of William Jones, William H. Doyle, Peter Anderson, Fred Williams and Alfred Woods for a writ of Habeas Corpus.

Order granting Writ

On reading and filing of the petition of the above named parties duly signed and verified whereby it appears that they and each of them are illegally imprisoned and restrained of their and each of their liberty by Jesse W. George U. S. Marshall in and for Washington Territory at the U. S. Pententiary situated on McNiels Island Pierce County W. T. and stating wherein the illegality consists, from which it appears to me that a writ of Habeas Corpus ought to issue.

Therefore it si ordered that a writ of Habeas Corpus issue out of and under the seal of the U. S. District Court holding terms at Olympia, directed to the said Jesse W. George U. S. Marshall commanding him to have the bodies and each of them of William Jones, William H. Doyle, Fred Williams and Alfred Woods, before me in the court room at Olympia, W. T., on the 10" day of March, 1885, at the hour of 1 o'clock in the after noon of that day to do and receive what shall then and there be considered concerning the said above named Petitioners, together with the time and cause of their detention and that he have then and there the said Writ.

Olympia W.T. March 6th 1885

John P. Hoyt Associate justce of the S.C. and Judge of the 2nd. District of W. T.
