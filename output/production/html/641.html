  • Summary of Proceedings: Walter Versteeg claims that he was deprived of his liberty by Martin J. White and H. P. Haze in the city jail in Omaha. He has been detained in the jail but there is no complaint against him whatsoever. Judge Baker affirmed an original writ of Habeas Corpus, ordering that he be discharged from being restrained of his personal liberty. The state recognized that they had received copies of the writ and that they had personally delivered the writ to H. P. Haze. The District Court Judge, W. W. Slabaugh, under the Clerk Albyn L. Frank and the Deputy A. Steere, Jr., ordered that White and Haze bring both Versteeg and documentation of their cause for his detention to court on 29 September 1898. According to the Appearance Docket, the writ was returned on 7 October 1898, signed by John W. McDonald (Sheriff).
