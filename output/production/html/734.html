  • Summary of Proceedings: In April 1878, Henry Palm was arrested in Olympia, Washington, charged with murder, and jailed to await a grand jury trial. In May 1878, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that he should not be jailed for eight months to await the next convening of the grand jury. The judge granted his petition, but the fate of Palm is unknown.
