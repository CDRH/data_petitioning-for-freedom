  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 21st, 1919, the bound party, John Sullivan, was arrested for fast and reckless driving. After being formally charged, the bound party was found guilty and sent to jail for 30 days. On February 27th, a petition was filed alleging that the government attorney, Murray, altered documents in the course of the trial without re-swearing testifiers or re-arraigning Sullivan. The writ was issued and on March, 3rd the realtor was discharged on a bond.
