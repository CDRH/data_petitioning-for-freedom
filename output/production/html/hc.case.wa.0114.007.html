In the United States District Court holding Terms at Olympia.

In the matter of
the application of
William Jones, William H. Doyle, Fred Williams and Alfred Woods,
Writ of Habeas Corpus.

The President of the United States:
To Jesse W. George U. S. Marshall Greeting:

In the name of the United States We hereby command you that you have the body of William Jones, William H. Doyle, Fred Williams and Alfred Woods by you imprisoned and detained, as it is said, together with the time and cause of such imprisonment and detention, by whatsoever names said William Jones, William H. Doyle, Fred Williams and Alfred Woods shall be called or charged, before John P. Hoyt, Esquire, Associate Justice of the Supreme Court and Judge of the Second Judical District of Washington Territory, holding terms at Olympia, at the Court room at Olympia on the 10th day of March 1885 at the hour of one o'clock in the afternoon of said   day to do and receive what shall then and there be considered concerning the said William Jones William H Doyle Fred William and Alfred Woods, And have you then and there this writ.

Witness the Honorable John P Hoyt Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of said Territory and Judge of said District Court and the seal of said Court at Olympia this 6th day of March A.D. 1885.

RG OBrien
Clerk
