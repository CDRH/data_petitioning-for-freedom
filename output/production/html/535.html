  • Summary of Proceedings: Arrested and convicted in the District Court of Sheridan County on the charge of grand larceny for stealing a cow, Herman Granger petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that Warden of the Nebraska State Penitentiary Leidigh was unlawfully detaining him. He had previously appealed to the Nebraska State Supreme Court, who had upheld the verdict. Granger argued that he was imprisoned under the NE House of Representatives bill no. 87 "an act to punish cattle stealing..." when the bill had not passed, making his incarceration unconstitutional. Judge Holmes granted the petition. After hearing evidence, Holmes denied the writ and remanded Granger back into the custody of the warden.
