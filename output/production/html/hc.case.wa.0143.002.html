In the District Court of Washington Territory within and for the 4th Judicial District thereof, holding terms at Cheney in Spokane County for the Counties of Spokane Lincoln and Douglas-

The Territory of Washington
vs.
Franklin Bearden}

To the Honorable George Turner Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of Washington Territory — Your petitioner S.C. Hyde Prosecuting Attorney for the District composed of the Counties of Spokane Lincoln and Douglas respectfully represents and shows unto your honor that one Sterling P. White who is now confined in the Territorial Prison at Seatco in Thurston

 

County under a sentence for the term of two years upon a conviction of the crime of Manslaughter is and will be a material witness for the Territory in the case of the Territory of Washington v. Franklin Beardon, now pending for trial in the District Court holding terms at Cheney for the counties of Spokane Lincoln and Douglas, at the adjourned[?] term of said Court to be held on the 2nd day of November next. Your petitioner further represents and shows unto your honor that he cannot safely proceed to the trial of said Court on the part of the Territory without the testimony of said Sterling P. White as he verily believes.

S.C. Hyde,
Prosecuting Attorney

 

Territory of Washington County of Spokane} ss

S.C. Hyde the above named petitioner being duly sworn, on his oath says: that the facts set before in the foregoing petiton are true.
S.C. Hyde

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 12th day of October A.D. 1885

J.S. Allen
Notary Public

Allowed the 12th day of October A.D. 1885 on the application of S.C. Hyde Esq. Prosecuting attorney for the District comprised of the counties of Spokane Lincoln and Douglas.

George Turner
Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of Washington. Ty.
