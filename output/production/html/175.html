  • Summary of Proceedings: Charles Willard was accused of embezzlement in Michigan. He fled to Nebraska, where he was arrested on an extradition warrant. Willard petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, the writ was denied and Willard remanded to the custody of his jailer. He appealed to the state supreme court, which upheld the decision of the lower court.
