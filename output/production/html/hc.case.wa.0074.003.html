City of Seattle vs Lee Kee }

Order Habeas Corpus

On reading and filing the petition of Lee Kee duly signed and verified on his behalf, whereby it appears that he is illegally imprisoned and restrained of his liberty by James H Woolery chief of Seattle Police and James Bogart in the City Jail, the said James Bogart being jailor of said city of Seattle, in the County of King and Territory of Washington, and stating wherein the illegallity consists, from which it appears to me, that a writ of Habeas Corpus ought to issue.

It is ordered that a writ of Habeas Corpus issue out of and under the seal of the District Court of the 3rd Judicial District of Washington Territory holding terms at Seattle, Commanding them to have the body of the said Lee Kee before me in the Court room in the said Court on the 27th day of May AD 1885 at 5 o'clock P.M. of said day to do and receive what shall then and there be considered concerning   the said Lee Kee together with the time and cause of his detention and that they have then and there the said writs

Dated on the 26th day of May AD 1885

Greene

4437

In District Court, 3rd Judicial District of Washington Territory holding terms at Seattle June Application of Lee Kee for a writ of habeas corpus

Order granting writ FILED MAY 27 1885 James Seavey, Clerk. By James Kudlow Deputy Clerk.
