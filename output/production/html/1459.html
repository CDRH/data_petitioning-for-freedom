  • Summary of Proceedings: In September 1919, according to some surviving court records, the Umatilla County Circuit Court sentenced W. Burlingame to serve at least ten but not more than twenty years in the Oregon State Penitentiary for "obtaining money by false pretenses." In November 1920, his next friend, Nettie Patterson, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on his behalf, which was granted. Patterson maintained that the maximum sentence for "obtaining money by false pretenses" was five years under Oregon law, meaning Burlingame's commitment was illegal. During the proceedings, the Umatilla County district attorney submitted an affidavit that Burlingame had not, in fact, been convicted of "obtaining money by false pretenses," but rather "knowingly uttering and publishing a forged customer's draft," which did carry a sentence of at least ten but not more than twenty years. After hearing the evidence, Burlingame was remanded back into the custody of the penitentiary warden.
