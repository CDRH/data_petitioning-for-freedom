In the District Court of the United States for the Western District of Washington Northern Division

In the Matter of the Application of Nora Sullivan, for a Writ of Habeas Corpus.

No. 4764

Return

Comes now Henry M. White, as Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, Washington, and for return to the petition and order to show cause herein:

I.

Denies each and every allegation of the I., II., III., VI., IV., VII., and VIII. paragraphs of said petition, except that the petitioner Nora Sullivan is an alien, and denies each and every allegation of paragraph V. of said petition, except that the petitioner at one time was an inmate of Warmsprings Hospital, near Butte, Montana.

For a further return, the Commissioner of Immigration denies that he has any further knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to any of the allegations set forth in the affidavit of Mary Sullivan attached to the petition herein.

For a further return the Commissioner of Immigration says:

I.

That respondent Henry M. White is now and at all times herein mentioned and in the petition herein has been United States Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, Washington.

-1-  

II.

That he holds the said petitioner Nora Sullivan in custody by virtue of a certain warrant issued by the Secretary of Labor of the United States, a copy of which is hereto attached, marked Exhibit "A" and made a part hereof as if herein set forth at length.

John H Sargent
Asst. Commissioner of Immigration.

Exhibit A

United States of America

Department of Labor

Washington

No. 54251/7

Incl. 5560

To FREDERIC C. HOWE, Commissioner of Immigration, Ellis Island, N.Y.H. WHEREAS, from proofs submitted to me, after due hearing before Immigrant Inspector Thomas Topping, held at Warmsprings, Montana. I have become satisfied that the alien NORA (or NORAH Sullivan, who landed at the port of New York, N.Y., ex SS "St. Louis", on the 16th day of July, 1916, has been found in the United States in violation of the immigration act of February 5, 1917, to-wit:

That she became a public charge in the Montana State Hospital for the Insane, Warmsprings, Montana, within five years after her entry into the United States from causes not affirmatively shown to have arisen subsequent thereto; and that she was a person likely to become a public charge at the time of her entry, and may be deported in accordance therewith:

I, JOHN W. ABERCROMBIE, Acting Secretary of Labor, by virtue of the power and authority vested in meby the the laws of the United States, do hereby command you to return the said alien to the country whence she came, at the expense of the steamship company importing her.

For so doing, this shall be your sufficient warrant.

Witness my hand and seal this 9th day of March, 1918.

(Department of Labor SEAL United States of America)

John W. Abercrombie,

Acting Secretary of Labor.
