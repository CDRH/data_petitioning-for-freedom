  • Summary of Proceedings: A complaint was made by J. B. Murray on October 22, 1918, against Charles Ferguson for possession of intoxicating liquor. Ferguson was found guilty and charged $100 and costs of prosecution or to stand committed until fine be paid. Ferguson filed for habeas with attorney Daniel Horrigan on October 24, charging that the court does not have a charge for such offense under Nebraska laws. The petition was granted and the writ sustained; Ferguson was released from custody October 26.
