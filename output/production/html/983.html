  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 3, Pierce H. O’Dell petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus to Judge L.D Day, arguing Sheriff Michael L. Clark was unlawfully depriving him of his liberty. He claimed that on September 27, 1922, the police arrested him, and the court tried him in the municipal court before Judge George Holmes. He was bound over to the district court of Douglas County under a $2000 bond. However, O’Dell argued that the clerk had not filed a transcript of the proceeding. he contended that a complaint was filed against him without the knowledge or consent of the county attorney of Douglas County or any office forces under the county attorney. Yet the court charged him with a felony. The next day Judge Day accepted and filed a writ of habeas corpus to Sheriff Clark. However, the outcome of this proceeding is unknown.
