  • Summary of Proceedings: Jim Jones was arrested for drunkenness and disturbing the peace on January 26th, 1916. He was ordered to stay in jail for 60 days and pay a $200 fine. The Police Magistrate asked for an extra $3.50 to assure his appeal was submitted. Jim Jones petitioned for Habeas corpus on February 3rd, 1916, due to the extra money demanded. The petition was approved on February 7th, 1916, and appearance in court was ordered. There is no information about the hearing or outcome.
