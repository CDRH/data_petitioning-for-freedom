IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT FOR THE WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON, NORTHERN DIVISION

IN THE MATTER OF THE APPLICATION OF GENEVIEVE WILDS FOR A WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS} 3895 PETITION.

The undersigned petitioner, Genevive Wilds, of Seattle, King County, Washington, respectfully shows to this Honorable Court that she is imprisoned and restrained of her liberty by J.S. McBride, City Health Officer of the City of Seattle, and is confined and imprisoned in the County-City Building in Seattle, against her will and protests; that she has not been committed or detained by virtue of any process of any final judgemnt or decree of any competent Tribunal, Court or Judge of Civil or Criminal jursdiction or by virtue of any execution, warrant, writ or process of any kind whatsoever; that the cause or pretense of her restraint and imprisonment, according to the best knowledge and belief of petitioner is an order issued by said J.S. McBride Health Officer of the City of Seattle, directing that petitioner be held in restraint and confinement for a period of from one to three months in order that she might receive treatment for a disease which said J.S. McBride alleges and pretends that petitioner is afflicted with; that as a matter of fact, the petitioner is not afflicted with any disease whatsoever, and no reason for holding the petitioner for said treatment exists. That peitioner has not been exposed to any contageous or dangerous or infectious disease and there is no danger of petitioner communicating any disease of any kind to the public or to any persons whith whom she may come in contact.

That said J.S. McBride pretends to have power and authority to hold and imprison petitioner as above set forth under and by virtue of Ordinance No. 37928, passed by the City Council   of the City of Seattle in the month of November, 1917; that as a matter of fact, said Ordinance is invalid because the provisions thereof are contrary to the constitution of the United States and constitution and laws of the State of Washington, and because no valid cause or reason exists, so far as petitioner is concerned, for exercising any power such as are pretended to be conferred by said Ordinance.

That petitioner is not permitted to receive visitors or to use the telephone, or talk with her friends or her family except that between the hours of 2 and 4 each day visitors are permitted to talk to petitioner through a wire netting for five minutes at a time; that petitioner is not being properly cared for and does not recive proper food, nor considerate or proper treatment; that petitioner is forced to come in contact with persons of vicious character and morals, afflicted with various infectious diseases, dangerous to the health of petitioner; that the imprisonment and restraint of petitioner are illegal and contrary to the provisions of the constitution of the United States and petitioner has no adequate remedy, except through a Writ of Habeas Corpus.

WHEREFORE, your petitioner prays that a writ of habeas corpus may issue directed to the said J.S. McBride, commanding him that he have the body of said petitioner, by him imprisoned and detained, together with a full report and return, setting forth the time, cause and authority of such imprisonment and detention, before this Honorable court, and that this petitioner be permitted to then and there offer evidence and testimony in regard to the facts in relation to the detention and imprisonment of petitioner, and that this court then and there enter such judgment or order as shall be just and equitable under the laws and statutes of the United States and the State of Washington

Genevieve Wilds
Petitioner

 

State of Washington County of King} ss.

Genevieve Wilds being first duly sworn on oath deposes and says; that she is the petitioner herein; that she has read the foregoing petition knows the contents thereof and belives the same to be true.

Genevieve Wilds

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 16th day of Febuary, 1918

Eugene A.Childe,
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
