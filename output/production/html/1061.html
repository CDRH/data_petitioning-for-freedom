  • Summary of Proceedings: D. O. Belt, the petitioner, filed for a writ of Habeas Corpus, as he was deprived of his liberty due to extradition papers from Kentucky stating that he had obtained money by false pretenses. Belt states that the documents do not say he is a fugitive from justice or charge a crime against him. Belt states that he agreed with a company to sell them hemp for a specific amount but then decided not to sell or take money from the company. R.C. Morgan & Company members state that Belt had indicated that he owned lots of hemp and was willing to deliver to Lexington and that they had paid $712 and then received no hemp. Judge Troup of Nebraska finds D. O. Belt to be deprived of his liberty.
