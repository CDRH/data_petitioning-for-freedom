  • Summary of Proceedings: William Wiggins was charged with grand larceny for taking money from the Occidental Hotel safe through a complaint brought by the petitioner, William Fox. After Wiggins was found not guilty in his case, William Fox was charged with the court costs. Fox was unable to pay and detained and sentenced to forty-one (41) days of hard labor for refusal to pay a fine. Afterward, Fox filed for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing his complaint against Wiggins was not frivolous, and, therefore he should not be charged with the court costs. Judge J. R. Lewis granted the writ, and Fox was released.
