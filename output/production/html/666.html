  • Summary of Proceedings: Accused of embezzlement, Thomas Van Sciever was arrested on the order of Lorenzo Crouse, governor of Nebraska. Crouse received orders for extradition from W. H. Auble, agent of the state of California on behalf of W. H. Markham, governor of California. Thomas Van Sciever, and his agent and wife Addie Van Sciever, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that chief of police P. H. Cooper and agent W. H. Auble were depriving him of his liberty. Judge Hall granted the petition. After hearing evidence, Judge Tibbetts remanded him to the custody of Sherriff Miller. Van Sciever filed a writ of error and appealed to the Nebraska Supreme Court, who in 1897 affirmed the decision.
