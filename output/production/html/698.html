  • Summary of Proceedings: In September 1913, Joseph Kopf was arrested in California and extradited to Nebraska to answer a charge of first degree murder in Nehama County. Kopf was accused of having bludgeoned a man to death during a robbery gone wrong in 1899 and subsequently fleeing the state in 1902. Kopf petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in federal court, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, he was remanded to the custody of his jailer.
