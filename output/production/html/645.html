  • Summary of Proceedings: O. J. Vallecott, relationship to bound party unknown, petitioned on behalf of Benjamin D. Mills claiming that an unidentified John Doe had illegally deprived Mills of his liberty. Arrested on the charge of embezzlement and sentenced to serve time in the state penitentiary, Mills had appealed his case to the NE Supreme Court, who suspended his sentence and allowed him to enter into bail. Vallecott claimed that the order from the Supreme Court supersedes any order that John Doe was acting upon. Judge Holmes granted the petition. Half a year later, the court ordered that the action be dismissed. Fate of bound party is unknown.
