  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 24th, 1888, J. R. Snyder issued the arrest of B. Norman. Snyder claimed that Norman had violated an ordinance that was passed in 1837, which established the fire limits in Spokane. On that same day, B. Norman filed a writ of habeas corpus, claiming that he was being wrongfully imprisoned by J. F. Warren, Spokane Chief of Police. The next day, Judge George Turner ordered Snyder to bring B. Norman to court. The outcome of the writ and the fate of the bound party is unknown.
