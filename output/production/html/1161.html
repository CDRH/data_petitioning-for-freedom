  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 6, 1922, around 4 PM, Peter Nelson was taken into custody by the Omaha police. He had no idea why. His attorney, B.W. Gill, could find no warrant for his arrest, although the police claimed that they were detaining him for further investigation. On July 9, his attorney filed a petition for a writ of habeus corpus demanding that chief of police Michael J. Dempsey, police commissioner Henry W. Dunn, and captain of detectives Charles Van Deusen produce Nelson in front of judge Carroll O. Stauffer. The writ was served to Dempsey, but not Dunn or Van Deusen. On that same day, a hearing was held, and Gill's petition was denied, with Nelson responsible for court costs. Whether Nelson ultimately remained in prison is unknown.
