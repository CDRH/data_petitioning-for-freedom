  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 2nd 1918, petitioner Roy McAllister was charged and allegedly sentenced in absentia by a police magistrate for the crime of having a social disease. Michael Clark, Sheriff for Douglas County, then took custody of McAllister. On July 25th 1918, a petition was filed by McAllister and his attorney Richard S. Horton. That writ was granted on July 26th 1918 and on August 5th 1918, the writ was allowed and the bound party was discharged from custody.
