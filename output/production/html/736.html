  • Summary of Proceedings: One May 13, 1918, a complaint was brought against eighteen-year-old, Harold Kent, for disorderly conduct. In the municipal court, Kent was sentenced to 90 days of service in the county jail. On May 16, 1918, Kent's father, J. W. Kent, filed for habeas against the county sheriff, Michael Clark, arguing that no offense for such charge can be found under the statutes of Nebraska. A writ was issued and sustained; Kent was released same day.
