  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 20, 1922, Marie Philbrick filed habeas against the superintendent and manager of the St. James Orphanage, Julius J. Hettwer, over the custody of her three children, Beatrice, Robert, and Kathleen. In her petition, Philbrick argued that she has and continues to hold full custody over the children, according to a divorce decree, and that the children were placed with the Orphanage under the condition that Philbrick maintains all legal and full custody over the children. Furthermore, Philbrick argues that Hettwer is refusing visitation with and access to the children. A writ was issued and sustained, and on June 21, 1922, the judge ordered the children to be released into custody of Philbrick.
