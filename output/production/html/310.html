  • Summary of Proceedings: Charged with assault with intent to kill and sentenced to three years hard labor in the Nebraska State Penitentiary, Theodore G. Clark petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against warden John H. Hopkins. Judge Holmes granted the petition. After reviewing evidence, Holmes denied the writ and ordered that Clark be remanded back into custody. Months later, Clark filed a motion for a new trial, which was overruled.
