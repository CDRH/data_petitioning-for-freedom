  • Summary of Proceedings: G. A. Applegate was arrested and jailed for violating a Lincoln, Nebraska city ordinance against "getting and become [sic] drunk." He petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, the judge ruled that while the city ordinance allowed a person to be jailed if they did not pay their fine, the "part of said Ordinance imposing a sentence for said charge under said Ordinance is illegal and void." Applegate was remanded to the custody of his jailer and the police judge was ordered to set his bail at $200.
