  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 13, 1862, Marion Fair petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. He was imprisoned by Walla Walla county sheriff James Buckley on a count of grand larceny by order of justice of the peace W.P. Horton, but Fair insisted that there was no evidence to convict him of the crime. Judge E.P. Oliphant ordered him released, but that he had to pay court costs.
