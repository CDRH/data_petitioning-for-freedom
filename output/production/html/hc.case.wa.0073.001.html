In the District Court holding terms at Seattle, King County Washington Territory.

Territory of Washingtonm County of King. ss.

In the matter of the application of AH SAM and AH WHO for a writ of Habeas Corpus.

To the Honorable Roger S! Greene, Judge of the above entitled Court and Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Washington Territory:

The petition of Ah Sam and Ah Who respectfully shows, that they and each of them are unlawfully imprisoned, detained and confined and restrained from his their liberty by John H. McGraw, sheriff of said King county, Washington Territory. That said imprisonment, detention, confinement and restraint, are is illegal and that the illegality thereof consists in this, to wit:

That on the 19th day of February 1885 at the city of Seattle in said King county, before G. A. Hill, Esq., a justice of the peace in and for Seattle precinct in said county, a sworn complaint was made by one Wm. Murphey, wherein your petitioners were, and each were, charged with the alleged pretended crime as follows: That at Seattle in said King county, on the 19th day of February 1885, Ah Sam and Ah Who did commit the crime of carrying on as proprietors thereof a gambling game as follows; By then and there being the said Ah Sam and Ah Who unlawfuly and wilfully did as proprietors thereof carry on a gambling game then and there played with cards and other gambling devices, for money and for gain,   of said grand jury or for their commitment to the custody of said sheriff. That there is no evidence against the said petitioners or either of them sufficient to constitute any probable cause for holding said petitioners or either of them to await the action of said grand jury or for their commitment to the custody of said sheriff.

Wherefore your petitioners and each of them pray that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted, directed to the sheriff John H. McGraw, sheriff of said King County aforesaid, commanding him to have the body of said Ah Sam and Ah Who and each of them before Your Honor at a time and place therein to be specified, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered by Your Honor concerning them or either of them, together with the time and cause of their detention and said writ, that they may be restored to their liberty.

McNaught Ferry McNaught & Mitchell Attorneys for Petitioners

(Ah Sam) (Ah Who)

Territory of Washington, County of King. ss.

Ah Sam and Ah Who being each first severally duly sworn, each for himself doth depose and say that he is one of the Petitioners named in the above and foregoing petition, that he has h ard the same read, knows the contents thereof and believes the same to be true. (Ah Sam) (Ah Who)

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 21st day of February 1885. John H Mitchell Notary Public in and for King County Washington Territory.
