Before the District Court of the Territory of Washington Wm G. Langford judge presiding at Chambers at Dayton Columbia County Territory of Washington

In the matter of L.T. Savacool habeas corpus vs J.H. Hosler

This cause coming on to be heard at chambers at Dayton Columbia County Territory of Washington on the 23d of February 1886, upon the application for the court and the return of J.H. Hosler and the said Savacool appearing in his own proper person and by his attorney M.M. Gudman his attorney and the said J.H. Hosler by this attorney Robert T. Sturdevant and the case being submitted the court adjudges that the process upon which the said Savacool is held in defective and as justification for the imprisonment of him the said Savacool and therefore orders an examination made as of whether he the said Savacool is guility of any offense   for which he should be held in custody whereupon the evidence is submitted to the Court and upon the evidence and how the judge Wm G. Langford finds that there is is no probable cause to find him the said Savacool guilty of any such offense and it is hereby orderered that he the said Savacool be and he is hereby discharged from all custody and imprisonment to go hence without day and what the costs of the prosecution hence be had by the County of Garfield of the Territory of Washington as per cost - bill[?] hereto attached

Dated this 23d of February 1886 -

Wm. G. Langford Judge
