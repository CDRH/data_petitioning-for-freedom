A.J. Duncan sworn and testified

Know defendant first saw her tuesday when she was turned in to the sheriff said

I had charge of the Prisoners had guards there and Cal Turner and Mr Forse had gun - ordered not to talk to any one. I informed her that her attorneys I had instruction from the sheriff to get the revolver from her she said Cal Turner has it I says the revolver you gave Cal Turner is the one you done the shooting with she answered that it was she said then she shot three times shot twice 2nd shot he fell then she shot him in the head then hit over the head with a club then came to town and gave herself up said she shot Frank Teller said at that time she done it in self defense

When I read the warrant to her said she did not deny killing him but if she had not he would have killed her

X

Do not remember her saying Do not he was coming at her with a double barrell shot gun Turner gave me instruction not to let any one see   or talk with her. Refused to let William[?] see or talk with her - Ray Raymond[?] present she said Frank Teller I think might have said her husband I gave her instructions before I asked her for the Revolver -- I asked her if she had a thick Revolver -- or fire arms of any kind on her person -- asked her if it was the Revolver she done the shooting out there with said she shot twice before he fell - Dont think she said anything about riding up with the gun but that she done it in self defense and under the fear that he would kill her if she had not him. She did not portend to go into a detailed statement of the affair.

AJ Duncan

Sworn and subscribed to before me this 8th Day of June 1887

Jno. J Booge

Justice of the Peace
