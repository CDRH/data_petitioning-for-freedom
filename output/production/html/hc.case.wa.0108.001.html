No 187

In the matter of the application of Maria L. Peterson for writ of Habeas Corpus.–

This cause coming on for hearing in open Court on this 13th day of September 1889 Gowin & Liner[?] appearing for the petitioner and George J. Moody appearing for the respondents. The court having heard the evidence of the parties and the arguments of counsel and being fully advised in the [?] it is by the court ordered adjudged and decreed that the said petition be denied with costs and the said children and be relinquished to the respondents Mrs. L. O. Hamilton and Hume respectively and that they be adjudged to have a right by adoption from the Portland Orphans House &[?] the full control and care of said children and that the petitioner Maria L. Peterson be adjudged to have no control of said children and that she be forever refrain from in any manner interfering with or molesting the said L.O. Hamilton and Hume in the control and custody & care of said   children, that respondents[?] have and recover of said petitioner Maria L. Peterson there costs taxed at $[] and that execution issue therefor

Frank Allyn Judge
