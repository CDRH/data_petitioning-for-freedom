Territory of Washington County of Walla Walla

Samuel F. Montgomery vs Edwina Montgomery def

To the Honorable Samuel C Wingard Judge of the District Court first judicial district of Washington Territory

The above named plaintiff complaining of said defendant states the following facts

1st That plaintiff is a resident of Columbia County in said Territory and has resided there for eight years last past

2nd That on the 23rd day of January 1870, plaintiff and defendant intermarried

3rd That plaintiff and defendant have two children the issue of said marriage to wit; George Clarence Montgomery six years old last January and Myra Bell Montgomery aged four years last April

4th That about the 1st of October 1876, plaintiff and defendant separated, the defendant leaving plaintiff, and   living separately and apart from him

5th That on Sunday the 27th day of May 1877 defendant was about going to Dayton under the pretense of finding employment, which, if she failed to find, she represented that she would go to Walla Walla or elsewhere

6th That plaintiff allowed said children to go with defendant to Dayton under a promise on the part of a brother of plaintiff to return the children to the house of plaintiff on the evening of said day; that defendant having arrived at Dayton refused to allow said children to be returned to plaintiff, but detained them in her custody.

7th That on the 29th of May 1877, plaintiff applied to defendant at the hotel in Dayton where defendant was stopping, and requested the defendant to return to his home and live with plaintiff as his wife, and that they would take the children with them and properly care for and raise them up and urged[?] that defendant should do so, and that defendant utterly   refused so to do, and that plaintiff there upon demanded the possession and custody of said children, as their father and natural gaurdian but that defendant absolutely refused to deliver said children to plaintiff the said Myra Bell Montgomery or to allow plaintiff to take her, threatening that before he should do so, she would kill plaintiff, and plaintiff believes she was ready to put the said threat into execution if he had attempted to take said child.

8th That said Myra Bell Montgomery, is unlawfully restrained of her liberty and kept from the custody of plaintiff by said Edward Montgomery the above named defendant at [?] hotel in Dayton in said County Columbia; that the cause and pretense of said restraint according to the best knowledge and belief of plaintiff are that defendant claims the custody of said child on account of being its mother

9th That plaintiff alleges said restraint to be illegal in this that plaintiff is the   Father and natural guardian of said child and in a better condition to properly raise educated and provide for her than said defendant

10th Wherefore plaintiff prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus issue to the end that said child Myra Bell Montgomery may be brought before your honor and discharged from said restraint, and delivered into the custody of plaintiff, and the said defendant be required to appear at the same time and place and show why she has held said child in restraint as aforesaid.

Witness [?] [?] Samuel F Thompson
