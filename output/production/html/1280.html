  • Summary of Proceedings: On 5 April 1912, Sarah Younsum presented a petition for a writ of habeas corpus to Judge A. L. Sutton who then ordered that a writ be issued in favor of Minne Boxter, the daughter of Younsum, returnable on 6 April 1912. The writ was directed to Bessie Early, who Younsum had stated was unlawfully depriving Boxter of her liberty. Younsum’s original petition only states that Boxter was “allowed to go to said Bessie Early,” and has been detained and “deprived of her right to go to her mother” since that time. The date at which Boxter went to Early is not stated. Sheriff McShane’s writ return, which was filed 17 April 1912, stated that he served the writ to Early on 6 April 1912. The Appearance Docket notes that the parties appeared in court before Judge Leslie on 6 April 1912 and then the trial continued on 13 April 1912. The Docket states that the writ was denied and the case dismissed on 20 April 1912. The "custody" of Boxter is not directly stated in the case file.
