  • Summary of Proceedings: In March 24, James T. English petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of his client Stanley Nielson on the basis Nielson was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty. Three days prior, Nielson was charged and found guilty of drunkenness and carrying concealed weapons and subsequently sentenced to sixty days in County Jail. The petition alleges that Nebraska State Statutes, Section 9829, outlines that the offense of carrying a concealed weapon was a felony providing for imprisonment in the penitentiary. The petition further alleged inferior courts had no jurisdiction to sentence or imprison for such an offense, and the sentence of sixty days was illegal.
