In the District Court, of the Second Judicial District, holding terms at Tacoma, Washington Territory.

2395

In the Matter of the Application of Ah Sing. For a Writ of Habeas Corpus. Petition for Writ.

To the Hon. Frank Allyn, Judge of the District Court of the Second Judicial District, Territory of Washington.

The petition of Gee Hee respectfully shows:

That the said Ah Sing, is unlawfully imprisoned, detained, confined and restrained of his liberty by T. J. Hamilton, United States Marshal for the Territoy of Washington, at the United States Prison on McNeils Island, Pierce County, Territory of Washington.

That the said imprisonment, detention, confinement and restraint are illegal; and that the illegality thereof consists in this, to-wit:

That the said Ah Sing is imprisoned for the alleged illegality of coming into the United States of America, contrary to section one (1), of " An act to amend an act entitled " An act to execute certain treaty stipulations relating to Chinese approved May sixth eighteen hundred and eighty-eight." passed by the forty-eighth Congress and approved by the President on July fifth eighteen hundred and eighty-four. But the said Gee Hee says: that Ah Sing was a resident of the United States of America, at the time of his arrest, and had been a resident for many years prior thereto, all of which he verily believes he can prove.

Wherefore, your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted, directed to the said T. J. Hamilton, Marshal as aforesaid, commanding him to have the body of said Ah Sing before your Honor at a time and place therein to be specified, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered by your Honor, concerning said Ah Sing, together with the time and cause of his detention, and said writ; and that he, the said Ah Sing, may be restored to his liberty.
