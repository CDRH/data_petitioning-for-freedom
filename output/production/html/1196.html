  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 4, 1918, Ike Payne filed for a second Habeas petition following his arrest for the unlawful possession and transport of intoxicating liquor, understood as second offense. In this new petition, Payne charged that this charge does not fall under Nebraska state laws and that the police magistrate does not hold the authority or power to sentence under such charges. A writ was issued and Payne was released the same day.
