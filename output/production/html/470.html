  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 3, 1918, a complaint was filed against Flossie Gordon in the Omaha police court, charging Gordon with being a vagrant. The sentence fined Gordon for $100, and if defaulted, Gordon would be placed in jail. On July 6, Gordon filed for habeas with attorney, Daniel Horrigan, claiming that a police judge lacks the authority to give such a sentence. The petition was granted, and on July 8, Judge Redick sustained the writ of habeas and Gordon was released from custody.
