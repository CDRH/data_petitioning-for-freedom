  • Summary of Proceedings: On March 15th, 1884 George R Daly petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus after being detained by John Harte McGraw in Washington for stealing a horse in Oregon. Daly petitioned for Habeas Corpus on the basis that he was not imprisoned under any commitment by any authority of law or court authorized to issue the warrant for his arrest and that he was innocent of the crime he has been accused of. The writ of Habeas Corpus was issued because Judge Roger Greene recognized that neither Washington nor Oregon had the jurisdiction to arrest and detain the petitioner and that the warrant for his arrest was not issued according to the law.
