  • Summary of Proceedings: In January 1885, three men were accused of land fraud in the Third Judicial District Court for the Territory of Washington. William Jones, William Doyle, Fred Williams, and Alfred Woods were all subpoenaed to testify in their trial. When the district court recessed, the judge required each of the four witnesses to pay a $500 recognizance guaranteeing that he would return to testify when the court reconvened. The four men refused, citing financial hardship, so the judge had them confined in the McNeil Island Federal Penitentiary. Jones, Doyle, Williams, and Woods petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in the Second Judicial District Court, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, the four men were remanded to the custody of the US marshal at the penitentiary.
