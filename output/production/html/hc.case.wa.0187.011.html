Now comes Marie Robina Grayson the petitioner above named, and for Reply to the Return of Henry M. White, to the Writ of Habeas Corpus, hereinbefore issued doth

I.

Deny each and every word, paragragh, sentence, and allegation in that portion of the Return of the said Henry M. White, denominated "Affirmative Defense".

WHEREFORE having fully answered the petitioner prays that she may be discharged as hereinbefore she has prayed.

Marie Robina Grayson
Petitioner By Noah Shakespeare
Louis A. Merrick
Her attorney.

IT IS STIPULATED by and between Noah Shakespeare, and Louis A. Merrick, attorneys for the petitioner, and Clay Allen and G. P. Fishburn, attorneys for Henry M. White, that the petitioner not being present in Court at the time of the making of this Reply, a verification thereof is hereby expressly and in terms waived, and it is consented that said Reply may be filed as of the date of the hearing upon the Return to the Writ of Habeas Corpus, herein, to-wit, as of July 3, 1914, with the same force and effect as if actually filed on said day, and with the same force and effect as if verified by the petitioner in person.

IT IS FURTHER STIPULATED AND AGREED by said attorneys that any additional exhibits that were used upon the hearing may be made a -1-   part of the record at any time before the Transcript of Record is sent by the Clerk to the Circuit Court of Appeals.
