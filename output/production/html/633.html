  • Summary of Proceedings: Lewis Johnson was arrested in Washington for being accused of stealing $1880 dollars from John Pritchard in a hotel in Vancouver, Canada while Pritchard was intoxicated. Witnesses Tate, Russel, and Garnett attest that Johnson had a lot of money with him around the time Pritchard's money was taken, around June 4th or 5th of 1915. While Kennedy declares cause for extradition so Johnson can be tried in Canada, Johnson petitions for writ of habeas corpus, arguing the statements against him to be untrue and without probable cause or legal evidence. Outcome is unknown.
