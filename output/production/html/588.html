  • Summary of Proceedings: Prosfrio Deffillipps and Filip Perill were seamen on the "Bark Eldorado," a ship that arrived at the port of Tacoma, Washington, on June 29th, 1886, from San Francisco, California. After the ship's arrival, the two men left the vessel, leading the shipmaster, L. S. Humphrey, to file a complaint against the seamen for breaching their contract to work on the boat. Authorities detained Deffillipps and Perill the next day and held them in county jail for their alleged crime. The two proceeded to file a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus that same day, June 30th, 1886. Their attorney argued that the local police had no authority over the dispute between an employer and his employees. Unfortunately, no document clearly shows whether the two men were granted their freedom.
