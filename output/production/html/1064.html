  • Summary of Proceedings: Wu Kao was arrested for deportation by an order issued by Immigrant Commissioner Henry M. White. He petitioned claiming that he was a student and is a merchant of China, his investigations alongside his secretary in the U.S. were legal, and a passport given to him by the Commissioner of Foreign Affairs allowed him to proceed. Writ was denied with the argument that Wu Kao didn't carry a certificate required by Section 6 of the Act of 1884 in order for his mercantile status to be accepted and his admittance to the U.S. It was further claimed that Wu Kao didn't investigate industrial conditions as granted by China, but opened up a local merchant shop, and his secretary has left and forfeited a $1,000 bond.
