  • Summary of Proceedings: On April 9, 1909 Bryce Crawford, the police judge of Omaha, tried and found George Rigby guilty of being drunk and disorderly. He was sentenced to 90 days of imprisonment but the mittimus now in the possession of the sheriff shows that it was changed from 90 days to 50. Rigby argued that he was wrongfully imprisoned because the order for detention was invalid if the days were changed. Sears granted the petition but there was no outcome on any of the documents.
