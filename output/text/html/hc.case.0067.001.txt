







#3097

In the District Court holding terms at Seattle in King County
In the Matter of the Application of Chin Chung (a Chinaman for a writ of Habeas Corpus
}

To the Hon. Roger S. Green Chief Justice of Washington Territory and Judge of the
above entitled Court.

The petition of Chin Chung respectfully shows:

That he is unlawfully imprisoned, detained and confined and restrained of his liberty,
by John H. McGraw Sheriff of the County of King in the Territory of Washington,

That said imprisonment detention, confinement and restraint are illegal; and that
the illegality thereof consists in this, to wit:



2

That on the 11th day of November 1882 at Seattle in said County, before T.H. Cann,
Esq., a Justice of the Peace in and for Seattle Precinct in said County a sworn complaint
was made by one J.S. Woolery wherein your petitioner was charged with the alleged
and pretended crime of "visiting a house kept for the purpose of opium smoking, and
had in his possession pipes and appliances for opium smoking and did visit such house
or place for the purpose of smoking or inhaling opium;" that upon such complaint a
warrant was issued by said Justice of the Peace, upon which warrant your petitioner
was arrested and brought before said Justice of the Peace; that there after such proceedings
were had that on the 17th day of November 1882 your petitioner was tried before said
Justice of the Peace for said alleged offense



3

and upon said trial was found guilty of said alleged offense and was by the judgement
of Justices Court sentenced to pay a fine of twenty five dollars

and in default of such payment to be committed to the County Jail of said County

that your petitioner failing to pay said fine was has been by said Court committed
to said Jail, and  now stands committed as aforesaid to said jail, and is in the custody
of said Sheriff at said Seattle, and is restrained of his liberty as aforesaid; that
said trial and commandment as aforesaid were had under and by virtue of section 2073
of the Code of Washington; that said section is unconstitutional and void, and that
said arrest, commitment and trail are therefore without any authority of law.



4

Wherefore your petitioner prays that a writ of habeas corpus may be granted, directed
to the said John H. McGraw, Sheriff of said King County, commanding him to have the
body said Chin Chung before your Honor at a time and place therein to be specified,
to do and receive what shall then be and there be considered by your Honor concerning
him, together with the time of cause of his detention, and said writ; and that he
may be restored to his liberty

Dated on the 18th day of November 1882
Chin Chung by J.C. Haines his Attorney
