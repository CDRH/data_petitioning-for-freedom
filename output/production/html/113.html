  • Summary of Proceedings: In August 1923, Thomas Vann petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus on the grounds he was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty by Michael Endres, Sheriff of Douglas County, Nebraska. Vann was charged, found guilty, and sentenced to sixty days in jail for vagrancy. At trial, on appeal from police court, Vann was sentenced by Judge L.B. Day to serve an additional nine months in jail for said offense. The petition alleged Judge Day exceeded his jurisdiction in passing said sentence. Judge Day ruled that the sentence of two hundred and seventy days in jail was excessive and illegal, as the maximum limit of the municipal court of the City of Omaha for matters of said kind is ninety days, and ruled Vann be released and discharged from custody.
