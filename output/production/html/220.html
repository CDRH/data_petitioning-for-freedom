  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 12, 1889 William Cohrane, sheriff of King County, Washington Territory, arrested and jailed Joseph Mifford. Two days later Mifford and his attorneys filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus claiming that Mifford's arrest was illegal in that Cochrane acted under no legal authority, gave Mifford no reason for his arrest, and in that Mifford was being held in jail without due process of law. The same day as Mifford filed a petition, Thomas Burke, judge of the third judicial district of Washington Territory, granted said petition. The fate of Mifford and the case is unknown following Burke's order granting Mifford's petition.
