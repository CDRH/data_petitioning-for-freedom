









6673

In the District Court of the 3rd Judicial district in and for the County of King Territory
of Washington

In the matter of the application of Jimmy Jardine alias Russell for a writ of Habeas
Corpus}

The petitioner by his Atty John E. Costello respectfully shows that said Jimmy Jardine
alias Russell is unlawfully imprisoned confined and restrained of his liberty by William
Cochran Sheriff of King County in said Territory

That said imprisonment confinement and restraint are illegal and that the illegality
thereof consists in this to wit:-

That said prisoner was committed on or about the 1st day of December 1888 by a Justice
of the Peace in and for the Precinct of Surgudine to await the action of the Grand
Jury next to convene on the 1st Monday of February 1889 that said prisoner has not
been indicted for the crime of attempt to commit Rape that the papers and



documents in said case have now been transmitted by said Justice to said District
Court that nothing in the records of said Court appears whereby said Court appears
whereby said Court has a centare jursidiction of or over the body of the said Prisoner.

Wherefore your said petitioner prays that a Writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted directed
to the said Cochrane commanding him to have the body of Frank OBrien before your Honor
at a time and place therein to be specified to do and receive what shall then and
there be considered by your Honor concerning him together with the time and cause
of his detention and said writ and that he may be restored to his liberty.

Dated on the 2nd day of March 1889

Jimmy Jardin alias Russell
by his atty John E. Costello

And now comes the said John E. Costello on oath and says that the above statements
subscribed to by him are true of



his best knowledge and belief.

The said John E. Costello further says that he sent us said petition for the reason
that said defendant is confined in the County Jail in said King County and therefore
unable to make personal 

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 2nd day of March 1889
