  • Summary of Proceedings: In June 1919, Charles Hoban filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on the grounds he was unlawfully imprisoned and restrained of his liberty. Hoban was charged with stealing an automobile and receiving a stolen automobile, and Police Judge, Charles E. Foster, set bail at $10,000, which the petition alleged was excessive. Respondent M. L. Clark, stated he had authority for holding Hoban on account of three arrest warrants issued by Charles E. Foster prior. Judge William A. Redick authorized the writ of Habeas Corpus, and required the magistrate to set bail at $2,500 for each of the three complaints against Hoban. The petitioner was remanded to custody subject to bail.
