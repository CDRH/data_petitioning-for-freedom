  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 21, 1918, a complaint was made by Arthur E. Volkmeier against James H. Parsons on the charge of Grand Larceny made, originally, in Bannock County, Idaho. Parsons was arrested and brought to the Police Court in Omaha on warrant by Judge Terrel in Bannock, and on the charge of being a fugitive from justice. Parsons filed for habeas against police chief, Micheal Dempsey, and police captain, Anton Vanous, charging that at the time of his arrest no warrant or complaint had been made against him. A writ of habeas was issued, but denied on September 4.
