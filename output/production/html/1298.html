  • Summary of Proceedings: William Mixon was arrested by federal agents in August 1918 and placed in the Douglas County jail on suspicion of draft evasion. He petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that he had been discharged from the National Guard "on disability grounds" the previous year and had otherwise registered for selective service. After hearing the evidence, Mixon was released from custody.
