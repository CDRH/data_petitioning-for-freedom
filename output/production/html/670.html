  • Summary of Proceedings: John Alperson was arrested by Michael Whalen for giving away and allowing to be taken, cigarettes and material for their composition known as cigarette paper. His legal team argued that his charge was unconstitutional because it was a subject not sufficiently expressed in the title of the act. They argued that the object of the constitutional limitation is “to prevent the legislature from passing a law under the guise of one name, which might affect some other interests or rights,” and it is contended that the right to give away cigarettes can not be abridged in a law where the title does not specify an intention to prohibit a gift. They also argued that the subject of the legislation is disguised so that legislators would not be sufficiently notified by the title that it was intended to discourage the use of and prevent all traffic of said articles. Alperson was released from custody.
