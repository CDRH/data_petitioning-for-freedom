  • Summary of Proceedings: Alfio DeGeorge was arrested on December 19th, 1923, after fleeing the scene of the murder of a man named Gibilterra. Following a preliminary hearing for the trial of Gibilterra's murder, DeGeorge filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus for being unlawfully detained by the Douglas County Sheriff, Michael Endres, as a suspect in the murder case. DeGeorge claimed that the witness examinations in the preliminary hearing had cleared him of guilt. Despite the Habeas Corpus petition being granted, his writ was denied as the judge deemed that he was still at risk of conviction as an accomplice in the murder of Gibilterra. DeGeorge remained in the custody of the Douglas Country Sheriff.
