  • Summary of Proceedings: In February 1908, fourteen-year-old Nebraska resident Albert Anderton enlisted in the U.S. Army while visiting relatives in Missouri. He had obtained an order from the probate court of Cowley County, Kansas, designating one Thomas Hawkins as his legal guardian and enlisted with his permission. In April, Albert's father, William Anderton, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of his son, which was granted. In his return to the writ, Brigadier General Charles Morton explained that Albert had been court-martialed for fraudulent enlistment and that it would be a bureaucratic hassle to present him before the court since he was technically detained by the authority of the Secretary of War. The judge dismissed the cause and Albert remained in the custody of the U.S. Army.
