  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 11, 1919, Crystal Gilson was found guilty in an Omaha court of the charge of unlawful sale of intoxicating liquor, and sentenced to be imprisoned in the County jail for 60 days, and ordered to pay the costs of the prosecution or stay imprisoned until the costs were paid. On that day, Charles E. Foster, the police magistrate of the city of Omaha, sent a letter to Omaha Sheriff Michael L. Clark and ordered him to detain her. Three days later, on February 14, Gilson filed for a writ of habeas corpus. Her writ was allowed, then on January 26, 1920, the petition was dismissed for want of prosecution of cost of release. Gilson's fate is unknown.
