  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 20, 1922, Robert Johnson filed for habeas against the sheriff of Douglas County. In his petition, Johnson explained that a false complaint was made against him by the City Prosecutor, Frank M. Dineen, under the charge of vagrancy on July 13, 1922 and that following his sentence in Police Court, an appeal in pending. Johnson argues that under such appeal the jurisdiction of the police court, which issued a new warrant without complaint while out on bond, had no authority to commit Johnson. A writ was issued, sustained, and Johnson was ordered to be released on October 27, 1922.
