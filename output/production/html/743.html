  • Summary of Proceedings: In June 1920, David Goertzen petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that he was being illegally detained by the U.S. Army at Fort Omaha on a pretended charge of desertion. Goertzen claimed that two years earlier he had received permission from his local draft board in Hamilton County, Nebraska to travel to Saskatchewan for harvesting season since he was exempt from combatant service and unlikely to be called up. He remained in Canada until some time after Armistice Day in 1918, and was arrested back in Nebraska in March 1920. Goertzen was court-martialed in May, dishonorably discharged, and sentenced to two years hard labor at Fort Leavenworth, which was reduced to one year's confinement at Fort Omaha. Upon hearing the evidence, his petition was denied, and Goertzen presumably remanded into military custody.
