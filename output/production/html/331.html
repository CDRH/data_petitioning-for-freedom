  • Summary of Proceedings: A. C. Rankin filed a petition for a writ of habeas corpus on September 22, 1924. Rankin alleged that he was unlawfully deprived of his liberty by Charles H. Van Dusen, chief of police. That same day, Judge James M. Fitzgerald issued a command that Van Dusen show cause for Rankin's detention to Judge A. C. Troup. After a hearing on September 24, 1924, Rankin's application was denied and he was remanded to custody.
