Sarah McDunnoh Defendant

UNITED STATES OF AMERICA.

TERRITORY OF WASHINGTON,}

COUNTY OF KING,}

CITY OF SEATTLE.} SS.

To the Chief, or any Police Officer of the City of Seattle, and the Keeper of the Jail of said City of Seattle:

Whereas, at a Justice Court held at my office in said County, for the trial of Sarah McDunnoh for the offence hereinafter stated, the said Sarah McDunnoh was convicted of having, on the 14th day of May 1888, in said County and City, committed the offence of public disorderly conduct towit using abusive language to one Emily Rose, calling her thief and other epithets and upon conviction, the said Court did ajudge and determine, that said Sarah McDunnoh should pay a fine of fifteen Dollars, and five Dollars Costs, and stand committed until the same be paid; and, whereas, the said Sarah McDunnoh has made default in the payment of the same.

Therefore, you, the said Chief, or Police Officer, are hereby commanded, in the name of the United States, forthwith to convey and deliever the said Sarah McDunnoh to the said Keeper: and you, the said Keeper, are hereby commanded to receive the said Sarah McDunnoh into your custody in said jail, and him there keep at hard labor for the space of twenty days, unless said fine and costs be sooner paid, or he shall thence be discharged by due course of law.

Dated this 25th day of May 1888

N. Soderberg seal

Justice of the Peace.
