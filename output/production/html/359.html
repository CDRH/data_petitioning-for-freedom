  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 26, 1928 Calorina Merales was sentenced to serve 90 days in the Douglas County jail under a complaint filed against him for carrying concealed weapons in violation of the ordinance of the city of Omaha. Merales was confined to the jail on a state mittimus which listed a violation of the statutes of the state of Nebraska as the reason for confinement. On these grounds, Sam Beber filed for a petition for a Writ of Habeas Corpus for Calorina Merales, arguing that the state mittimus ordering confinement did not align with the complaint he was found guilty under. The outcome of the case and fate of Calorina Merales are unknown.
