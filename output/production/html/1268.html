  • Summary of Proceedings: J. H. McConnell filed a habeas corpus petition on December 12, 1898. McConnell claimed that he was unlawfully restrained by John McDonald (Sheriff of Douglas County) and George Shand (jailer of Douglas County). He was arrested and charged with providing a false and forged railroad ticket from the Union Pacific Railroad Company knowingly, with the intent to commit fraud. The judge ruled that there wasn't sufficient enough evidence for his arrest and McConnell was released from custody.
