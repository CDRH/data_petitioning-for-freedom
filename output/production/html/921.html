  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 1st, 1884, Sam Hing, John Sam, Joe Chinaman, Jim Kee, Ah Kee, Ah Sing, Ah Him, and James D. Patterson were all arrested at Sam Hing's house and charged by a justice of the peace for opium use. The court sentenced the eight men to a fine of ten dollars plus court costs for "inhaling and smoking" opium. Following their imprisonment for failing to pay their fine, Sam Hing filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of the entire group and was represented by the attorney Edward Everice. The group had their case heard by Judge Roger S. Greene on July 7th, 1884; however, the outcome of their petition is not revealed in the case file.
