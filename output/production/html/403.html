  • Summary of Proceedings: On April 19, 1922, Fred Sledge filed for habeas against sheriff, Michael L. Clark. In the petition, Sledge explained that on April 5, 1921, he had been sentenced to 1 to 7 years in the State Penitentiary, to take effect within 30 days. Sledge argued that those 30 days have past and that he had been confined for over 1 year. In returning the writ, Clark explained that the penitentiary had no room for Sledge and was ordered to remain in Douglas County jail until room was opened on April 20, 1922. The writ for habeas was denied on April 24, 1922, and Sledge was ordered to remain in custody.
