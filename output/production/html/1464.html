  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 16, 1918, Marie Wells filed for habeas alongside attorney, John M. Macfarland, against the city Health Commissioner, Ernest T. Manning. A writ was issued, and on return, Manning explained that Wells had been found to have a venereal infection and required, under his authority, isolation and quarantine. The writ was denied, and Wells motioned for a new trial, arguing that such evidence was contrary to facts. The motion was overruled on October 19 and Wells remained in custody of the detention home.
