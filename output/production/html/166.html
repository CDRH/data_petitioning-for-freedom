  • Summary of Proceedings: In August 1905, the Omaha Nation brought a civil suit against the U.S. Secretary of the Interior, Ethan Hitchcock, and two of his agents, William Beck and John Mackey, in the Thurston County District Court for violating an 1882 act of Congress regulating Omaha monies in the U.S. Treasury. Acting under a 1904 act of Congress, Beck and Mackey had been distributing Omaha funds to individual tribal members contrary to the wishes of the Nation. The Thurston County District Court issued a writ ne exeat compelling the two men to each pay a $500 bond ensuring that they would answer the Omaha complaint and then jailed them when they refused. Beck and Mackey petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in federal court, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, they were released from custody, to which the Omaha Nation excepted.
