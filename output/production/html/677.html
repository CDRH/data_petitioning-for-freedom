  • Summary of Proceedings: Sam Greer was arrested on October 21st, 1916, in Omaha, after being charged with "carrying concealed weapons." He was sentenced to forty-five days in the Douglas County jail; however, he claimed that he had not violated any laws in the City of Omaha. He filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus and presented his case to the honorable Willis G. Sears. Sears granted Greer the writ, and the petitioner was released from the Douglas County sheriff's custody.
