  • Summary of Proceedings: On January 15th, 1921, Amos Milling plead not guilty in a criminal trial, but was convicted and sentenced to six months in prison and a $500 fee. On March 17th, 1921, Milling filed a writ of Habeas Corpus, claiming that he was being unlawfully held and imprisoned by Michael L. Clark, a sheriff of Douglas County. The outcome of this case and the fate of the bound party is unknown.
