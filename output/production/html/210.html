  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 16, 1887, Gus Bendjus, Robert Strangland, Ben Fredericksen, Frank Nelson, Andrew Johnson, Leander Brown, John Thompson, Alex Agren, and Thomas Jacobs met with their comrades in the Gray's Harbor Branch of the Columbia Fisherman's Protective Association at the group's headquarters in Aberdeen, Washington. After some discussion, various groups set out to destroy wealthy businessmen's traps on the river. Bendjus and his friends destroyed Wililam Church's, causing $650 in damage. A warrant for their arrest was filed the same day for one count of rioting and two counts of malicious mischief, and they were found guilty by a grand jury. They petitioned for their freedom, but this was denied on October 17, 1887. However, all of the men were released on a $400 bail on October 19, 1887.
