  • Summary of Proceedings: Thomas Thompson was arrested by John Briggs for peddling without a license. Thompson was the agent of C. F. Adams Company, a corporation from New Jersey, which was in Omaha to sell goods. His original petition was denied and he reapplied later in July. He argued that he was arrested under a valid warrant but that the warrant was issued charging him with a crime for the violation of an ordinance (954) that is void and unconstitutional and that all business he was conducting was legal. On July 23, the writ of Habeas Corpus was denied.
