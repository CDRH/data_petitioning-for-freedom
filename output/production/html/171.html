  • Summary of Proceedings: Rooney is charged with the burglarizing of a saloon in the city of Omaha. Specifically, with the taking of cigars, he is given a bail amount of five hundred dollars on July 28th of 1882. He is then found guilty along with Thomas Ruby and Manus Correll by Police Judge Gustave Anderson. He petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus. With this petition, he asserted that the building in question, the saloon, is not protected under Nebraska law because it is a retail liquor saloon. A jury in the courtroom of Judge James Neville allowed the writ and discharged Rooney from custody.
