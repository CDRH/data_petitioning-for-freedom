  • Summary of Proceedings: The Judge convicted John Suelling of Grand Larceny and sent him to County Jail. Suelling escaped from jail and became a fugitive from justice. On October 13, 1869, Suelling petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against Sheriff James McAuliff. He claimed McAuliff had unlawful detention at the county jail. He does not know the cause of his restraint. He argued that he had not committed any crimes or violation of the statutes of Washington Territory. There was no order for the police to hold Suelling by any courts or police magistrate of Washington Territory. The Judge accepted his petition and filed a writ of habeas corpus. However, the outcome of this proceeding is unknown.
