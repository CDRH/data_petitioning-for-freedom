  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 25th, 1917, the Omaha Police Department filed a complaint against and arrested E. C. Smeeth for allegedly obtaining goods under false pretenses. He was charged for acquiring $25 worth of goods and sentenced to thirty days in county jail. A few days after his sentencing, he filed for a writ of Habeas Corpus claiming that his actions did not violate any ordinances of the City of Omaha. Unfortunately, the case file does not show whether or not the petition resulted in an allowance or denial for a writ of Habeas.
