IN THE DISTRICT COURT OF THE UNITED STATES, WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION

In the Matter of the Application of JANET MACKAY, formerly Janet Roy, for a writ of habeas corpus,

No. 4517

Answer to the Return of Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration.

The complainant above named in answer to the return of Henry M.White, Commissioner of Immigration, at Seattle,Washington, heretofore filed herein, alleges as follows:

1.

In answer to paragraphs 1-2-3-4-& 5 of said return, this complainant admits the same.

II.

In answer to paragraph VI of said return this complainant denies that she had a full and fair hearing as provided by law.

III.

In answer to paragraph VII. of said return, this complainant denies each and every allegation therein contained and alleges the facts to be that she is now and has been since the 21st day of January, 1919, the lawful wife of Ian Valentine Mackay and that the said Ian Valentine Mackay is a citizen of the United States of America; that the complainant was duly and regularly married to Ian Valentine Mackay on the 21st day of January, 1919, at Seattle Washington, by Abraham Vereid, a person authorized under the laws of the State of Washington to solemnize marriages, and that at the time of said marriage she had not been notified directly or indirectly that she had been ordered deported by the immigration authorities of the United   and that she did not contract or enter into said marriage for the purpose of defeating any warrant of deportation that had been or that might be issued by the secretary of labor of the United States; that said marriage was a result of a deep attachment and affection existing between herself and the Ian Valentine Mackay and was not contracted nor entered into for the purpose nor with the intention of defeating the compaliant's deportation; that Ian Valentine Mackay was born in Scotland in March 1884, entered the United States at New York City in 1898 and was at such time accompanied by his parents; that thereafter his father, Alexander Mackay was duly and regularly naturalized as a citizen of the United States at New York city in the year 1904; that the said Ian Valentine Mackay has endeavored to secure certified copies of his father's naturalization papers, but has been unable to secure the same. That at the time of his entry into the United States, Ian Valentine Mackay was 14 years of age and that he has resided in the United States continuously since that date; that he has voted as an American citizen in the City of New York and at Elgin, Oregon, and the he registered under the selective draft at La Grande, Oregon as an american citizen and sought and endeavored to enter the army of the United States but was rejected because of physical incapacity; that while still a resident of New York the said Ian Valentine Mackay consulted with friends of his and took such steps as hav they informed him were necessary to secure him the full rights of american citizenship and that the said Ian Valentine Mackay has assumed and since he became of age has acted upon the assumption that he was an american citizen; that this complainant now claims american citizenship by virtue of her marriage to said Ian Valentine Mackay; that on the 25th day of January, 1919, Ian Valentine Mackay at the request of Henry M.White, Commissioner of Immigration of Seattle, Washington, presented himself at the -2-   Immigration Detention Station at Seattle, Washington and was then sworn as a witness in the case of Janet Roy by W.P.Callahan an inspector of immigration at Seattle, Washington, and at such time made full disclosure as to his birth and such other facts that had to do with his american citizenship; that a transcript of said testimony was subsequently made by a stenographer in the employment of the immigration authorities and is now in the possession of Jenry M.White, commissioner of immigration at Seattle, Washington; that at such hearing, Ian Valentine Mackay made a full statement with reference to his marriage to the complainant and that all of the facts with reference to said marriage are now in the possession of the immigration authorities and of the said Henry M.White as commissioner of Immigration, in the form of a sworn statement.

WHEREFORE, this complainant prays that a writ of habeas corpus may issue as in her original complaint prayed.

Ralph S Pierce
Attorney for Complainant.

State of Washington County of King

SS

Janet Mackay
