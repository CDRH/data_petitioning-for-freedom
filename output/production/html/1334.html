  • Summary of Proceedings: Petitioner Fred L. Smith, attorney for the held party Red Johnson, filed a writ of habeas corpus alleging that Mr. Johnson was being unlawfully deprived of his liberty by Chief of Police Martin White and Police Captain John J. Donohue. These parties submitted a statement to the court arguing that Mr. Johnson was not being unlawfully deprived of his liberty but, instead, had been taken into custody for suspicious behavior. Nonetheless, Judge Scott ordered that the writ be allowed on 14 January 1899 stating that it appeared that Mr. Johnson was unlawfully detained.
