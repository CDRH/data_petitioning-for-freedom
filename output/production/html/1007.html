  • Summary of Proceedings: In the spring of 1922, James Ingoldsby was extradited from Texas to Otoe County, Nebraska to respond to accusations that he had forged and uttered a check two years earlier. He was convicted of both charges and sentenced to a minimum of ten years in the state penitentiary in accordance with recent changes to state law. When Ingoldsby's appeal to the state supreme court was unsuccessful, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in federal court. He argued that his trial, and especially his sentencing, had been marred by ex post facto applications of new statutes. After hearing the evidence, he was remanded to the custody of the warden.
