IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT FOR THE WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON, NORTHERN SOUTHERN DIVISION.

In the Matter of the Application of Tonka Bajlo for Writ of Habeas Corpus.

The petition of Tonka Bajlo, of Seattle, of the County of King and State of Washington, respectfully shows to the Court that Tonka Bajlo is imprisoned and restrained of her liberty by Ellis DeBruler, Commissioner of Immigration at the port of Seattle, Washington.

That said imprisonment and restraint are because of the following facts, to-wit :-

That said Tonka Bajlo is a married woman working as a domestic at the occupation of housekeeping and cook in the city of Seattle at 815 Charles Street; that while so employed she was arrested upon a warrant issued by Hon. W.B.Wilson, Secretary of Labor, upon the charge that she is "an alien found practicing prostitution after entry." That on the 7th of April, 1913, a hearing was had by Thomas M. Fisher, Inspector and Examiner, at Seattle, Washington.

That said Tonka Bajlo is confined by the Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle in violation of the Constitution of the United States; That at the hearing held on April 7, 1913, said Tonka Bajlo was compelled to testify against herself upon a criminal charge; that she was not represented by counsel and was not informed that she had the right to refuse to testify on the criminal charge made against her; that she had no opportunity to present evidence for or on her behalf; that on the evidence produced at said hearing the Secretary of Labor has ordered that said Tonka Bajlo be deported from this country to Austria from whence she came, upon a criminal charge, that of practicing prostitution after entry into this country. That she does not understand the English language and did not understand the nature of the proceeding against her and when informed of the result thereof she demanded that she have counsel and a rehearing.

 

That she has been a resident of the State of Washington for one year and of the State of Pennsylvania for one year; that the charges against her are without foundation of law and fact, and orders for her deportation have been made by the Hon. Secretary of Labor without an opportunity to have a legal hearing before the Hon. Commissioner of Immigration at the port of Seattle, Washington.

That said Tonka Bajlo is not committed or detained by virtue of any process issued on any final judgement or decree; that the cause or pretense of such confinement according to the knowledge and belief of this petitioner is that said Tonka Bajlo is imprisoned and restrained of her liberty by reason of false charges made and instigated against her by Jack Bajlo, her husband, who is seeking to wreak revenge upon said Tonka Bajlo because of the fact that she refused to live with him by virtue of his inhuman treatment of her and his constant and continuous brutal acts of cruelty.

Your petitioner therefore prays that a Writ of Habeas Corpus may issue directed to the said Ellis DeBruler, Commissioner of Immigration at the port of Seattle, commanding him that he have the body of said Tonka Bajlo by him imprisoned and detained, together with the time and cause of said imprisonment and detention before Hon. Edward E. Cushman, Judge of the United States District Court for the Western District of Washington, Northern Division, at the Federal Building in Seattle, State of Washington, at 10 A 2 pM. on the 21st day of April, 1913, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered and ordered concerning said Tonka Bajlo in pursuance of the statute in such case made and provided.

DATED this 16th day of April, A.D. 1013.

Tonka Bajlo
Petitioner.   STATE OF WASHINGTON COUNTY OF KING } ss.

Tonka Bajlo, the petitioner named in the foregoing petition, being first duly sworn on oath says that he has read the said petition knows the contents thereof, and that the same is true of his own knowledge, except the matters therein stated upon his information and belief, and as to those matters he believes it to be true.

Tonka Bajlo

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 16th day of April, 1913.

Chas H Ennis
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
