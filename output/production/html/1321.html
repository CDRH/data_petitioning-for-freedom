  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 12, 1916, Festino Ranurez was arrested on a mittimus signed by Vincent C. Hascall, a justice of the peace in Douglas County, Nebraska. There was no charge stated, and he was incarcerated in the Douglas County jail simply to be "held for further investigation." On January 20, 1917, with no formal legal proceedings begun against him, Joseph O. Burger, Ranurez's attorney, filed a writ of habeus corpus against the Douglas County sheriff, Michael L. Clark, who produced Ranurez in court on January 24 in front of judge Willis G. Sears. A writ of return was filed on January 26, 1917, but the fate of Ranurez is unknown, except for the fact that a man with his name was buried in Omaha on March 27, 1918.
