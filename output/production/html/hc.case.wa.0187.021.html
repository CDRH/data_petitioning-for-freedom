UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION.

In the matter of the Application of MARIE ROBINA GRAYSON, alias Mare Roine, for a Writ of Habeas Corpus. ) No. 2780. PROPOSED BILL OF EXCEPTIONS.

Irons

This cause came on for hearing on July 3, 1914, and the following stipulation was entered into in open court between Noah Shakespeare, attorney for petitioner, and George P. Fishburne, attorney for respondent, to-wit:

"It is agreed by the attorneys for the petitioner and for the respondent that they may put in a general denial to the return to the writ or such pleading as they may desire, at a later date, and that such pleading may be considered as having been put in this morning, prior to the hearing, and it is further stipulated that the signing of the return by G. P. Fishburn, the attorney for Henry M. White, is a sufficient verification of the return, and shall be the same as if signed by Henry M. White, and duly acknowledged before a proper official with seal attached. Dated July 3, 1914."

And the following matters set forth in the stenographer's report also occurred in open court:

 

UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION.

IN RE MARIE ROBINA GRAYSON, alias MARIE ROBINA, for a writ of habeas corpus ) No. 2780.

BY THE COURT: I understand there is no testimony to be offered.

BY MR. FISHBURNE: Nothing further, Your Honor.

BY THE COURT: The matter is submitted upon the record?

BY MR. FISHBURNE: Yest, the matter is submitted upon the record. The only matter that we might show is this: There is one thing before the stenographer goes that I would like to call to counsel's attention. The files of the Immigration office show the first hearing, and to sustain the return to our writ, we stated in there that she was arrested on a certain date; that she had a hearing on a certain date; that her bond was given on a certain date, and that she was discharged on a certain date. Unless the attorneys will admit that, we will state that the files in the Immigration office show that. At these hearings, as we understand the law, all the files of the Immigration office there are considered as bearing on the issue. But I expect they will admit that.

BY THE COURT: Now, I am unable to apply the answer to this petition because the petition is not paragraphed by number, and this refers to paragraphs.

BY MR. FISHBURNE: Your Honor, I have numbered those in the way they come. I intended to call the court's attention to that before.

BY THE COURT: Well, now we have there paragraphs seven, eight, nine and ten denied, and then we have the affirmative defense all denied, and you say there is no evidence to be presented.

--1--  

BY MR. FISHBURNE: The only evidence, as I understand it, is what I am just calling your attention to with reference to what I understand to be the evidence in the files in the Immigration hearing, and that is that a former hearing of a person who is being deported is a part of the records of this hearing.

BY THE COURT: You had better introduce the former, if you have anything.

BY MR. FISHBURNE: I thought possibly that as having been a part of the original hearing, the files of the former hearing would show when the bond was given, when the petitioner was arrested, and when she was discharged, and when her hearing was had, and we will state that we believe the Immigration files are admissable in evidence at the hearing here, and in addition to that, in her testimony she refers to the former hearing.

BY THE COURT: Has counsel any objection to that?

BY MR. SHAKESPEARE: Yes. I would object to it as being irrelevant to any issue in this proceeding. Now it seems to me to be wholly irrelevant and immaterial as to what was done prior to the proceedings under which she is now held. That was a proceeding that was institued in Oregon.

BY THE COURT: Let it stand then. I will pass upon it later, when I pass upon the whole question. Is there anything else to be offered?

BY MR. FISHBURNE: That is all on either side. I think the attorney on the other side and myself both rely on the record that was taken at the hearing.

BY THE COURT: You had better identify it so there will be no question. Have you the record here?

BY MR. FISHBURNE: Yes, Your Honor, Now this is the record: The whole of the Immigration record we offer in evidence.

--2--  

BY THE COURT: Any objections?

BY MR. SHAKESPEARE: No, Your Honor.

BY THE COURT: Very well, admit it.

BY MR. FISHBURNE: That is the record of this proceeding, Your Honor.

BY THE COURT: Is that all?

BY MR. FISHBURNE: Yes.

BY THE COURT: Very well, you may proceed with your argument.

 

The files of the former hearing were offered in evidence by George P. Fishburne, and by an oversight were not marked for identification; the Court, as appears in the stenographic report, did not pass on the admissability of said files on the morning of the hearing, but admitted them later. They are marked "Respondent's Exhibit Two".

The reply of the petitioner to the return of the respondent by an oversight was not filed, and is now filed and made a part of the record the same as if it had been filed immediately after the filing of the return of respondent.
