In answer to the writ of Habeas Corpus served on me, commanding me to have the body of Leo Sun before the Hon. C. H. Hanford at the Court house in the city of Seattle on June 8th 1889 at the hour of one o'clock P.M. together with the time and cause of imprisonment and detention, I give the cause and time of said imprisonment as follows, towit: said Leo Sun was arrested in Ellensburgh on the 14 day of May 1889 on the charge of smuggling opium, and was taken before U.S. Commissioner S. C. Davidson who held him to answer appear and answer the charge against him at the October Term of the North Yakima District Court, fixing the amount of Bail at $300 which he failed to give, and was then committed to the custody of the U.S. Marshal. He was then committed to the Kettitas Co. Jail until the 29th of May when I brought him to Seattle and committed him to the King Co. Jail on the the way to the U.S. Penitentiary to be held for tiral at the Oct. Term of District Court for North Yakima for the crime of smuggling. In obedience to said writ of Habeas Corpus I now have the said Leo Sun before the Hon. C. H. Hanford Judge of 3rd Judicial District   at the Court House in Seattle, and to obey the orders of said Judge in the premises

T. J. Hamilton

U.S. Marshal

June 8th 1889
