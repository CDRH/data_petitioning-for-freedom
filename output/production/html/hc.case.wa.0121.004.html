In the District Court of the Territory of Washington and for the First Judicial District thereof holding terms at Walla Walla Dayton, for the county of Walla Walla and Franklin Columbia in said Territory

In the matter of the application of Jacob Schuman for a writ of Habeas Corpus}

To the Hon Wm G Langford Judge of the above District:

The petition of Jacob Schuman respectfully shows as follows

1. That he is unlawfully imprisoned detained confined and restrained of his liberty by J.H. Hosler, at Dayton in Columbia County Washington Territory, as sheriff of said County

2. That according to the best of the knowledge and belief of your petitioner the cause and pretense of the aforesaid imprisonment confinement and restraint are that he is charged with a violation of the provisions of that certain pretended   act of the Legislative assembly of the Territory aforesaid known as the "local option act" by selling malt liquor without a license at Dayton in Columbia County Territory aforesaid on July 27th 1886

3. That the aforesaid imprisonment, confinement and restraint is illegal in this towit

That the aforesaid pretended act is unconstitutional and void

That your Petitioner was and is duly and legally licensed and authorized by the County of Columbia as a liquor dealer and thereby authorized to sell malt liquor that such license was issued and granted to Petitioner upon the payment of the sum of $50.00 is dated on the 6th day of May 1886 and will not expire until November 7th 1886

That your Petitioner was on said July 27 1886 duly and legally licensed and authorized by the City of Dayton as a liquor dealer and thereby authorized to sell malt liquor which said City License was issued and granted to petitioner upon the payment   of the sum of $100.00 to said City and is dated February 6th 1886 and did not expire until July 31st 1886 and was in full force and effect on said July 27th 1886, that no part of the money paid by your Petitioner for such license had been paid or tentered to your Petitioner by any one or at all at the time of said alleged sale or at any time thereafter or at all

That your Petitioner was prior to the passage of the said pretended act known as the Local option act as aforesaid and now is the owner and proprietor of a saloon in said City of Dayton together with a large amount of fixtures furnature and liquor of the value of $900.00 the said saloon fixtures furnature and liquor being heretofore used and employed by Petitioner in the conduct of his business as a dealer in liquor aforesaid and are of no value except for the purpose of conducting such business   that petitioner has not been paid or offered any compensation whatever for said property or any part thereof or for the destruction of his said business

Wherefore your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted and issued by your honor directed to said J.H. Hosler commanding him to have the body said Jacob Schuman Petitioner before your honor at a time and place to be therein specified to do and receive what shall then and there be considered by your honor concerning him together with the time and cause of his detention and said writ and that he may be restored to his liberty

Jacob Schuman
Petitioner

Territory of Washington County of Columbia ss

I Jacob Schuman being duly sworn on oath depose and say I am the above named Petitioner and know the contents of the above petition and believe the same true

Jacob Schuman

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 16th day of August 1886

J.C. Dorr Justice of the Peace
