To any Constable, and the Keeper of the Jail of said County,:

WHEREAS, Ah Sam and Ah Who of King Co WT has been brought this day before the undersigned, one of the Justices of the Peace in and for said County; charged on the oath of Wm. Murphy with having, on the 17th day of Feby 1885 in said County, committed the offense of running a gambling game as proprietors thereof and in the progress of the trial of said charge, it appearing to the said Justice that the said Ah Sam and Ah Who has been guilty of the offense of conducting a game of tan as proprietors thereof committed at the time and place aforesaid; and

Whereas, the said Ah Sam and Ah Who has failed to give bail in the sum of for each one hundred dollars for his appearance to answer at the next term of the District Court, as required by me; or has failed to pay a fine of dollars, and costs of suit, taxed at _ dollars. which said term will be held at Seattle in said King County W.T. on the first morning April 1885, to wit April 6th 1885

Therefore, you the said Constable in the name of the United States, are commanded hereby forthwith to convey and deliver the said Ah Sam and Ah Who to the said Keeper; and you, the said Keeper, are hereby commanded to receive the said Ah Sam and Ah Who into your custody in said Jail, and him there safely keep until the expiration said Term of said Court to await its action on said charge or until he shall be discharaged by due course of law.

Dated this 21st day of Feby 1885 G. A. Hill Justice of the Peace in and for King County, W. T.
