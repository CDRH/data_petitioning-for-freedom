  • Summary of Proceedings: Louis S. Lathrop, the parent of Clara Lathrop, filed a writ of Habeas Corpus to regain custody of his daughter who was nine years old from his sister, Maria M. Bradway et. al. Lathrop had placed Clara in the custody of Bradway when she was two years of age. Bradway claimed that Lathrop was an inadequate guardian because of his second marriage to Nelle Patterson. Bradway characterized this individual as a "common prostitute" and presented documents that showcased her history of prostitution, vagrancy, and disturbance. Louis S. Lathrop was denied the writ and custody continued to be granted to Mary Chambers, Maria M. Bradway, and P. A. Whitlock. Lathrop was permitted to see the said Clara Lathrop at all "suitable and reasonable times for a reasonable period at each time" by Judge Joseph R. Clarkson.
