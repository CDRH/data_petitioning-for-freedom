  • Summary of Proceedings: The petition alleges that Mr. James Sparks is being unlawfully deprived of his liberty by Sheriff John Drexel. Sparks pled guilty to the charge of maliciously destroying property of a less value than $100.00 during the September 1893 term of the District Court. On 2 December 1893, petitioner was sentenced to imprisonment in the County Jail for a term of 4 months and to pay a fine of $100.00 plus the costs of the trial. The petition states that the maximum penalty provided by law for said offense is imprisonment for 30 days. Therefore, petitioner is being unlawfully deprived of his liberty. Judge Cunningham R. Scott ordered that the Writ be allowed, returnable before the Court on 8 February 1894. Judge Scott determined that Sparks was being unlawfully deprived of his liberty by Sheriff Drexel and ordered that he be discharged. He further ordered that the costs of the proceedings be taxed to the County.
