Mr. MURPHEY, recalled:

Q. [Gilman] Are you acquainted with the residences in the vicinity of Mrs. McKay's residence? A. Yes sir.

Q. Who lives in the house next her? A. There is no house.

RONALD: We object to that, it is a part of their case.

The COURT: You will have the right to recall witnesses.

RONALD: We have no money to get witnesses.

A. Howard Carr day policeman, he lives within the width of an alley.

Q. Any other house next? A. No one close, none within two or three lots.

Q. Does Antoine live in there in this house next to her? A. A. Well, their house is very umall, his house is joined right on to her, and there is a door leading from the front room into his.

CROSS-EXAMINATION:

Q. [Ronald] What were you doing around the front room? A. I was in there quite often.

Q. That is the only time you were there? A. Yes--

Q. Why did you not arrest Antoine as a gambler? A. I did not see him gambling when I was on the police force.

Q. Did you not testify on your former examination? A. I saw him gambling when I was not on the police force.

GILMAN: We rest.
