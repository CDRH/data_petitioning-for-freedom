  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 5, 1923, Al Madison was charged with forgery. He was accused in a complaint filed by county attorney Henry J. Beal of forging a State Bank of Omaha check on on September 29, 1923. On March 17, 1924, he pled guilty to the charge, probably hoping to be released on parole. On September 17, 1924, a man named C.S. Nelson, with the help of his attorney, Joseph J. Powers, of the firm O'Brien and Powers, field a petition for a writ of habeus corpus against chief of detectives Charles Van Deusen and a John Doe. It is unknown if Al Madison and C.S. Nelson are the same person, however, there would be little reason for their files to be kept together if they weren't. The petition was dismissed on the motion of the petitioner on September 18, 1924.
