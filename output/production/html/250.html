  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 1st, 1923, John N. Baldwin petitioned for and was given an order allowing a writ of Habeas Corpus for Arthur Williams after he had been arrested by Daniel B Butler and Charles Van Duesen the morning of that same day when they had reason to believe he had committed larceny. In the petition, Baldwin argued that Williams had been detained without justification. In their response, Van Duesen and Butler presented that they arrested Williams on a charge of investigation and that at the time they did not have sufficient time to properly investigate. The court granted Van Duesen and Butler leave to amend their response to the writ. The final order of the writ and the fate of Williams is unknown.
