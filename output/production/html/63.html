  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 8, 1888 in Slaughter County, Washington, C.H. Smith was detained and imprisoned by Justice of the Peace of Slaughter County, James Hart, for the crime of larceny. Smith was sentenced to one year in county jail by Hart, which Smith and his attorney, Richard Osborn, challenged through petitioning for a writ of Habeas Corpus on February 8, 1889. Smith and Osborn stated that the imprisonment of Smith was illegal and unjust as a justice of the peace may only imprison someone if they fail to furnish bonds or as payment of fine or costs. The next day, Judge Thomas Burke ordered the release of Smith.
