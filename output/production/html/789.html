  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 23, 1919, George Rigby was confined to the county jail after being accused of vagrancy and pimp. On July 31, Rigby filed for habeas and a writ was issued. Upon return, city health commissioner, Ernest T. Manning held that under his authority of commissioner, Rigby was being held under city Ordinance No. 10222, under condition of having a social disease. Rigby's writ of habeas was denied November 5, 1919, and Rigby was ordered to remain in custody. Note: Rigby may be same person in the 1910GeorgeRigby case (hc.case.ne.1066).
