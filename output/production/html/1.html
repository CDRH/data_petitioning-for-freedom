  • Summary of Proceedings: In March 1919, Bartholomeo Massulo petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. Massulo was a subject of Italy and was initially arrested in 1917 by an Immigration Inspector charging Massulo with being an illegal alien and having advocated or taught anarchy and the overthrow of the government. Massulo was released upon depositing bail in the sum of $2,500 but was arrested later in 1919 by respondent Henry M. White, and the secretary of labor issued an order of deportation. The petition alleged there was no evidence to sustain said charges, and stated Massulo demanded a copy of said evidence but was denied by the respondent. Judge Neterer denied the writ, and in a stipulation, both the petitioner and government agreed the record of proceedings before the Immigration authorities and the testimony adduced would be withdrawn from the records and files of the court. One month later in April, 1919, the peititoner and his attorney filed an assignment of error stating the warrant of deportation was void, there was no evidence to sustain such charges, and the court erred in denying the writ of habeas corpus. The judge allowed the appeal to the Circuit Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit, yet the outcome and the fate of Massulo is unknown.
