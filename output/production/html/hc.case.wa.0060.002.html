In the District Court for the 3rd Judicial District of Washington Territory.

In the matter of the application of John Matthews, Joseph Lewis, and Thomas Wright, for a writ of Habeas Corpus

The Petitioners allege as follows.

1st That the said John Matthews, Joseph Lewis and Thomas Wright are confined in the Jail of Jefferson County, Washington Territory, at Port Townsend in said County, by J. J. H. VanBokkelen, Sheriff of said Jefferson County

2nd That the cause or pretence of said confinement is a committment issued by Wm H. H. Learned Justice of the Peace for said Jefferson County, upon a charge made by of murder in the second degree, in causing the death of an indian whose name is to your petitioners unknown, and said cause at Port Ludlow in said Jefferson County, on or about the [] day of May 1875.

3rd That the said imprisonment and   confinement is illegal. that said illegality consists in this. That the petitioners are white men, and no evidence was given upon the examination before said Justice, implicating these petitioners except as the guilty persons, except by witnesses who are North American Indians.

Wherefore complainants pray that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted directed to said J. J. H VanBokkelen, Sheriff as aforesaid, commanding him to have the bodies of your petitioners before your Honor at a time and place therein to be specified, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered by your Honor concerning your petitioners, together with the time and place cause of their detention, and said writ and that your petitioners may be restored to their liberty

Dated June 2nd 1875

C. M. Bradshaw

Attorney for Petitioners
