  • Summary of Proceedings: Carl Shearer was arrested and placed in confinement by Officer Cashman, a special agent for the Union Pacific Railroad Co., on October 7, 1918, for a crime committed outside Omaha city limits. A petition of habeas was filed against Chief of Police, Marshall Eberstein, who claimed that Officer Cashman took Shearer out of confinement soon after his arrest and was the last to hold custody of Shearer. The petition was granted, but the writ was dismissed on January 26, 1920, for want of prosecution.
