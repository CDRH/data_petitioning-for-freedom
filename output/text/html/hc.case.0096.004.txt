







IN THE DISTRICT COURT,

Of the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding Terms at Seattle,
for the County of King.

HABEAS CORPUS.

The People of the United States of America, to the Sheriff of King County T.J. Hamilton
United States Marshal Greeting:

We command you, that you have the body of Wa Kim by you imprisoned and detained as
it is said, together with the time and cause of such imprisonment and detetnion, by
whatsoever name said Wa Kim shall be called or charged, before the HON. RICHARD A.
JONES CH Hanford, Judge of the aforesaid Court at the Court House in the City of Seattle
W.T. on June 27th 1889 at the hour of 10 oclock AM of said day to do and receive what
shall then and there be considered concerning him the said Wa Kim. And have you then
and there this writ.

WITNESS THE HON. RICHARD A. JONES C.H. Hanford, Judge of said District Court, and
the seal thereof this 25th day of June A.D. 1889.

RM Hopkins Clerk.

By HT. Hannon Deputy Clerk.
