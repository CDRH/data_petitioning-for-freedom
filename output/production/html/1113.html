  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 16, 1868, an order had been issued by Charles E. Mix, the U.S. Commissioner of Indian Affairs, that stated that Native peoples would not be allowed to visit Washington D.C. due to lack of federal funding. Moses Keokuk, the Chief of the Sac & Fox Indians, used his own money to leave the reservation and visit Washington. On his way out of Kansas, he was arrested on November 23, 1868 by Albert Wiley, the Indian Agent for the Sac & Fox Indians. On this order, Keokuk was transported to the county jail of Douglas County and held there for two days. Keokuk petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus which was granted. He continue to sue for damages against Wiley. After hearing the evidence, the jury found for Keokuk. Wiley filed for a new trial, and appealed to the Kansas Supreme Court. After reviewing the evidence, the Supreme Court of Kansas upheld the decision of the District Court.
