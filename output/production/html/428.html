  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 2, 1918, George Brown was arrested and charged with social disease, vagrancy, in the Omaha Police Court and sentenced to 60 days in jail. On July 31, 1918, Brown and attorney, Richard Horton, filed for habeas charging that Brown had not been present for the sentencing and therefor the sentence was void. The petition was granted, sustained, and Brown was released from custody the same day.
