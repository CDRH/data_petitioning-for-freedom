William Jamison, Michael Daley and William Winters. ) Complaint for conspiracy.

William F. Prosser, being first duly sworn deposes and says:

That he is a citizen of the United States, over twenty one years of age and is a special agent for General Land office of the United States. That on the 31 day of January 1885 at Seattle in King County, in the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, William Jamison, Michael Daley, alias Michael Healy, an William Winters, then and there being did unlawfully conspire together to defraud the United States out of its public lands situated in Chehalis county Washington Territory. towit:

Sections two, four, six, eight, ten, eighteen, twenty, and thirty in township 20 north of range 7 west of the Williamette Meridian by hiring divers and sundry persons, towit: Edward Taylor, Wm. H. Doyle and Twenty others, whose true names are to affiant unknown, to unlawfully and fraudulently enter said lands at the United states land office at Olympia, Washington Territory, under the provision of the act of Congress approved the third day of June 1878 entitled "an act for the sale of timber land in the states of California, Oregon, Nevada, and in Washington Territory." and did then and there unlawfully and fraudulently   proceed to carry into effect the conspiracy and purpose and did then and there unlawfully and fraudulently procure and hire the said Edward Taylor, William H. Doyle and twenty other persons to go into the land office at Olympia aforesaid and falsely swear to the sworn statements required by said act of Congress and the rules of said land office of applicants to enter land under said act, and to file in said land office their sworn staesments and application to enter portions of said above described public land.

Contrary to the statute in such case made and provided and against the peace and dignity of the United States

(signed) Wm F. Prosser.

Subscribed and sworn to before me the 8th day of Feby A. D. 1885.

(signed) Eben Smith. United States Commissioner in and for Third Judicial district, Washington Territory.
