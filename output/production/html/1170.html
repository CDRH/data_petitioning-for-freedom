  • Summary of Proceedings: Machida was being arrested and sent for deportation to Japan for likely being a public charge at the time of his entry into the U.S. or within five years of his entry. He petitioned for habeas and claimed said accusation to be false, and described himself as hard-working and frugal. Machida added that he was given improper counsel and lead to plead guilty. Writ was dismissed, so Machida appealed to federal circuit court, and was accepted. However, cause was dismissed, and Machida returned to the custody of the U.S. Immigration Station.
