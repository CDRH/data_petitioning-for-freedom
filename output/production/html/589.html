  • Summary of Proceedings: In December of 1921, Joe Strilka was arrested for contempt of court and taken in front of Honor Carroll O. Stauffer, who sentenced Strilka to 15 days. On February 10, 1922, Strilka filed for habeas, arguing that after an arranged appeal to the Supreme Court on January 28, 1922, Stauffer reduced Strilka's sentence to 13 days, which have now passed. In addition, Strilka argued that he had been charged taxed costs, which were retaxed at a high rate, without any legal authority, and that the judge had not sentenced Strilka to costs or taxed costs. A writ was issued and Strilka was released.
