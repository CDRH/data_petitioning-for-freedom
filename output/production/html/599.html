  • Summary of Proceedings: Iwamatsu was ordered for arrest and deportation on the account that he violated Section 11 of the Immigrated Act of 1917. He petitioned for habeas, and claimed to be a legal immigrant under Section 34 of the same law, to which he entered the U.S. by sea and had remained in the country for more than three years, exempting him from deportation. Petition was denied and Iwamatsu remained in custody of Thomas W. Lynch, Acting Immigrant Commissioner.
