  • Summary of Proceedings: In 1911, the Yamhill County Court found William Gardner to be insane and committed him to the Oregon State Hospital. In 1913, Edwin Cady, whose relationship to Gardner is unclear, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on Gardner's behalf, which was granted. Records from both the insanity and habeas hearings reveal that Gardner believed his wife and daughter were being unfaithful in their marriages and that he had threatened violence against all parties as well as himself. After hearing the evidence, Gardner was remanded to the custody of the superintendent of the state hospital. In 1915, another party petitioned for habeas corpus on behalf of Gardner.
