  • Summary of Proceedings: The Donohue family petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus to retrieve a boy named Bernard Seward from the custody of the Noonan and Seward families, which was granted. Bernard Seward was apparently the nephew of Agnes Donohue and Kittie Noonan. When Bernard had come to Lincoln to see Kittie Noonan and her family, they had kept him there, not returning him to the Donohues in Chicago. After hearing the evidence, the writ was denied and Bernard was remanded to the custody of the Noonan and Seward families. This was apparently because the judge decided that Lincoln was a better place to raise the boy than Chicago. Bernard was also the heir to a farm in Butler county, Nebraska. Almost two years later, the Donohue family successfully challenged the jurisdiction of the court to hear the earlier proceedings. The outcome of this challenge and the fate of Bernard are unknown. However, 1930 census records show Bernard living with the Donohues in Omaha.
