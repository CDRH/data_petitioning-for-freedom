  • Summary of Proceedings: Arrested and jailed for selling liquor without a valid license, Mark J. Wilbur petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that his license was valid, therefore, he was being unlawfully detained by sheriff Ress. Judge Holmes granted the petition. After reviewing evidence, he denied the petition and ordered that Wilbur be remanded back into custody. Wilbur moved the Court for a rehearing, which was overruled. Wilbur then appealed to the NE Supreme Court, who ruled in favor of Wilbur and ordered that he be released from custody.
