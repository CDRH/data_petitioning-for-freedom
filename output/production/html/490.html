  • Summary of Proceedings: Mike Miller was arrested near the Omaha Police Department on October 15th, 1916, for allegedly committing assault. A few days later, Miller was officially charged with "Assault with Intent to Commit Great Bodily Harm" and sentenced to 90 days in the county jail. Though the case never made any explicit claims as to why Miller deserved to be set free, the petitioner argued that he was wrongfully being detained and filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on November 14th 1916. Just a day later, his case was heard and a writ was allowed. The court ordered Felix J. McShane and Henry Schroeder, the respondents, to release Miller from their custody.
