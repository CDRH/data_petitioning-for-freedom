No 110

October Term 1854

Habeas Corpus in the matter of Rebecca A. Moore

Oct 24th Discharged 3h 126,

The Bearer of this: Rebecca Ann More, "Coloured Woman" Was partly Raised and owned by the undersigned then residing in Pinkneyville Mississippi on the first of January 1831 I sold her to Doctr Noah S Webb whose property she continued untill his death, which took place in the Year 1840 or 1841 I think at the aforesaid town of Pinkneyville Mississippi. Dr Webb by will gave the woman Rebecca & her daughter Mary Ann who was born while Dr Webb owned the mother their freedom, this Will is recorded in the town of Woodville State of Mississippi in the Probate Judges Court there after the death of Doctr Webb & after she had obtained her freedom this woman Rebecca & her daughter lived in New Orleans, La, in the family of Mr John Armor of that city for some years, afterward she & her daughter came to the City & has been residing on the Steam Boat Niagara for some time past in the capacity of Chambermaid - her daughter is now at Pittsburgh Pennsylvania - I believe

The above facts as narrated I know to be true

James Wilson of Cincinnati Ohio

21st February 1850

Signed in presence of Miles Fabes

Wm H Woods
