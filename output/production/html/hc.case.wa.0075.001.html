In the District Court of the Third Judicial district of Washington Territory holding terms at Seattle.

In the matter of the application of Lee Kee for a writ of habeas corpus } No 4437 Judgment.

This cause coming on to be heard upon the return of the writ of habeas corpus herein before granted herein by which said return it appears that the said Lee Kee is held in custody by James Woolery, chief of Police of the city of Seattle and keeper of the city jail of said city in the jail of said city by virtue of a committment[?] issued by G G Lyons, Esq Justice of the Peace for King County WT (Seattle Precinct) (the body of defendant being here produced together with the authority of said James H Woolery for his said detention.) and the court having heard argument thereupon and being fully advised in the premises finds that the said committment[?] states as cause for petitioner's detention that which is no cause and finds that the said Lee Kee is unlawfully restrained of his liberty by the said James H Woolery.

It is therefor ordered that the said Lee Kee be forthwith discharged from custody, and that he do recover of & from the said James H Woolery, his costs and disbursements in this action

Dated Seattle May 27 1885
