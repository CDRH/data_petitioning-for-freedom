  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 12, 1922, Carl Fisher filed for habeas against chief of police, Michael Dempsey, and chief of detectives, Charles Van Duesen. In his petition, Fisher charged that the respondents had arrested him on July 3, 1922 and has remained in jail with sentence or bond. Fisher further argued that his constitutional rights to bond and a speedy trial had been denied to him. A writ was issued, but later dismissed on motion by the relator on July 13.
