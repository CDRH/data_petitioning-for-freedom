  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 14, 1896, Ernest Kinloch enlisted in the U.S. Army. Two days later, he was arrested on his way to the train station, charged with insanity, and jailed to await a hearing. He quickly petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, Kinloch was remanded to the custody "proper military authorities."
