In the District Court of the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding terms at Seattle

In the matter of the application of Lee Kee for a writ of Habeas Corpus }

No 4444 Order

On reading and filing the petition of Wo Lin duly signed and verified by him whereby it appears that one Lee Kee is illegally imprisoned and restrained of his liberty by James H Woolery, chief of Police and Jailer of the city of Seattle at the city jail of said city in King County Washington Territory and stating wherein the illegality consists from which it appears to me that a writ of Habeas Corpus ought to issue:

It is ordered that a writ of habeas corpus issue out of and under the seal of the District Court of the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory holding terms at Seattle directed to the said James H Woolery, chief of Police and Keeper of Jail of the said city of Seattle, commanding him to have the body of the said Lee Kee before and in the court room of the said Court on the 1st day of June, AD 1885 forthwith of in of that day[?] to do and receive what shall then and there be considered concerning the said Lee Kee together with the time and cause of his detention, and that he have then and there the said writ.

Dated at Seattle June 1st 1885

R. S. Greene Judge &c
