  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 5th, 1887, George Woodard was arrested by Sheriff James Glispin for unlawfully selling intoxicating liquor. Justice of the Peace, J. R. Yildea, then prosecuted Woodard for the alleged crime. George Woodard then filed a writ of Habeas Corpus on July 8th, 1887, claiming that he was being unlawfully imprisoned and being held on excessive bail. Judge George Turner heard the writ and ordered J. R. Yildea, James Glispin, George Woodard, and two other witnesses to appear in court on July 9th, 1887. The outcome of the writ and the fate of the bound party is unknown.
