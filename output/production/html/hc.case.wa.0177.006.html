EXHIBIT C

UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION

In the Matter of the Application of Tonka Bajlo for the Writ of Habeas Corpus. )

2459 no. 1328 AFFIDAVIT OF L.G. RASHO.

STATE OF WASHINGTON COUNTY OF KING ) ss.

L.G. Rasho being first duly sworn on oath deposes and says: That I am a citizen of the United States and a resident of Seattle, an Austrian by birth and that I have resided here for the past 15 years; that I am employed in the city at various occupations, and on Monday, April 28th, 1913, Jack Bajlo, in company with Chas. H. Ennis, called upon Nick Radovich who conducts a lunch counter in the Frog Solo Bar at the corner of 3rd and Pike Streets, and I was employed there at the time behind the lunch counter. That Mr. Ennis introduced Jack Bajlo to myself and Nick Radovich, and requested that Nick Radovich act as his interpreter; that at that time said Jack Bajlo made a request to visit his wife at the Detention Station; that several statements were made back and forth relative to the deportation of Tonka Bajlo, wife of Jack Bajlo, and said Bajlo made use of the following expressions and language in reply to questions which Nick Radovich asked him at the instigation of Chas. H. Ennis:

Questions of Nick Radovich, and Jack Bajlo's answers to same.

Q. Why do you say your wife is a dirty old whore A. Because she will not live with me.

Q. Is that the only reason that you say she is a dirty whore? A. Yes.

Q. Would you call her a dirty old whore if she would live with you? A. No.

 

Q. Why did you have her arrested? A. I had her arrested and sent back home because she would not live with me.

Q. Then if she was willing to live with you you would not send her back home? A. No. I do all old country style. Make her fear me. She know I boss.

I asked him who told him that his wife was sleeping with the men where she worked and he said another Austrian, and then I asked him who he was and he said that he did not know his name, and then I asked where said Austrian could be found adn he said he did not know. When questioned about the men, who they were, and where the could be found he did not seem to have anyone in mind at all; that he seemed to be lying and not at all sincere about his statements that his wife was a prostitute, or that she had been living with these men, and he said that previous to their marriage he knew her to be a virtuous girl.

L. G. Rasho

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 2nd day of May, 1913.

Chas H Ennis
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
