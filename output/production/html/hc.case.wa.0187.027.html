In re Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina. BOND.

KNOW ALL MEN BY THESE PRESENTS That we Marie Robina Grayson, sometimes known as Marie Robina, as principal, B. P. Castner P. T. Stangeland and J. L. Lyen as sureties, are held and firmly bound unto the United States of America, in the penal sum of $2000.00, for the payment whereof well and truly to be made in good and lawful money of the United States, we do bind ourselves and each of our heirs, executors, administrators and assigns jointly and severally firmly by these presents.

Sealed with our seals and dated this 27th day of June, 1914.

The condition of the above obligation is such that whereas the above named principal Marie Robina Grayson, has applied to the Court above entitled for a writ of habeas corpus, to Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, directed, commanding the said Henry M. White, to produce before said Court, the body of the said Marie Robina Grayson, at a time and place therein specified, together with the rights of her detention, if any, and then and there to submit rights of such detention to such Court, and whereas said writ of habeas corpus has issued

NOW THEREFORE if the said Marie Robina Grayson, shall and will abide and perform all and any and every judgment, or order given, and made by the Court above entitled, in the matter above entitled, and shall appear before such Court, at its sessions in Seattle, at such times, as she may be directed to appear, and shall appear before said Court, on the return day and hour of said writ of habeas corpus, and thereafter at such other time as said Court shall order, and shall, if said writ be discharged, submit herself and render herself -1-   into the custody of said Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, forthwith, then this obligation to be null and void, otherwise to be and remain of full force and virtue.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF We have hereunto set our hands and seals the day and year first above written.

SIGNED SEALED AND DELIVERED IN THE PRESENCE OF

J [?]
Lulu J. Shakespeare P. T. Stangeland
(SEAL) J. L. Lyen (SEAL) [] (SEAL)

WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON.

B.P. Castner P. J. Stangeland AND J. L. LYEN first being duly sworn on their oaths do severally say; that they are the sureties namee in, and by whom the foregoing instrument was executed; that they are residents and freeholders of and in the State of Washington, and are worth in separate property the sum of $2000.00, over and above their just debts and liabilities, and exclusive of their property, exempt from execution; that they are neither an attorney-at-law, sheriff, clerk, of any Court, or other officer of any Court, and this affidavit is made severally.
