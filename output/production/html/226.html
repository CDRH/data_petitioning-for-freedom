  • Summary of Proceedings: John Owen was sentenced to five years in the Nebraska State Penitentiary in 1909 for unspecified reasons. In September 1910, he was granted parole by the governor. Owen found a job and began working, but was imprisoned the following month after the governor rescinded the parole. Owen petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus challenging the rescission, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, the writ was denied and Owen remanded to the custody of the penitentiary. He appealed to the state supreme court, which affirmed the decision of the lower court.
