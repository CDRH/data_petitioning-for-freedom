IN RE MARIE ROBINA GRAYSON, alias Marie Robina, for a WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS, Petioner, ) No. 2780 ORDER

This cause coming on to be heard on the 3rd July 1914, at the hour of ten o'clock a. m., the petitioner appearing in person and by her attorneys Noah Shakespeare and Louis A. Merrick, and the defendant Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration appearing by George P. Fishburne, Assistant United States Attorney, and the court having heard the said petition and the return thereto and having taken the said matter under advisement, and having on the 17th day of July 1914 filed his opinion herein, and the court being fully advised in the premises,

IT IS CONSIDERED, ORDERED AND ADJUDGED by the court that said petitioner is unlawfully held and restrained of her liberty by the said Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, and the petitioner having given bond to answer to the order of the court herein, said bond is to be discharged and the sureties exonerated, on the failure of the Government to appeal within twenty days from July 17 - 1914 and petitioner is entitled to be discharged from the custody, and it is so ordered. on the failure to appeal

Done in open court this 20th July 1914.

Jeremiah Neterer
JUDGE

Let all proceedings under ths order be stayed for the period of twenty (20) days from the 17th July 1914 for the purpose of enabling the Government to appeal herefrom if it be so advised.

Jeremiah Neterer
JUDGE OK. Allen
US Dis Atty
