  • Summary of Proceedings: Daniel Buckovis, the petitioner, filed for a writ of Habeas Corpus, claiming unlawful detainment by Chief of Police Martin White. Buckovis was detained due to a charge of assault with the intent to inflict great bodily injury. On January 16th, 1899, Buckovis petitioned for release by writ of Habeas Corpus, and Judge Willard W. Slabaugh allowed the writ of habeas corpus to be returnable on January 7th of, 1899. On the day of release, Buckovis was arrested again by a deputy of Chief White for the same offense that he was released for by writ of Habeas Corpus. Judge Scott R. Cunningham finds Buckovis unlawfully detained and he is released from custody.
