  • Summary of Proceedings: Victor McCarty, Thomas McCarty, John Gallegher, and William Simpson, alias Alexander Chapin, petitioners, were charged with assault with pistols loaded with gunpowder and leaden bullets with six counts. The six counts are intent to kill, wound, the act of assault, shooting, and abetting the said actions. In 1895, Thomas McCarty plead guilty. McCarty then petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus which was denied in 1900.
