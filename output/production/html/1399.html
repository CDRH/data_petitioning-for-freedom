  • Summary of Proceedings: Aspasia was born in Kaskaskia, Illinois around 1806 to an enslaved mother who allegedly had been brought to the region before the passing of the Northwest Ordinance of 1787. Aspasia was treated as enslaved herself and sold to multiple parties. In 1821, Pierre Menard purchased the girl from Baptiste Gindreau. Shortly after that, Menard gave Aspasia to his daughter and son-in-law, Bernice and Francois Chouteau, who brought her to St. Louis. Late in 1827, Aspasia petitioned for the right to sue as a poor person and to have her freedom determined by the court. She charged Francois Chouteau and Pierre Menard with assault, battery, and false imprisonment and asked for $1,000 in damages. In response to the charge, Menard had Aspasia jailed, at which point she petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. Her fate is unknown, although the defendants in her freedom suit claimed that she was legally enslaved because her mother had been brought to Northwest Territory before the passage of the 1787 Ordinance, which broadly prohibited the introduction of enslaved people into the region.
