Territory of Washington agt James Riley} Criminal Action

Return Comenced before A B Rabbesen J P on the 1st day of March A.D. 1861

This day comes now Mowgatt Lo Who make Oath in writing and say that on the night of the 28th of February 1861 James Riley did in a rude and insolent manner unlawfully Strike and beat one A. Loe and likewise on same night he Jame Riley did make an assault upon an Indian woman named Emma with intent to commit a rape, and further says on Same Evening he said Riley did in a rude and unlawfull manner did touch and push deponant and furthermore that he the said Riley did in a rude and threatening Manner is in Crowd of two or more persons Exhibit a pistol and bowie knife, and at Same time he Riley did feloniously take steal and carry away one Silver Watch from the Hotel Kept by this deponant and that the value of said watch is fifteen dollars and that the same was the property of A. Loe and demand that said Riley be dealt with according to law. Whereupon was Issued a writ for the arrest of Said Riley and the Same placed in hands of Sheriff Tucker for Service. Same day Comes Sheriff and makes return of writ by bringing prisoner into Court and he the said Riley being arrained and charge read to him pleads not guilty and say that he is ready for trial but the Court being of the Opinion that he the said Riley was not in a condition to proceed with the examination ordered that the Case he continued until next day in being the 2nd day of March at 11 oclock A.M. and he the said Riley gave bonds in the sum of One Hundred dollars for his appearance at Court at Said time with James Hughes as Said and the Same was approved and a subpoena was filed at the request of defendant for nine persons

March 2nd 1861 12 oclock in Case Called and defendant asks to have the Case Continued as the principal Witness on the part of defendant is absent on account of Sickness in the family and it is not posabe for him said Witness James Hughes to attend and it was Ordered by the Court that the case be continued until Tuesday March 5th 1861 10 oclock PM

March 5th 1861 11 Oclock AM case called and defendant appears and by his attorney Geo Gallagher waves the examination and files with the Court a bond in the sum of two hundred dollars for his appearance

over
at the next setting of the District Court to answer with James Hughes and P. J Moorey as further[?] and the same was approved

A.B. Rabbesen J.P.

This certifies that the foregoing is a true Copy of the proceeding in the above entitled Cause as appears on the Docket in my Office

A.B. Rabbesen J.P.
