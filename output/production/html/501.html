  • Summary of Proceedings: In August 1885, William C. Swartz petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on the grounds he was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty by E.J. Whittier, Sheriff of Spokane County. Swartz was charged with stealing one red line back cow, valued at $35. The petitioner was given bail in sum of $500 yet failed to give bail and remained in custody awaiting his appearance. The petition alleged there was no probable cause, and the bail amount was excessive. Judge George M. Turner ruled Swartz was not illegally restrained and remanded him to the custody of the Sheriff.
