  • Summary of Proceedings: On March 11, 1919, Grafton W. Coffin file petition for habeas against the chief of police, Marshall Eberstein, and chief of detectives, John Dunn, after a complaint for larceny by bailee had been made on March 4 and a warrant issued March 7. Coffin argued that he had been in the state of Iowa working for the Nelson-Zarp Co. when the offense occurred, the he was unaware of property missing, and had left Iowa for Kansas City, Missouri, before being brought to Omaha on March 9. In addition, Coffin argued that he had not been provided council in his proceedings and that he had not received demand for the missing property. A writ was issued but the cause was dismissed upon motion by Coffin, the relator.
