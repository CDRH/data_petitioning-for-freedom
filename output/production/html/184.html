  • Summary of Proceedings: In June 1923, Richard J. Organ filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of Charles Flournoy. The petitioner alleged that Flournoy was committed and detained by Michael L. Endres (Sheriff of Douglas County), and the Superintendent of the Douglas County Hospital. Organ states the confinement was without an order or judgment from a civil or criminal jurisdiction, under the pretense that Flournoy was mentally insane. The petition stated the confinement was illegal and contrary to law as Flournoy was of sound mind and capable of "taking care of himself and his business." Judge Charles A. Goss authorized the writ of Habeas Corpus. At trial, Michal L. Endres, stated Flournoy was not in his custody and the writ was returned without further action.
