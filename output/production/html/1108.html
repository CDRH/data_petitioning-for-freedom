  • Summary of Proceedings: William Giles, petitioner and father, filed for a writ of Habeas Corpus in an effort to gain custody of Haeckel Humboldt Giles. Mary A. Giles was granted a full divorce in Illinois from William Giles in July of 1889. In this divorce, William apparently allowed Mary to keep their son temporarily, but that William would have full control of the child. He accused her of kidnapping the child on December 7, 1889. Since the divorce agreement did not contain a provision allowing Mary full custody of Haeckel, due to this William argues he has rightful and full custody of the child. The writ is denied by Judge Joseph B. Clarkson, and the Mother, Mary A. Giles, retained custody of the child.
