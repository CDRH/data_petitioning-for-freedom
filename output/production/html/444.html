  • Summary of Proceedings: Arrested on the charge of murder, George W. Davis petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that James Malone and P. H. Cooper were depriving him of his liberty. Judge Hall granted the petition, but after discovering that neither Malone nor Cooper had custody of Davis, he denied and dismissed the writ. Davis was found in the prison of the city of Lincoln. No holding party appeared, and Judge Hall ruled that Davis should remain imprisoned.
