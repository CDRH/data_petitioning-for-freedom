IRA COGSWELL, a witness called on behalf of the Petitioner after being duly sworn, testified as follows.

Q. [Ronald] You live in Tacoma, do you? A. Yes sir.

Q. What is your business? A. Contractor now.

Q. You are a contractor now? A. Yes sir.

Q. Do you know the petitioner Mrs. McKay? A. Yes sir.

Q. How long have you known her? A. Oh, I have known her 14 years.

Q. Have you known her pretty intimately the last two or three years, or since she and her husband separated? A. Yes, I have been better acquainted since then than I was before.

Q. What has been her general conduct, her general demeanor and her business since she and her husband separate? A. Well I have been past there---I have some land near there, and have been down there to see that several times in the last year. I found her washing and ironing and sewing, around thehouse.

Q. Is she an industrious or lazy woman? A. She is industrious woman, a hard working woman.

Q. Always seems busy? A. Yes sir.

Q. Do you see her children frequently? A. Yes sir.

Q. How do they appear, do they appear to be well dressed, well kept? A. Yes sir.

Q. They are comfortably dressed, not rich but comfortably cloathed.

Q. Do you think from what you know of her, do you consider her a fit and proper person to take care of her own children.

A. I do, to take care of her own.

Q. You would consider her a proper person and morally fit person, would you not to care for and instruct and take care of her own children? A. I consider her as good as the average woman in her circumstances.

Q. What do you mean by her circumstances? A. Poor and have but little money--working and making a good living.

Q. That is the only draw back that you consider against her? A. That is all.

Q. Is she living an honest upright life? A. I dont know, only what I see.

Q. Is that the appearances she presents to you and is that the reputation she bears now? A. Well, both appearances and reputation that I get abroad.

Q. That she is leading an honest and industrious life? A. Yes sir. Much better now than when living with McKay.

Q. Is it not a fact that her husband caused her to do what she did do? A. That is what I supposed, I was not at the house a great deal then, but I heard it [Cogswell, D-EX.[] 76--215. 17   said from others that he was the cause of it.

Q. Did you ever hear her charged with living in adultery with any man?? A. No.

Q. From what you know of her do you believe that she would do that?

GILMAN. We object.

A. I have no reason to believe she would any more than any other woman.

Q. You dont believe that she would be guilty of that from what you know of her and see her? A. I do not.

The COURT: How close do you live to her? A. I live two miles and a half. I have some land that I go down occasionally to sell lots from.

CROSS-EXAMINATION:

Q. [Gilman] How frequently do you see her? A. Oh, I see her some six or eight times a year.

Q. She might have been drunk five times a month and you not know it? A. She might have been drunk out of my sight and I not know it.

Q. She might have been living in adultery with a man and you not know it? A. Of course.

Q. Who did you ever hear say that she lead an honest upright life?

A. I have heard several speak of her since McKay left.

Q. Is not this what was said, that she had done better since McKay left? A. Yes that is it.
