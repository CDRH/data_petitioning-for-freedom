  • Summary of Proceedings: Frances Fidler petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus to establish who should have custody of her daughter, Mary Thomas, then living with her grandmother, which was granted. By the mutual consent of both parties, they recessed for thirty days. At the hearing, Frances, alongside Mary's father, Archie Thomas, asked that custody of Mary be given to the child's grandmother, also named Mary Thomas. Archie and Frances had divorced on June 9, 1913 on the grounds of extreme cruelty and non-support. The judge agreed to this new arrangement, and Mary was remanded to the custody of her grandmother and namesake.
