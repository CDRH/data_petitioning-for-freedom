  • Summary of Proceedings: In September 1916, Frances Murphy accused Chase Snyder of bastardy in an Omaha justice court. Snyder could not or would not pay a $900 bond and was placed in the Douglas County jail to await trial. He petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing among other things that he had informed on Murphy to the U.S. Department of Justice and that the bastardy accusation was her making good on a promise to seek revenge. After hearing the evidence, the writ was denied and Snyder remanded to the custody of his jailer.
