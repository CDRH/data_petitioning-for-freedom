  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 3, 1867, Robert Ross testified as a witness in another case titled "Territory vs. Michael Williams". On September 13, he was accused of perjury by the foreman of the jury. On September 18, he was ordered by Justice of the Peace William L. Chalmers to pay a $1000 fine and was committed to the Port Townsend Jail after not paying the fine. On September 25, Robert petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. His writ was delivered to the respondents. Robert's fate is unknown.
