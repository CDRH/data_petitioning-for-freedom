  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 16, 1864, J. W. Tripp was charged with the murder of Alexander Clarke. On May 18, Justice of the Peace William L. Chalmers ordered the sheriff to commit Tripp. On June 3, 1867, Tripp petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. The next day, Washington Supreme Court Justice Charles B. Darwin delivered the writ and subpoenaed Chalmers. The witnesses were subpoenaed in the following days. On June 10, Justice Darwin denied the writ and remanded Tripp to the custody of the respondent.
