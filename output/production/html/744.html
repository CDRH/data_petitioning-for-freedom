  • Summary of Proceedings: Nora Sullivan, an Irish born immigrant, came to the United States through New York City in 1916. Sullivan then moved to Butte, Montana, and the next year stayed at the Montana State Hospital for the Insane for a few months to cure her rheumatism. In April of 1919, Sullivan moved to Seattle, Washington. Shortly after, she was taken to the Bureau of Immigration Detention Station, where officials were attempting to deport her for being "an insane alien". On August 17, 1919, Sullivan filed a writ of habeas corpus in an attempt to be released from the station and to remain in the United States. Judge Jeremiah Netherer allowed the writ and ordered Nora Sullivan and Henry M. White, the Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, to court. The outcome of the writ and the fate of the bound party is unknown.
