  • Summary of Proceedings: Walter Scott, an employee of the Tabitha Home, and Jennie Adams, a long-term resident there, eloped in early October 1910. Scott petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in order to establish custody of Adams, which was granted. At the same time, a case to annul their marriage on the grounds that Adams was developmentally disabled and unable to give her consent was also before the court. While the annulment and habeas corpus proceedings were pending, Adams was placed into the temporary custody of one A. A. H. Mayer. Ultimately, the court annulled the marriage between Scott and Adams but also allowed Scott's writ. The result was that although Adams was released from the custody of the Tabitha Home, Scott had no legal authority to assert custody over her.
