  • Summary of Proceedings: In August 1923, Alma Bushey petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of her son, Harry Cherritt. The writ alleged Cherritt was restrained of his liberty by the respondents and held in jail for more than forty-eight hours without being able to communicate with the petitioner, friends, counsel, and was denied bail. The petition further alleged Cherritt had not committed any crime and was not detained by virtue of any final judgment from a competent tribunal. The appearance docket reveals the writ was denied, and Cherritt remained in custody.
