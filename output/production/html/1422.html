  • Summary of Proceedings: Walter Roseker was arrested in Omaha, Nebraska on December 28,1923 along with Robert Thomas. On December 29, 1923, Thomas was charged with the unlawful sale of intoxicating liquor while Roseker was held as a witness, he then testified that he did not purchase any liquor from Thomas, the Municipal Court for the city of Omaha then sentenced Roseker to 30 days in jail for perjury. Roseker, with the help of his attorney, C. E. Walsh, filed a writ of Habeas Corpus on January 25, 1924, claiming that Roseker was being unlawfully detained after he was not given a fair trial and that the court did not have jurisdiction in pronouncing his sentence, based on the perjury provisions set in the Statutes of Nebraska for 1922. On that same day, Judge Charles A. Goss ruled in favor of Roseker and ordered his release.
