  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 1, 1916, the Douglas County Circuit Court found Roy Stearns to be insane due to his use of "alcohol to excess" and had him committed to the Oregon State Hospital. On July 18, 1916, Stearns' attorney, Albert Abraham, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on his behalf, which was granted. Stearns maintained that there had been no physician present in the circuit court as required by law, a claim seemingly at odds with the order of commitment. By mutual assent, the two parties postponed the habeas proceedings to a later date, during which time the hospital released Stearns. With Stearns no longer bound, the judge dismissed the cause.
