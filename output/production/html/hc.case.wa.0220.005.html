IN THE DISTRICT COURT OF THE UNITED STATES FOR THE WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION.

IN THE MATTER OF THE APPLICATION OF ELSIE MILLER DICKINSON, ALIAS ELSIE MILLER, for a WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS:

NO. 2965 WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS.

UNITED STATES OF AMERICA, to Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration:

WHEREAS, it has been made to appear to our District Court that you are unlawfully detaining and unlawfully depriving of her liberty one Elsie Miller Dickinson, alias Elsie Miller, and we being willing that the cause of her detention should be inquired into to the end that justice may be afforded her, -

NOW, THEREFORE, you are COMMANDED and SOLEMNLY ENJOINED to produce before this court at a regular session thereof, appointed to be holden at the Federal Building in the City of Seattle, in said district, on the 24th day of February, 1915, the body of the said Elsie Miller Dickinson, alias Elsie Miller, together with the cause of her detention fully and at large set forth, and that you then and there this Writ have.

You are further Commanded that you do forthwith certify unto this court all of the records, documents and proceedings had before you, or before any officer of the Immigration Service in this district, or in your office, relating to the deportation of the said Elise Miller Dickinson, alias Elsie Miller, to the end that such things having been examined such order may be made therein as of right and justice ought to be made.

1  

It is further ORDERED that pending the hearing upon the return date of this writ, or to such other dates as the same may be continued, and until the final order of the court in the premises, the said Elsie Miller Dickinson, alias Elise Miller, be released and discharged from your custody upon giving a bond to the United States of America in the penal sum of one thousand dollars ($1000) conditioned for the obedience of the said Elsie Miller Dickinson to the orders of this court in the premises, and that she render herself into your custody in case such Writ of Habeas Corpus is by this court discharged.

Such bond to be in a form satisfactory to the United States District Attorney for this district and with sureties to him satisfactory.

WITNESS the Honorable Edward E. Cushman, Judge of said court, and the seal thereof, at Seattle, in said district, on the 18th day of February, 1915.

FRANK L. CROSBY
Clerk
By Ed M Lakin
Deputy.
