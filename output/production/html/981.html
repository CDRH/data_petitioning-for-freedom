  • Summary of Proceedings: Esther Hilderbrand submitted a petition for a Writ of Habeas Corpus on 23 September 1912, stating that Margaret Carr (whose assumed Convent name is Catherine) was being unlawfully detained and deprived of her liberty by the Mother Superior of the Good Shepherd Convent (whose first and real name is unknown). Judge Sutton ordered that Margaret Carr be discharged from the custody of the Mother Superior on 23 September 1912. He stated that the petition and “proofs” showed that Margaret Carr was being unlawfully detained and deprived of her liberty.
