#1433

Jacob Schuman}

Transcript Docket crim. Ex

On this 27th day of July A.D. 1886 A.L. McCauley complained under oath, that on the 27th day of July A.D. 1886, in the Precinct of Dayton in the County of Columbia Washington Territory, Jacob Schuman did unlawfully and in violation of the "local option law" sell to him, the said A.L. McCauley, one pt of Lager beer. Warrant issued and given to Sheriff JH Hosler at once, this warrant was returned on the same day and the defendant Jacob Schuman brought into Court, present R.F Sturdevant Prosecuting Atty and Sheriff Hosler with prisoner, when the examination was adjourned to the 29th day of August 1886 at 10 o'clock A.M. and the defendant Jacob Schuman ordered to enter into recognizance in the sum of five hundred dollars with good and sufficient sureties for his appearance and subjection to all the orders of the court. The defendant   filed his Recognizance with L. E. Harris as his surety, which was approved and the defendant Jacob Schuman was discharged from arrest. Precipe for witnesses on the part of the Territory filed by R.T. Sturdevant Pros. Atty. and subpoenas issued for A.L. McCauley and Geo V. Crossler July 28th subpoenas returned endorsed with service.

July 29th at 10 o'clock A.M. by request of B.L. Sharpstein of counsel for the Defendant this examination was continued to 2 o'clock P.M.. At 2 o'clock P.M. July 29th/86 by mutual consent this examination was adjourned to the 2d day of August 1886 at 10 o'clock A.M..

August 29 /86 now comes the attorneys in this case and by mutual agreement the further examination of this charge was adjourned to the 9th day of August 1886 at 10 o'clock A.M.. At 11 o'clock on the same day the defendant Jacob Schuman was surrendered to the Sheriff J.H. Hosler, by his surety L.E. Harris and by the said Sheriff to the Court. Wherefrom commitment to Jail was made and given to the said Sheriff.

August 7th 1886 Sheriff J.H. Hosler brought the Deft Jacob Schuman into court and the said defendant filed his recognizance in the sum of five hundred dollars with Jacob Weinhard as his surety which was approved and a certificate of discharge from the commitment, was made to the Sheriff J.H. Hosler.

August 9th at 10 o'clock A.M. on acct of the absence of the Pros. Atty. R.T. Sturdevant this examination was adjourned to the 16th day of August 1886 at 10 o'clock A.M. with the full consent of the defense.

Aug 16th at 10 o'clock A.M. now comes on this examination present Pros. Atty. R.T. Sturdevant, Sheriff J.H. Hosler Defendant Schuman and the witnesses A.L. McCauley and Geo V. Crossler who was sworn and examined on the part of the Territory. No defense made and the court having considered all and singular the premises of the case and the law and the evidence, ordered that the defendant Jacob Schuman be held to answer before the next grand jury for the Dist Court for Columbia County holding terms at   Dayton, in the sum of five hundred dollars with good and sufficient surety and in default thereof stand committed. Default being made commitment was made out and given to the Sheriff. The witnesses for the Territory were ordered to enter into personal recognizance in the sum of one hundred dollars each for their appearance at the said next term of the District Court, and A.L. McCauley and Geo V. Crossler executed and filed their recognizances as required which was filed and approved.

J.C. Dorr
Justice of the Peace
