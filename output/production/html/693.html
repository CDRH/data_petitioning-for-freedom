  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 15, 1922, Attorney John N. Baldwin petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of Willian Maier (alias Maher). The petition was against John Pszanowski, acting Chief of Detective, Michael Demsey, Chief of police, and Henry Dunn, Commissioner of Police. Baldwin claimed they unlawfully detained Maier for "implicitly in the transportation of liquor." There was no probable cause for his detention. Maier had no connection with the transportation of the said liquor because he was not physically present at the time of the crime the police allegedly accused him of committing. Additionally, Maier had no connection in any manner to any person associated with said transportation of liquor. On August 16, 1922, the Judge accepted the petition and filed a writ of Habeas corpus. On January 21, 1924, the Judge dismissed the case "for want of prosecution."
