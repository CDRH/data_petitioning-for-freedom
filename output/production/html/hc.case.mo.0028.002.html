To the Honorable James C. Moody Judge of the St Louis Circuit Court

The Petition of Heinrich Teusel respectfully represents that he is the husband of Rosa Teusel formerly Rosa Baumgarter, having been lawfully married to her in the City and County of St Louis on the 8th day of June 1865. That the said Rosa has since the 6th day of September 1865 and now is unlawfully detained and kept in custody by George Wolbrecht at his dwelling on the north east corner of Fourth and Elm Streets in the city of S Louis in said county

Your petitioner further states that the said Rosa on or about the said sixth day of Sept. 1865. was induced as your petitioner believes by certain false & unfounded statements of said Wolbrecht & members of his family and by the use of improper means and artifices on their part, to desert the house of your petitioner and was invighted[?] by them into the house of said Wolbrecht where she has ever since been and now is being there unlawfully detained by as aforesaid that your petitioner though he has applied at said house and to the persons in charge thereof for an interview with his said wife was denied access to her presence and was forcibly prevented from seeing her.

Your petitioner further states that the detention & restraint of said Rosa is illegal in this that your petitioner being the lawful husband of said Rosa is legally entitled to the society[?] care & custody of his said wife & that said Wolbrecht has no legal right to the custody of said Rosa & [?] to detain her from your petitioner or to restrain her of her liberty.

Your petitioner there fore prays Your Honor to issue a writ of Habeas Corpus directed to said George Wolbrecht commanding him to have the body of the said Rosa Teusel so detained by him before you at such time & place as may be named to do & receive what shall then & there be considered concerning the premises and that if on a hearing on the return of this writ it shall appear that your petitioner said Rosa Teusel is unlawfully detained from your petitioner by said George Wolbrecht & is restrained of her liberty that she be discharged from such restraint & detention.

Woerner & Kehr Attys for Petitioner

Heinrich Teusel the petitoner being duly sworn says the facts stated in the foregoing petition are true to the best of his knowledge & belief. Sworn to & subscribed to before me this 8 day of September 1865

H Teusel Witness[?] [?] [?] Seal of St Louis Circuit Court the date last aforesaid [?] [?] [?]
