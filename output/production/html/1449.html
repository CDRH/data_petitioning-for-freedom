  • Summary of Proceedings: In August 1902, Joseph Janda, a minor, enlisted in the U.S. Navy in Omaha, Nebraska, but took no further action. In November, he was arrested for desertion after having failed to show up at the Navy Yard in Norfolk, Virginia. In December, his parents, Thomas and Frances Janda, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, Joseph was released from custody.
