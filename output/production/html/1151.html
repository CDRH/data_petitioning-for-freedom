  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 12, 1920, Daisy Hartman was arrested in Omaha and charged with being an inmate of a common ill-governed house. Hartman was found guilty and sentenced to pay a fine of $25.00 and costs. Hartman subsequently provided $200.00 for an appeal bond which was approved by Charles E. Foster, the police magistrate, who then signed an order issuing Hartman's release from prison but mandated that she be held at the Detention Hospital of the city of Omaha if found to be infected with a venereal disease upon examination. On September 13 Hartman was taken to the Detention Hospital and subjected to physical examinations she did not consent to. Hartman and her attorney's filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus, arguing that her confinement in the Detention Hospital and the forced examinations constituted a violation of her rights under Article 5 of the Amendments to the Constitution of the United States. Hartman was released on bond, but her fate and the outcome of the trial are unknown.
