  • Summary of Proceedings: Jessie's mother, Rhoda, filed for a writ of habeas corpus. She placed her son under the care of Louis Figgins, but later claimed that Figgins (Fagan) unlawfully held her son and would not release him into her care. After hearing the evidence, Jessie was remanded to the custody of Figgins. In 1898, the county was ordered to pay the costs of the petition; it is unclear what prompted this payment or the impact it had on Fiandt's position.
