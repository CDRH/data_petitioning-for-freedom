In the District Court of the United States, in and for the Western District of Washington, Northern Division.

In the Matter of Marie Miler for a Writ of Habeas Corpus, } No. 2457

Reply of Marie Miller to Answer and Return of Ellis DeBruller, Commissioner of Immigration.

Comes now Marie Miler and by way of reply to the further and separate answer and further return of Ellis DeBruller, Commissioner, alleges:

I.

Petitioner admits that she was arrested by one Thomas M. Fisher, an Inspector of Immigration, on or about the 8th day of January, 1913, under purported authority from the Department of Labor of the United States, and charged with being an alien found in the United States in violation of the Act of Congress Feburary 20th, 1907, as amended by the Act approved March 26th, 1910, but petitioner specifically denies that she is a member of one of the classes of aliens required to be deported under the laws of the United States, as outlined by the said Act as amended March 26th, 1910, and specifically denies that she was connected with a house of prostitution and practiced prostitution and derived therefrom after she entered the United States.

II.

Petitioner further denies that she was duly and regularly examined by the said Thomas M. Fisher, acting Inspector of Immigration stationed at Seattle, Washington, and denies that he acted under the authority and laws of the United States and under the rules and regulations of the Bureau of Immigration and Department of Commerce and Labor of the United States. Petitioner futher denies specifically that she was informed that she might, if she wished, employ an attorney, but alleges the fact to be that it was not until after   the purported examination had been held that she was informed of her right to be represented by an attorney. Petitioner futher denies that the said Thomas M. Fisher, and other members of the Department of Commerce and Labor stationed at Seattle, Washington, endeavored to induce Roger Marchetti to present any other or further evidence which he mightthave touching the matter of said deportatioon, and denies further that A.T. White, Inspector of said Bureau, personally delievered to said Roger Marchetti a letter notifying him that further proceedings would be had on March 7th, 1913, at the Detention House in Seattle, Washington, and that at the close of said examination the record would then be submitted to the Secretary of Commerce and Labor for his decision.

III.

Petitioner denies that Roger Marchetti informed the Inspector that he could not take the matter up on March 7th, 1913, but would take it up the next morning without fail. Petitioner admits that the said Roger Marchetti did not appear in said proceeding or produce or make any effort to produce any other or further evidence, but specifically denies that she, the said Marie or Mary Miler, at any time did not offer to produce or procure any other or further evidence, but specfically alleges that she has been ready and willing at all times to procure further evidence to controvert the evidence introduced against her in this proceeding, but has been prevented from so doing. Petitioner further denies that by reason of the premises as set forth in Commissioner Ellis DeBruller's answer and return to the writ and petition herein on file, that this Court has no jurisdiction to review the Acts of the Secretary of Commerce and Labor, or the Acts of the Secretary of Labor who now has charge of said matter, and therefore prays in accordance with her petition herein now on file, and that your petitoner may be restored to her liberty.

Elias A. Wright
Sam A. Wright
Attorney for Marie Miler.  

United States of America, District of Washington. } ss.

Marie Miler being first duly sworn on oath deposes and says: That she is the petitoner in the above entitled action, that she has read the foregoing reply, knows the contents thereof and that the same is true.

Marie Miller Subscribed and sworn to before me this 2nd day of May, 1913. Elias A. Wright
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
