  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 14, 1919, Elizabeth Kreishutz petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus for her husband, Frank Kreishutz. She contended that the superintendent of the State Hospital for Insane, J. D. Case, unlawfully deprived her husband of his liberty. She did not know the superintendent’s real name when writing this petition. She argued that the superintendent claimed to be holding Frank Kreishutz because of a commitment. However, Elizabeth Kreishutz could not find a copy of such commitment or any process to mandate the superintendent to hold and keep Frank, so he was being held unlawfully. The judge accepted the petition and filed a writ of habeas corpus, which the superintendent responded to. However, the outcome of this proceeding is unknown.
