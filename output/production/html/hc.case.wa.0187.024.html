NOAH SHAKESPEARE
ATTORNEY AND COUNSELLOR AT LAW

Everett, Washington, June 1, 1914.

n. g.

Mr. C. A. Turner,
Immigrant Inspector,
Everett, Wash.

Dear Sir:

I have yours of the 29th May last advising that I might have the privilege of cross examining the several officers who have submitted their statements and views making up the record in Marie Robina Deportation proceedings. Being mindful of the fact that this matter has been pending since the month of January last, and realizing the impracticability and great inconvenience of getting the testimony of these officers located as they are in Salem, Oregon, Portland, Oregon, and Seattle, Washington, I am willing that the case be now submitted with a view to getting a decision without other and further delays, From the record in this case I note that my letter to you of date 21st May 1914 relative to this proceeding has been detached. In order that the contents of said letter may come to the notice of the department at Washington, D.C., may I ask that the same be attached and made a part of this record for whatever consideration the latter tribunal see fit to give it.

Yours very truly,

(Signed) Noah Shakespeare,
Attorney for Marie Robina, respondent herein. Dict.
