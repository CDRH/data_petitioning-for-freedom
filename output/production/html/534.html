  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 18, 1905, Donald McKerracher petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against United States Marshall Charles. B. Hopkins. Citing an annexed report from the Warden O. P. Halligan, McKerracher argued that he was entitled by a U.S. law to a deduction from his sentence due to good behavior on September 15, 1905, but that Hopkins refused to release him. On September 18, Hanford commanded that Hopkins show cause to the Court for McKerracher's detention and issued an order of discharge. United States Attorney Jesse A. Frye issued a response taking exception to the discharge of McKerracher and appealed to the Circuit Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit. Hanford gave McKerracher permission to be at large, conditional upon paying a $1500 recognizance with sureties; McKerracher's ultimate fate is unknown. For a similar and related case in which Judge Hanford ruled that a good behavior sentence reduction law applied retroactively, see Turner Jackson's linked case.
