In the District Court holding terms at Seattle, in King County, Washington Territory

In the matter of the Application of Ah Foong for a Writ of Habeas Corpus} No. [] Applications for writ of Habeas Corpus

To the Honorable Roger S. Greene Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Washington Territory and Judge of the above entitled Court.

The petition of Ah Foong respectfully shows

I

That said Ah Foong is unlawfully imprisoned, detained, confined and restrained of his liberty by William Murphy Acting Chief Police of said City of Seattle in the City Jail at Seattle in King County, Washington Territory that the said Ah Foong is a subject of the Emperor of China

II

That the cause or pretense of said restraint according to the best knowledge and belief of this applicant is this, to wit: that said Ah Foong was accused of violating a certain pretended   ordinance of the City of Seattle known as Ordinance number Seven hundred and five (705) entitled "An Ordinance to license tax and regulate auctioners, hawkers and peddlers," and upon such accusation was arrested and tried before George G. Lyon Esquire a Justice of the Peace in and for Seattle Precinct King County Washington Territory, and was by said Justice, convicted of a violation of said ordinance No 705 and sentenced to pay a fine of five dollars ($5.00) dollars and costs are amounting to eleven & 85=100 dollars ($11.85)

That in default of the payment of said fine and costs, your petitioner has been committed by said Justice to the custody of said William Murphy, Acting Chief of Police of the City of Seattle by whom he is now unlawfully imprisoned, detained, confined and restrained of his liberty by reason of his conviction and commitment as aforesaid

 

III

That said imprisonment, detention, confinement and restraint are illegal, and the illegality thereof consists in this to wit that the said pretended Ordinance No 705, by virtue of which your petitioner was convicted and committed as aforesaid is unconstitutional, illegal and void, for the reason that the said City of Seattle had not at the time of making said Ordinance, any power to make the same.

That said pretended Ordinance is unequal in this operation; oppressive[?] in restraint of trade and in contravention of common rights and in opposition to the laws and treaties of the United States.

Wherefore your petitioner prays that a writ of habeas corpus may be granted, directed to the sheriff said William Murphy, acting Chief of Police of the City of Seattle commanding him to have the body of the said Ah Foong before your honor at a time and place specified to do and receive what shall   4 then and there be considered by your honor concerning him, together with the time and cause of his, the said Ah Foong's detention, and said writ; and that he may be restored to liberty.

Ah Foong his [?] mark

Rasin & Gilman

Attys for Petitioner
