  • Summary of Proceedings: In December 1917, several dozen people were accused of having conspired to violate the Espionage Act earlier that year and jailed to await a grand jury hearing the following April. Bail was set at $5,000. In August 1918, nine individuals petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that they were still being detained despite never having been indicted. The respondents countered that the April 1918 term of the court was still ongoing and therefore the confinement was legal, adding that none of them had posted bail. After hearing the evidence, the writ was denied and the nine petitioners remanded to the custody of their jailers.
