  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 16, 1918, a justice of the peace for Linn County, Oregon, ordered George Uber to be committed to the state hospital. In early 1921, George Brown petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of Uber, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, Uber was released from custody on the grounds that a justice of the peace did not have the jurisdiction to commit people to the state hospital.
