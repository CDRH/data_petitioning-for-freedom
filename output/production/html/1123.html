  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 15, 1887, two sisters named Hilma and Ida Lundvall were adopted from the Portland Orphans' Home. Hilma, born in 1881, was adopted by Leuvica and William Hume, while her sister, born in 1879, was adopted by Leuvica's mother, Leuvica O. Hamilton. Both families lived in Chehalis County, Oregon. On September 17, 1888, the children's mother, Maria Kristina Peterson, filed a writ of habeus corpus to get custody of the children. She claimed that the children had not been given into their adoptive parents' custody by any kind of due process. After the hearing being delayed several times, judge Frank Allyn determined on September 13, 1889, that the children should stay with their adoptive parents and that Maria would have no contact with them.
