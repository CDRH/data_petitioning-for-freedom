  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 15, a complaint was made against Mary Doe alias Sloan for obtaining money under false pretenses. Oddly enough, her husband, John Sloan was the one brought into police court the next day. The proceedings against him are mysterious, as Sloan couldn't explain them to his attorney, J. Gerald MacVeigh. Nevertheless, Joseph was committed to the Douglas county jail on December 29, 1923. Shortly after, on New Year's Eve, 1923, MacVeigh filed a petition for a writ of habeus corpus demanding that the Douglas county sheriff, M.L. Endres, produce Joseph in court before judge Charles A. Goss. The case was dismissed on the motion of the applicant on the same day.
