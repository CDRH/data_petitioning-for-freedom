  • Summary of Proceedings: Oliver Filley, the mayor of St. Louis, accused Eliza Edwards of exposing her nine-year-old daughter, Martha, to a house of prostitution she was running, for which offense Martha was arrested and removed from her mother's custody. Eliza petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus to retrieve her daughter, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, the court ruled that Eliza could regain temporary custody of Martha on the condition that she leave St. Louis "to return with it to it's [sic] father."
