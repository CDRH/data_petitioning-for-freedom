UNITED STATES OF AMERICA. DISTRICT COURT FOR THE WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON.

In re Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina. | WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS.

UNITED STATES OF AMERICA, TO HENRY M. WHITE COMMISSIONER OF IMMIGRATION.

Whereas it has been made to appear to our District Court, that you are unlawfully detaining and unlawfully depriving of her liberty one Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, and we being willing that the cause of her detention should be enquired into, to the end that justice may be afforded her,

NOW THEREFORE you are COMMANDED AND SOLEMNLY ENJOINED to produce before this Court, at a regular session thereof, appointed to be holden at the Federal Building in the City of Seattle, in said District, on the 3 day of July, 1914, the body of the said Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, together with the cause of her detention, fully and at large set forth, and that you then and there this writ have.

YOU ARE FURTHER COMMANDED that you do forthwith certify unto this Court all of the records, documents and proceedings had before you, or before any officer of the Immigration service, in this District, or in your office, relating to the deportation of the said Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, to the end that such things having been examined, such order may be made therein as of right and justice ought to be made.

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that pending the hearing upon the return date of this writ, or to such other dates as the same may be continued and until the final order of this Court in the premises, the said Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina be released and discharged from your custody, upon ghe giving of a bond to the United States of America -1-   in the penal sum of $2000.00, conditioned for the obedience of the said Marie Robina Grayson, to the orders of this Court, in the premises, and that she will render herself into your custody in case such writ of habeas corpus is by this Court discharged.

Such bond to be in a form satisfactory to the United States District Attorney for this District, and with sureties to him satisfactory.

WITNESS the Honorable Jeremiah Neterer, Judge of said Court, and the seal thereof at Seattle, in said District on the 27th day of June, 1914.

Frank L. Crosby,
Clerk Ed M Lakin
Deputy Clerk.
