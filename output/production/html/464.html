  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 9, 1922, Louis Loukas file for habeas against Chief of Police, Micheal F. Dempsey, and acting Chief of Detectives, Ben Danbuam. In his petition, Loukas denied that charge of fugitive from justice, arguing that he had never been or lived in the state of Kansas. On the return of the issued writ, E. E. McKnight refers to a warrant issued by the Governor of Nebraska on behalf of the Governor of Kansas, under the charges of wife desertion and fugitive from justice. The case was later dismissed for want of prosecution on January 21, 1924.
