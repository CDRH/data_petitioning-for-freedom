  • Summary of Proceedings: Arrested on the charge of grand larceny, Daniel S. Conroy petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that Sherriff Miller was depriving him of his freedom. Judge Holmes granted the petition; however, after hearing evidence, he remanded Conroy back into custody. One month later, Conroy filed a motion to amend the record after Sherriff Miller admitted guilt. The motion was granted, but presumably Conroy remained in custody. Two years later, a Supreme Court decision affirmed the court's decision.
