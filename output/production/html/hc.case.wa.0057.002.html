In the District Court for the 3rd Judicial District of Washington Territory holding terms at Port Townsend for the Counties of Jefferson, Island, Clalam, Whatcom and San Juan.

Jefferson County S.S.

In the matter of the application of John Cain, John Brown, Victor Vaneck and Jose Carma for a Writ of Habeas Corpus

Upon reading the petition of the above named persons, duly signed and verified by them and filed in our said court, whereby it appears that the petitioners are illegally imprisoned and restrained of their liberty by J. J. H. VanBokkelen Sheriff and Depty US. Marshal at the County Jail in Port Townsend in the County of Jefferson Territory of Washington, and stating wherin the alleged illegality consists from which it appears to me that a writ of Habeas Corpus ought to issue:

It is ordered, that a writ of Habeas Corpus issue out of, and under the seal of our said Court, and that the Clerk thereof issue the same, directed to the said J.J. H. VanBokkelen, Sheriff, and Depty U.S. Marshal commanding him to have the bodies of the said petitioners before me, at Port Townsend in said Jefferson County on the 23 day of February 1875 at 2 O'Clock PM to do and receive what shall then and there be considered concerning the said petitioners together with   time and cause of their detention, and that he have then and there this the said writ.

O. Jacobs

Judge 3rd Judicial Dest. of Wash. Ty.

Dated February 23 1875
