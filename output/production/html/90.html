  • Summary of Proceedings: Junichi Yamamoto was ordered for deportation by U.S. Commissioner of Immigration Luther Weedin upon a warrant which claimed that Yamamoto was in violation of Rule 11 of the Immigration Rules for not possessing a Japanese passport when entering the U.S. Yamamoto petitioned for habeas claiming himself to be an able-bodied member of the work force, said deportation violated his right to remain silent in accordance with the fifth Amendment of the Constitution, and that there was no due process of law. Petitioner attorneys claimed Yamamoto was entitled to a three-year statue of limitation. Writ was denied and Yamamoto was deported.
