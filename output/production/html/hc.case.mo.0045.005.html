THE STATE OF MISSOURI, COUNTY OF SAINT LOUIS, } SS.

The State of Missouri to John Sturdy a member of the day[?] guard of the City of St Louis a Constable within and for St. Louis township and county, and to the Kepper of the County Jail of St. Louis county--Greeting:

YOU, THE SAID CONSTABLE, are hereby commanded to take and convey the body of George Washington Kerr to the St. Louis county jail, and there deliver him to the keeper thereof and

YOU, THE SAID KEEPER of said jail, are hereby commanded to receive the said George Washington Kerr at said jail, and there safely keep him in custody, until he shall be discharged by due course of law.

The Warrant of Commitment is issued by the undersigned Justice, because the said George Washington Kerr failed to give the required bail, dollars, for appearance before the St. Louis Criminal Court on the first day of the next term thereof, which will be on the first Monday in next a negro was this day brought before me the undersigned Justice by said John Sturdy and proven upon the oath of said John Sturday to be a runaway slave in pursuance of a Warrant issued against by said Justice, upon the affidavit of from the annexed transcript

Given under my hand, this 14 day of June 1856

Mr Herkenrath

Justice of the Peace

George Kerr. 22 yrs old, mulatto 5 ft 4 inches high crippled in left hand

No 86 June /56

The State vs George W Kerr a runaway slave

Warrt of commitment

Justice $1.50
