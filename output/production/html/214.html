  • Summary of Proceedings: In May 1872, William Kirkland pled guilty to the murder of Andrew Campbell in Chehalis County, Washington Territory. In August 1872, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, citing procedural and jurisdictional concerns, which was granted. Although his fate is unknown, his petition seems to have compelled Judge Roger Greene to subpoena multiple witnesses.
