  • Summary of Proceedings: In October 1895, William Garrett and John Meyers were arrested on the Winnebago Reservation in northeastern Nebraska and indicted on multiple counts including obstruction and assault. Both men paid a $500 bond and were released. The pair were involved with the Flournoy Live Stock and Real Estate Company, which had been illegally leasing Reservation land, and had resisted the efforts of the US government, including Winnebago police officers, to evict them. After having been released on bail but before their trial date, Garrett and Meyers petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. They claimed that the Winnebago Reservation had been effectively dissolved and that federal authorities had no jurisdiction to arrest them on state land. The respondents countered that the Reservation remained, that federal authorities retained jurisdiction, and that, in any case, Garrett and Meyers were not being detained. After hearing the evidence, the writ was denied and the petitioners ordered to pay the costs.
