  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 10th, 1887, Thomas Brown, G. Geods, George Downing, Charles Schubard, C. J. Young, and Peter Wegener were arrested for disobeying the orders of the master of the American ship Ivanhoe. They were jailed at McNeil Island Penitentiary, and one of their attorneys, Junius Rochester, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on their behalf. The petition was granted, and their case was heard by Judge Richard A. Jones. Judge Jones ruled in favor of the petitioners, and they were discharged from custody.
