  • Summary of Proceedings: Found guilty of perjury in 1897 and sentenced to four years hard labor in the Nebraska State Penitentiary, in 1900 Leonard Kephart petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that he was eligible for "commutation for good behavior as provided for by Section 569" of Nebraska's Criminal Code. Therefore, he claimed that warden John H. Hopkins was unlawfully imprisoning him. Judge Holmes granted the petition. After reviewing evidence, Holmes allowed the writ and ordered that Kephart be released from custody.
