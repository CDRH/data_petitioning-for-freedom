  • Summary of Proceedings: James Herbert Van Closter and Andrew W. McLaughlin filed a writ of Habeas Corpus because they claimed, they had been detained by Sheriff E. T. Werden with a pretended warrant for a pretended justice court. They were detained for the alleged fraudulent selling and disposing of mortgaged property during a lease, a complaint that was filed by John L. Miles. Closter and McLaughlin were then taken by Werden from Omaha, where they lived, to the county of Nuckolls, Nebraska. Closter and McLaughlin were released from custody on bail and made to return on the 5th of June to be released from confinement by Judge Lee S. Estelle.
