  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 28, 1924, Samuel R. Thompson was arrested on the charge of being a fugitive from justice from Illinois that was wanted for wife abandonment. On June 6, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus and argued that he was being illegally detained for the purposes of extradition to Illinois. The respondent replied the same day and stated his reason for arresting Samuel. On May 31, Illinois Governor Len Small addressed a petition to Nebraska Governor Charles W. Bryan asking him to apprehend and return Samuel to Illinois. On June 3, Governor Bryan issued a warrant for Samuel’s arrest to all law enforcement officers in the state. According to the appearance docket, the hearing for the writ continued on June 7 and the judge discharged Samuel from custody.
