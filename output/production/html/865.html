  • Summary of Proceedings: Bessie Berry was arrested and tried in a Police Court in Omaha on the charge of vagrancy. This charge was successfully appealed on July 6, 1918, and an order of release was issued by Police Judge James M. Fitzgerald. Berry was then transferred to a detention home, when Berry, by attorney John M Macfarland filed for habeas against Police Captain, Henrey Heitfeld who refused to release Berry, C. C. Tomlinson who is in charge of the detention home and refused to grant Macfarland consultation with Berry, as well as Ernest T. Manning. The petition was granted, and the writ allowed; Berry was discharged on July 9th.
