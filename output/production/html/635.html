  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 29, 1921, police officers arrived at the home of Mabel McKillip and her husband, and compelled her to accompany them to the jail where she was forcibly imprisoned. Charges for vagrancy and prostitution were brought against her, and on September 30th, McKillip was fined $15.00 and costs for vagrancy. McKillip paid this amount and was not released, but instead held in the Detention Home. McKillip subsequently filed a petition for a Writ of Habeas Corpus, arguing that the charge of vagrancy does not authorize a commitment to the Detention Home. On January 21, 1924, the petition for a Writ was dismissed for want of prosecution.
