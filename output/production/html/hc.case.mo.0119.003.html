To the Honorable Law Commissioners Court of St. Louis County:

Your petitioners respectfully represents that one Martha Edwards, a female minor child of the age of nine years, is unjustly and unlawfully imprisoned and restrained of her liberty by one Chas. G. Ward, at his place of residence on Eighth street between Locust and St. Charles streets, in the city of St. Louis or his agents or servants that your petitioner is the mother of said child, and her legal guardian, and entitled to her care and custody; that said child was forcibly and violently taken from your petitioner, at her place of residence in said city, by two persons by name to your petitioner unknown, and forcibly and violently incarcerated and confined at the place of residence of said Ward as aforesaid that said child was taken and confined as aforesaid as your petitioner believes, without any process, warrant, or other authority of law that your petitioner is now denied by said Ward, his servants and agents, admission to the company and presence of her said child as is her right and is prevented from providing for and protecting her said child, as it is her right and duty to do and that no application for the relief sought for by this petition, has been made to and refused by any Court officer or officers, inferior to your Hon Court our petitioner therefore prays that a writ of habeas corpus may be issued to said Chas. G. Ward, commanding him to produce the body of said Martha Edwards before your Honorable Court, that she may be restored to the custody of your petitioner

Eliza Edwards her mark

Duly sworn before me on this 24th day of Augt 1858 - H Dusenbury Law Com

Eliza Edwards, the petitioner, makes oath and says that the matters and things stated in the within and foregoing petition, are true, to the best of her knowledge and belief

Eliza Edwards her x mark

"By the Habeas Corpus Act"
