  • Summary of Proceedings: Henry Carlton was arrested by N. Soderberg, the Justice of the Peace for Seattle, in April 1888 and was convicted of the crime of vagrancy by gambling. Carlton petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that the commitment from N. Soderberg did not show Carlton had been convicted, that the court who issued the commitment did not have jurisdiction over the issue, and that it was not a commitment at all. R. A. Jones, a District Judge, ordered that the defendant should remain in custody on April 28, 1888, and Carlton returned to the custody of William Cochrane, the sheriff of King County, Washington Territory.
