  • Summary of Proceedings: Lelah Puc-Ka-Chee was detained by Indian Agent, W. G. Malin and brought to a the American Indian Boarding School at Toledo, Iowa. She was brought there under the guardianship of her court-appointed guardian, Malin. Her husband petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus under his and her name. Circuit judge, O. P. Shiras, ruled that Malin had no right to guardianship over Lelah Puc-Ka-Chee, who was released from his custody and was granted her freedom. Furthermore, Shiras asserted that as a married woman, and therefore a legal adult, Puc-Ka-Chee had the right to decide whether or not she would remain enrolled at the school.
