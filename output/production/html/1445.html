  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 12th, 1889, the District Court for the 2nd Judicial District of Washington ordered Albert Tait to provide testimony in the case against C E. Case. The relationship between the two men is unknown; however, Tait refused to provide his testimony and eluded the court's commands. Sheriff James H. Price of Pierce County, Washington, promptly arrested Tait for refusing to comply with the court's orders. Tait responded by filing a habeas corpus petition on August 14th, 1889. Upon hearing the arguments of the bound and holding parties, Judge Frank Allyn denied Tati's writ and ordered him to appear in Case's trial to provide testimony.
