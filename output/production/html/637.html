  • Summary of Proceedings: Attorney John E. Costello filed the petition on behalf of his client, Jimmy Jardini (aka Jimmy Russell), claiming he was being held unlawfully. Jardini was confined, beginning December 1, 1888, to await a Grand Jury to be impaneled on February 1, 1889 to indict him for the crime of attempt to commit rape. It is not known why he was still in jail a month later, and it is not known whether he was indicted (presumably he was not.) In an unusual ruling, Judge Thomas Burke, rather than ruling on the petition itself and ordering the jail to produce Jardini, ruled (on the same day in fact) that the writ be granted.
