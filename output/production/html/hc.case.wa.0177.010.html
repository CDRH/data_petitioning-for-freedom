EXHIBIT G.

UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION

In the Matter of the Application of Tonka Bajlo for the Writ of Habeas Corpus.

No. 2459 1328

AFFIDAVIT OF JERRY BOLANCIA

STATE OF WASHINGTON COUNTY OF KING ) ss.

Jerry Bolancia being first duly sworn on oath deposes and states: My name is Jerry Bolancia; I reside at 810 Charles Street, Seattle, Washington; I am employed as a watchman with the Rainer Foundry Company, corner Stacy and East Waterway Streets, Seattle, Washington; that I know Tonka Bajlo and have known her for about one year; that during that time she was employed as cook and housekeeper on 10th Avenue, Seattle, Washington, for some Austrians; that I visited the house frequently as I had friends living there; that on the occasions of my visits I frequently saw Tonka Bajlo and talked with her and never saw anything wrong in her conduct; that I never heard anything wrong about her character; that I knew her husband and have heard him say in the presence of others at 810 Charles Street, the home of Jerry Bolancia, that he had beat his wife frequently and that he had jumped upon her after throwing her down and stomped her with his feet and beat her with his fists several times while in the old country; that I heard Tonka Bajlo say that she would not live with him because he had beat her so often and injured her health; that during the fore part of April 1913, I heard him say to his wife that if she would live with him he would not beat her any more and she said that she did not believe him and would not live with him; that I know   she has been ill and to the hospital while she has been living at 815 Charles Street; that I heard him say he would send her or have her sent to the old country; that I know that people of the Austrian nationality and other old country nationalities frequently band together and hire a house and a woman for a housekeeper and cook, as they find it cheaper and more satisfactory than to live in boarding houses; that women who act as housekeepers and cook are thought well of and no ill thoughts are entertained towards them because of the fact that they accept such employment; that I know a number of Austrians and any of them never say a bad word against Tonka Bajlo; that Tonka Bajlo told me that she had two children in the old country; that from what I know of her during the past year that she has been in Seattle, I believe her to be a woman of good character.

Jerry Bolancia (his X mark)

Witnesses to signature:

Frank Millowan Ivan Skorich

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 15th day of April, 1913.

Chas. H. Ennis.
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
