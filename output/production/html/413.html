  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 4, 1864, Walla Walla county policeman W.J. Tompkins got a letter from an A. Lavor in Montana asking him to arrest a J.M. Burks and William Moore. He did, and incarcerated the men in the city jail. On October 3, 1864, the men petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, saying that there was no evidence proving that they had committed a crime and a formal complaint against them had never been filed. Judge E.P. Oliphant ordered the men released, stating that they were innocent.
