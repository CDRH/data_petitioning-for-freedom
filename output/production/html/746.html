  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 3, 1917, Franklin Kapps, a Chicago man taking the train through Nebraska with his wife and child, was arrested without a warrant. According to the omaha Daily Bee, he was accused of larceny after he had taken some documents from his former office. With the help of his attorney, Daniel H. Horrigan, he filed a writ of habeus corpus for his freedom against the Omaha chief of police Henry W. Dunn on February 5. Judge Willis S. Sears not only allowed the writ, but ordered his release on February 6th of that year.
