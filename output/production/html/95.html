  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 2, 1919, George Duncan filed for habeas after being arrested on August 15th for vagrancy. In his petition, Duncan argued that police judge Foster's sentence of $100 exceed the $50 maximum in the 1913 revised statutes of Nebraska. A writ was issued, and Duncan was discharged after the payment of costs.
