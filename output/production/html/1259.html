  • Summary of Proceedings: On November 28, 1922, the justice of the peace for Council Bluffs, Iowa issued an order accusing Viola McAtee Jacobson of the crime of conspiracy, and requesting that she either be brought before him or the nearest magistrate. On this order Omaha Chief of Police Michael Dempsey detained Jacobson and held her at the police headquarters. On this same day, William G. Hastings filed for a petition of a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of Viola McAtee Jacobson, alleging that she is a resident and citizen of Nebraska and has not been charged with committing any crime against the state of Nebraska. On this same day Jacobson was released under a written bond for $750.00, the bond was later exonerated on December 1 and the application for a writ of Habeas Corpus was granted.
