  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 3, 1924, Florence Godly was sentenced by Judge John McGee to 12 months in the Lancaster County Common Jail and ordered to pay a $500 fine for violating the National Prohibition Act. On November 29, 1924, she filed a petition for a writ of habeas corpus and argued that she should be discharged after serving four months in the Lancaster County Jail because the cumulative sentences imposed upon her did not specify the order in which she needed to serve them. She also cited Sec. 1042 of the Revised Statutes of the U.S. and argued that she had served 30 extra days in replacement of the fine. On December 2, 1924, the petition and writ were allowed and the judge ordered her release from police custody after the hearing on Dec. 3, 1924. On March 13, 1925, the respondents appealed the case to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Eighth Circuit in St. Paul, Minnesota. On January 30, 1926, the decision of the lower court was reversed and Chief Justice William Taft remanded Florence to the custody of the respondents. The higher court also refered to the Cronin vs. William J. Fox decision.
