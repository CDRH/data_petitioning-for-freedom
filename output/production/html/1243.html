  • Summary of Proceedings: Petitioner Emma Clifton asserts that her children (Charles, Roy, and Clarice) were being unlawfully restrained of their liberty by Charles Clifton, Sr. in Douglas County. She explained that a court order from 16 November 1892, modified on 28 November 1892, assigned custody of the aformentioned children to her and that Charles Clifton refused to uphold this decree. Because of this, she asked for a writ of habeas corpus so that the children can be “discharged from said unlawful imprisonment.” The custody agreement was attatched to the court files as Exhibit A. This document finalized the divorce of Charles and Emma Clifton, concluded that Charles must pay allimony, and that the custody of the children shoul be assigned to Emma. Judge Scott ordered that the writ be allowed. On 2 December 1892, Judge Scott stated that the children were being unlawfully restrained and ordered that they be discharged from the custody of Charles Clifton.
