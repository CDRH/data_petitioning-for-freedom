  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 28, 1916, J. Sidney Smith was convicted of conspiracy to use the United States mails for a defrauding scheme and was sentenced to two years in the United States Penitentiary in Leavenworth, Kansas along with a fine of $10,000. On September 29, 1921, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus and argued that newspaper coverage of the trial made the jurors biased and unable to be fair and impartial. He cited the 5th and 6th Amendments of the Constitution in his petition. His writ was denied and his fate is unknown.
