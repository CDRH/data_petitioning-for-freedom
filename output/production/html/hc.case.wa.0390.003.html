Territory of Washington
County of Walla Walla } SS

At Chambers, October 13th, 1869, before James K. Kennedy, Judge 1st judicial district.

James McAuliff, Sheriff of Walla Walla County makes affidavit filed therein charging John Suelling with committing the crime of Grand Larceny in Umatilla County, State of Oregon, in the latter part of the year 1868, and escaping from the County Jail, to which he had been committed, and that he was is a fugitive from justice, liable to be demanded by the Governor of Oregon, from the Governor of Washington Territory.

The defendant John Suelling was brought before me, and cautioned to say nothing to incriminate himself; his position was fully explained to him.

He then voluntarily stated that he had been committed to the jail of Umatilla County to answer the offense of larceny.

James McAuliff was then sworn, and testified as follows.

The prisoner, John Suelling,   voluntarily stated to me that he broke out of the jail of Umatilla County, Oregon, by sawing his way out with a saw which some person from the outside had given him. The Sheriff of Umatilla County is now about to gone to get a requisition for him

Subscribed and sworn before me this 13th day of October 1869

James McAuliff

Whereupon the prisoner was ordered to enter into recognizance, conditioned for his appearance on or before the 31st day October 1869, to answer any demand that might be made for his body on a requisition, in the sum of five hundred dollars, or be committed in default, to the County Jail of Walla Walla County till said day.
