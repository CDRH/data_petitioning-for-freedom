  • Summary of Proceedings: On March 28, 1919, B. E. Atherton filed a petition for a writ of habeas corpus, claiming that Marshall Eberstein unlawfully deprived him of his liberty. He stated that the police held him in the Omaha City Jail for a complaint filed against him in Minneapolis, Minnesota, charging him with wife and child abandonment in the state. However, he claimed that he had been a resident of Omaha for the past twelve years. Additionally, on the contrary, his wife abandoned him in Omaha about three years ago. The same day, the judge accepted the petition and filed a writ of habeas corpus. However, the judge dismissed the writ for “wants of prosecution at relator’s costs.”
