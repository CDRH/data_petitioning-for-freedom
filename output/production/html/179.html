  • Summary of Proceedings: Hans A. Hulke filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on May 24th, 1921, after being arrested with no complaint filed against him. He argued that Michael F. Dempsey was holding him in custody illegally and that no evidence or affidavit had been provided to justify his arrest. The judge agreed to hear his plea and ordered Dempsey to return Hulke to court for a petition hearing. The petition's outcome is unknown; however, the suit was continued in a separate case file.
