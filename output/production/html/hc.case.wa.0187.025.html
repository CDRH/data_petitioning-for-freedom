Everett, Wash., May 21, 1914.

n.g.

Chas. A. Turner, Esq.,
U. S. Immigration Inspector,
Everett, Washington.

Dear Sir:

I have to acknowledge receipt of record in re Marie Robina, alian Lena Mitchell, deportation proceedings, which since I last saw it has had attached certain communications from Immigrant Inspectors Barbur, Barker, and Fisher which I have read. Such communications are attached I presume for the purpose of giving information to the Department which will affect its action in the matter of its decision in this deportation case.

It is probably true that in matters of this kind the same rules of evidence do not apply as in Common Law Courts, but as far as my knowledge goes I have never known that all rules of evidence had been abolished in proceedings of this kind, and that the impressions, surmises, guesses and dreams of Inspectors at divers places would have conferred upon them the dignity of evidence.

The sole and only issue in this case is whether or not Marie Robina was a citizen of the United States at the time of her arrest January 24th, 1914.

The communications from the different Immigration Inspectors might make an interesting chapter in a work of the character of Gaboriau or Paul Kock, or possibly the world renowned series of Ned Buntline's Five Cent Detective series, but when any higher degree of literary merit is claimed for them it undoubtedly appears that the party so claiming is in gross error.

The Department of course will bear in mind that these various effusions result apparently of div rs brai n s torms in these officials while they assert no fact positively, do state a number of conjectures, surmises and guesses, and that the party most affected by these various lucubrations has had no opportunity to meet them.

It is supposed that the Department will brush aside as mere teeming fancies the matters which are beside the real question, and will not allow such things to affect its determination in any regard whatever, where the civil rights of the party are determined under the Constitution and the Laws enacted in pursuance thereof.

Respectfully submitted,

(Signed) Noah Shakespeare, Attorney for Respondent, Marie Robina
