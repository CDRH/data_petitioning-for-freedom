To the Honorable John W Colvin Judge of the Law Commissioners Court

The Petition of Alexander S. Butler would respectfully show to your honor that he was arrested in the city on or about the 10th day of September 1854 by M. Lawyer a policemen as a runaway slave that he was taken before one Liberty Waite a Justice of the Peace and by him sent to the jail of St Louis County kept by one John Hempstead where your petitioner is still confined- that the warrant by which he is held is illegal informal and incomplete - that he is illegally restrained of his liberty that he demanded a copy of the process by which he is held but was refused

Your petitioner further states that he is a free Negro was born in Pennsylvania of free parents has always acted and locomoted as a free person and that he can prove that he is free.

Your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may issue in his case directed to the said John Hempstead commanding him to bring the body of your petitioner before your honor and on its appearing that he is illegally restrained of his liberty that your petitioner may be discharged.

MacDonal for Petitioner
