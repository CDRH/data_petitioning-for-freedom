  • Summary of Proceedings: The bound party, James Cummings, having allegedly committed Grand Larceny in Yellow Medicine County, Minnesota was issued an arrested warrant. On or about November 5th, 1919, Alfred C. Anderson reported that Cummings was allegedly a fugitive from Justice. This report prompted Police Magistrate James M. Fitzgerald to authorize the arrest of Cummings. The bound party and his attorney then initiated an application for a writ of Habeas Corpus on November 5th. The writ was allowed to issue but was denied and the the petitioner remained in custody.
