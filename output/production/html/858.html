  • Summary of Proceedings: On January 7, 1922, Edward Toman filed for habeas with attorney John M. Berger, against R. D. Clark and chief of police, Michael Dempsey. In the petition, Toman argued that he had been taken into custody by Clark and police on January 6 to be transferred to the State Industrial School at Kearney, NE. Toman further argued that he had committed no crime to warrant such detention by the school. A writ was issued but the case was dismissed for want of prosecution on January 21, 1924.
