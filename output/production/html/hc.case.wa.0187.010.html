UNITED STATES OF AMERICA, DISTRICT COURT FOR THE WESTERN DIVISION OF WASHINGTON, NORTHERN DIVISION.

In re Marie Robina Grayson alias Marie Robina for Writ of Habeas Corpus. Petitioner.

No 2780 PETITION

Iron[?]

No 1.

TO THE HONORABLE THE JUDGES OF THE COURT ABOVE ENTITLED: Now comes Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, represents and shows unto yourhonors, that she is a citizen of the United States of America, over the age of twenty-one (21) years, a resident and citizen of the State of Washington, and the District and Division of the above entitled court before specified. That your petitioner is a native of the Province of Bretagne, in the Republic of France, and came to the United States of America in December of 19o2 where your petitioner has since resided.

No 2.

That your petitioner intermarried with William Grayson at Salem, in the State of Oregon, on the 1st day of April 1912, and thereafter at all times has been and still is the lawful wife of the said William Grayson. That at the time of said intermarriage of your petitioner and William Grayson in the said State of Oregon, in the said United States of America, the said William Grayson was a natural born citizen of the United States of America, having been born in the City of St. Louis, in the State of Missouri, in the said United States of America, and never having renounced the alegiance acquired by such birth. That thereafter and on or about the 23rd January 1914 your petitioner was taken into custody by one C. A. Turner Immigration Inspector of the United States upon a warrant issued by the Assistant Secretary of the Department of Commence and Labor of the United States charging yourpetitioner with being an alien in the United States of America, and of   page 2.- practising prostitution after her entry into the United States of America. That thereafter a hearing was had before the said C. A. Turner as such Immigration Inspector upon such charge and thereafter your petitioner was taken into custody and is now in custody of Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration under and by virtue of a warrant issued by the Secretary of the Department of Commerce and Labor of the United States of America, directing that yourpetitioner be deported the United States of America, and transported to the Republic of France for the reason as stated in such warrant, that your petitioner is an alien to the United States of America and has been found practising prostitution in such United States of America.

No 3.

Your petitioner further avers that she is restrained of her liberty by the said Henry M. White as such Commissioner of Immigration under a claim that she is subject to deportation for a violation of the laws of the United States of America, in that, being an alien she was found practising prostitution in the United States of America.

No 4.

Your petitioner further avers that she is held in custody under and by virtue of a claim that she has violated the laws of the United States of America and isbeing held in such custody within the District and Division aforesaid.

No 5.

Your petitioner further avers that she is unable to attach hereto a copy of the process under which she is held in custody and restrained of her liberty, for the reason that she has no copy in her possession and for the reason that she has not been furnished with such a copy.

No 6.

Your petitioner however avers that she is being now restrained of her liberty and held in the custody of Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, under a pretense of a law of the United States of America.

  page 3.-

No 7.

Your petitioner further avers that at the hearing before the said C. A. Turner, Immigration Inspector, it appeared conclusively and without contradiction that your petitioner at all times since her said marriage wi th the said William Grayson has been a resident of the said United States of America and a citizen thereof, andthat the said warrant of the Secretary of the Department of Commerce and Labor is invalid and of no force and effect in law, for the reason that yourpetitioner is not an alien, and has not been an alien since the institution of such deportation proceedings.

No 8.

You petitioner now complains that she is unlawfully restrained of her liberty by the said Henry M. White under the pretense that she has violated a law of the United States of America which pretense and claim is unfounded in fact as is to the said Henry M. White and to the Secretary of the Department of Commerce and Labor well known.

No 9.

Your petitioner avers that in her said confinement by the said Henry M. White, her rights as an American citizen are infringed and she is deprived of her liberty without any warrant of law, and that such restraint is illegal and invalid.

No 10.

Your petitioner further avers that in the hearing before the officers of the Immigration Service of the United States she was denied the right to cross examine the witnesses, to meet the witnesses face to face, and that your petitioner in such hearing before the officers of the Immigration bureau of the United States was denied a fair and impartial hearing, and that there was no testimony taken before the said C. A. Turner tending to show or which did show that your petitioner was at the time of the institution of such proceedings or at anytime subsequent to her said intermarriage with said William Grayson an alien to the United States of America.

  page 4.-

WHEREFORE, your petitioner prays that she may be allowed the writ of right, a writ of habeas corpus to the Honorable Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration directed, commanding and requiring the said Henry M. White to produce before your honors at a time and place to be specified, the body of your petitioner together with the cause alleged for her detention by the said Henry M. White, and that she further be awarded a writ of certiorari to the Hon. Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration, commanding and requiring said Commissioner to certify unto this court, all and singular the proceedings had before C. A. Turner as such Immigration Inspector respecting the matter of the deportation of yourpetitioner, and that thereupon, upon the return to such writ of habeas corpus and the coming in of the return of such writ of certiorari such order be had thereon by your Honors as upon due consideration is consonant with law under and pursuant to the Constitution of the United States of America, and as in duty bound, your petitioner will ever pray.

Mary Robina Grayson
PETITIONER Noah Shakespeare
and Louis A. Merrick
