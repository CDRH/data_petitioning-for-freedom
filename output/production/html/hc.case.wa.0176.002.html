United States District Court Western District of Washignton Northern Division.

In the Matter of The Application of Marie Miler for a Writ of Habeas Corpus. } No. 2457

Answer and Return to Writ and Petition Therefor.

To the Honorable Edward E. Cushman, Judge of the above entitled Court:

Comes now Ellis DeBruler, Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, Washington, and for answer to the Writ of Habeas Corpus issued herein, and for answer to the petition filed herein, states as follows:

I

That for answer to Paragraph One of said petition, said Ellis DeBruler admits the same.

II

That for answer to Paragraph Two of said petition, said Ellis DeBruler admits that the petitioner came to the United States about the year 1909, where she has since resided, but denies each and every other allegation therein contained.

III

That for answer to Paragraph Three of said petition, said Ellis DeBruler admits that complaint was made by him in his official capacity at Seattle, Washington, against the said alien, and that as the result thereof, certain proceedings   were had, and that on or about March 18, 1913, the petitioner was ordered deported and sent from the United States of America, and denies each and every other allegation contained in said paragraph.

IV

That for answer to Paragraph Four of said petition, said Ellis DeBruler denies each and every allegation therein contained, except such as are specifically admitted in the further and separate answer and return herein contained.

V

That for answer to Paragraph Five of said petition, said Ellis DeBruler denies each and every allegation therein contained.

VI

That for answer to Paragraph Six of said petition, said Ellis DeBruler admits that the government of the United States was about to deport said petitioner, and would have so deported her on or about April 16, 1913, unless the Court had issued its Writ of Habeas Corpus, and denies each and every other allegation therein contained.

VII

That for answer to Paragraph Seven of said petition, said Willis DeBruler admits that he is detaining said petitioner in his official capacity as United States Commissioner of Immigration, at the Dentention Station in Seattle, and refuses to release her from custody, and denies each and every other allegation therein contained.

VIII

And for a further and separate answer and return to said application for a Writ of Habeas Corpus, and for further (2)   return to said Writ, said Ellis DeBruler alleges:

That on or about the 8th day of January, 1913, siad Maria, or Mary, Miler, was arrested by one Thos M. Fisher, an Inspector of Immigration, stationed at Seattle, Washington, under authority from the Department of Labor of the United States in violation of the Act of Congress of Feburary 20, 1907, as amended by the Act approved March 26, 1910, to-wit: That she is and was a member of one of the classes of aliens required to be deported under the laws of the United States, to-wit: (That the said alien was connected with a house of prostitution, and practiced prostitution and derived benefits therefrom after she entered the United States.) That said authority for said arrest and detention is found in the warrant duly and regularly issued on the 7th day of January, 1913, by Ben J. Cabel, Acting Secretary of Commerce and Labor, copy of said warrant being attached hereto and made a part of this return.

That on January 13, 1913, said Marie, or Mary, Miler was duly and regularly examined by said Thos. M. Fisher, who was then and there a duly qualified and acting Inspector of Immigration, stationed at Seattle, Washington, acting under the authority of the laws of the United States, and the rules and regulations of the Bureau of Immigration and Department of Commerce and Labor of the United States, and as such Inspector, had power to examine said Marie, or Mary, Miler, under oath as to her right to remain in the United States. That at said hearing the said Marie, or Mary, Miler was duly informed that she might, if she wished, employ an attorney, and that she then stated that she would have a friend (3)   of hers get an attorney, and that she afterwards did employ an attorney, to-wit: One Roger Marchetti, to represent her, and that thereafter on numerous and sundry occasions said Inspector, Thos. M. Fisher, and other members of the Department of Commerce and Labor, stationed at Seattle, Washington, endeavored toinduce the said Roger Marchetto to present any other or further evidence which he might have touching the matter of said deportation, and finally, on March 5, 1913, A.T. White, Inspector of said Bureau, personally delievered to said Roger Marchetti a letter notifying him, the said Roger Marchetti, that further proceedings would be had in said matter on March 7, 1913, at the Dentention House in Seattle, Washington, and a failure on his part to appear would be considered as notice that he had no further evidence to produce, and that the record would then be submitted to the Secretary of Commerce and Labor for his decision, a copy of which letter is hereto attached and made a part hereof.

That on March 7, 1913, the said Roger Marchetti, informed the Inspector that he could not take the matter up that day, but would take it up the next morning without fail, and neither the next morning, nor at any other time, did the said Marie, or Mary, Miler, at any time offer to produce or procure any other or further evidence, and that thereupon the record of said proceeding, together with the report of the examining Inspector, and with the report of Commissioner Ellis DeBruler, was forwarded to the Department of Labor at Washington, D.C., and recieved by it, and said Department (4)   did then and there carefully consider said matters, and on the 18th day of March, 1913, said Department, and also W. B. Wilson, Secretary of Labor, became and were satisfied that said alien was one who had been found practicing prostitution and was a prostitute after her entry to the United States, and decided adversely to said alien, and ordered her deported to the county from whence she came.

That a copy of the testimony so taken at the hearing aforesaid is attached hereto and made a part hereof, and that in addition, other documents considered in said examination of the matter are attached hereto, and made a part hereof.

Special attention is called to the following documents, found on pages of the record indicated:

1. Copy of complaint by Commissioner, Ellis DeBruler, (p.79), with Report of Inspector Fisher attached thereto (80-81).

2. Original warrant by Ben J. Cable, Acting Secretary of Commerce and Labor, for arrest of the alien, Mary Miler, and hearing on charge as to deportation (p.77)

3. Original report of Acting Commissioner Brym H. Uhl, as to record of landing of alien, Mary Miller (p.72)

4. Copy of proceedings had and testimony taken before Insepctor Thos. M. Fisher, on the hearing held under the complaint and warrant. (pp 36-44 inc).

5. Copy of affidavits of Angelo Bassinni, G. Polet and Vincent Petrosini, submitted to the Department to be used in the matter of Marie or Mary Miller, and which were so used (pp 32, 33, and 34).

(5)  

6. Copy of Certificate of the Medical Inspector showing alien afflicted with Gonorrhoea (p.48).

7. Copy of Report of Immigrant Inspector, B.A. Hunter, to Commissioner DeBruler, showing steps taken to induce the attorney of Marie Miler to Proceed with the matter and present evidence why she should not be deported, which steps respondent alleges were actually taken (p. 46).

8. Copy of letter which Respondent alleges was personally handed to the attorney for Mary or Marie Miller, by inspector A. T. White, on March 5, 1913 (p. 60).

9. Copy of findings by Inspector Thos. M. Fisher, after March 8, 1913, when alien's attorney had agreed to present his evidence, but in spite of verbal and written notices, and promises, had failed so to do (p.45).

10. Copy of letter of Ellis DeBruler, Commissioner, to the Commissioner General at Washington, enclosing the record, containing all the above matters, and recommending the deportation of Mary or Marie Miller (pp 19,20).

11. Letter of the Commissioner General, Daul J. Keege, to Commissioner Ellis DeBruler, announcing the decision which the department reached on the record and recommendation referred to above, said decision being that the alien was in the United States contrary to law. (p.8).

12. Copy of warrant by W. B. Wilson, Secretary of Labor, stating that he is satisfied that the alien is a (6)   prostitute and has been found practicing prostitution subsequent to her entry into United States, and ordering her deportation (p. 1).

13. Letter to Commissioner DeBruler, from Commissioner Williams, based on above warrant, and authorizing Commissioner DeBruler to bring the alien to Ellis Island at any time (p. 12).

That the foregoing are the reasons why the said Marie, or Mary, Miler, is detained by Ellis DeBruler, in custody, and why the said Ellis DeBruler now detains her in custody pending the orders of the above entitled court in the premises; that by reason of the premises, this Court has no jurisidiction to review the acts of the Secretary of Commerce and Labor, or the acts of the Secretary of Labor, who now has charge of said matter.

Wherefore, said Ellis DeBruler prays the above entitled Court that the said writ be dismissed and the application and petition denied.

C.F. Riddell
