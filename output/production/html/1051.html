  • Summary of Proceedings: In December 1874, J. J. Johnson pled guilty to selling liquor to Indians and was sentenced to three months in jail, ordered to pay a $300 fine, and threatened with an additional three months in the event he did not pay said fine. In March 1875, Henry Struve, secretary of the Washington Territory, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on his behalf, arguing that Johnson had not conducted the transaction in Indian Country, which was granted. The fate of Johnson is unknown.
