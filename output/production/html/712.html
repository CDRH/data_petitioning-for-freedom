  • Summary of Proceedings: Jacob Penner petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that he was unlawfully detained by James L. Greene, Superintendent of the Nebraska Hospital for the Insane. Judge Cornish granted the petition. When Penner came before the court, Cornish ruled that Penner be released on his own recognizance. Cornish also set the hearing date for March 10th, 1906. The writ outcome and fate of the bound party are unknown.
