J. H. McGRAW, a witness called on behalf of the respondent, after being duly sworn, testified as follows:

Q. [Gilman] Up to last March you were Sheriff of this county for four years?

A. Nearly five years.

Q. FPrior to that what position did you hold.

A. Policeman and chief of Police of this city.

Q. You had quite a general acquaintance with the citizens of the town? A. Yes.

Q. Do you know the petitioner in this case, Alice McKay, how long have you known her? A. I have known her personally and by reputation some eight or nine years?

Q. Do you know what her habits are as to sobriety?

RONALD: At the present time? A. Not at the present time.

Q. Up to what time, were you acquainted with her habits Mr. McGraw? A. Some three years ago.

Q. What were they up to that?

RONALD: We object to any testemony of this witness as to reputation prior to three years ago?

The COURT: Objection overruled, and exception allowed.

A. Her habits were that she would get intoxicated.

Q. She was frequently intoxicated A. Yes sir.

Q. Did you often see her in that condition? A. I have seen her under the influence of liquor frequently.

Q. [Ronald No questions.

GILMAN: We rest.
