Comes now Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, Washington, and as a Return to the Petition in the above entitled matter, alleges:

I.

That he denies each and every allegation of the petition in the above entitled action, save and except what is herein specifically admitted.

II.

That he admits the last part of paragraph one, commencing at the words, "That your petitioner is a native of the province of Bretagne".

III.

That he admits paragraph two, except the words "and thereafter at all times petitioner has been and still is the lawful wife of the said William Grayson", and that as to this he has not knowledge, and information sufficient to form a belief, and therefore denies the same.

IV.

That he admits the allegations set forth in paragraphs three and four.

-1-  

V.

That as to the allegation set forth in paragraph five, he has not knowledge, and information thereof sufficient to form a belief, and therefore denies the same.

VI.

That he admits the allegation set forth in paragraph six, except that the restraint is "under a pretense of law", and alleges that the restrain was lawful.

VII.

That he denies the allegations set forth in paragraphs seven, eight, nine and ten.

AS AN AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE SAID HENRY M. WHITE ALLEGES:

I.

That the petitioner was accorded a proper legal hearing before Immigration Inspector C. A. Turner, and was ordered deported on the warrant of Secretary of Labor, as appears by the warrant hereto annexed and made a part of this return, marked for identification "Ex. A".

II.

That a warrant was issued for the petitioner and she was arrested March 14, 1912, on a charge of having been found practicing prostitution; that testimony was taken in the case on March 27, 1912, and she was released on bond March 27, 1912; that she was married April 1, 1912, to the best knowledge, information and belief of respondent, to prevent being deported; that at the time of her marriage to the said William Grayson she was a prostitute and was practicing prostitution; that she was still out on bond when she was married, and was not released until the 9th
