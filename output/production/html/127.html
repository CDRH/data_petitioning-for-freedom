  • Summary of Proceedings: Keyt was arrested in violation of the Sabbath. His violations included managing and operating a pool room wherein pool and other games were played by customers and by selling and offering for sale at said place cigars and tobacco. Keyt was asked to pay a fine of $1. He then filed a petition of habeas corpus and was denied on grounds that his imprisonment was completely lawful.
