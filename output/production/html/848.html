  • Summary of Proceedings: Louis Seiffer, alias David S. Berman, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus claiming that warden of the State Penitentiary Leidigh was depriving him of his freedom. Seiffer had been arrested on the charge of forgery, fined, and sentenced to five years imprisonment "and kept at hard labor." Seiffer claimed that before his initial trial in Furnas County's District Court, deputy sheriff A. E. Peterson insinuated that Seiffer would be lynched and hung and that the gathered crowd joined in the intimidation. Additionally, he claimed that he was told that if he pled guilty, he would only receive one year in prison. Judge Hall allowed the petition. After hearing evidence, Hall granted a Seiffer a "writ of relief" from the custody of Leidigh and ordered that Seiffer be transferred into the custody of Furnas County sheriff to await additional legal action. According to the August 11, 1895, Chicago Chronicle, Seiffer (spelled Seiffert) was a well-known forger throughout the United States.
