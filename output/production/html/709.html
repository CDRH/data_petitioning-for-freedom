  • Summary of Proceedings: G. W. Gardner, petitoner, along with James Nethesny, E. Neale, & Mayo C. Boyd, was found riding a bicycle without a lighted lamp on June 23rd of 1899. Gardner filed for a writ of Habeas Corpus on the grounds that the findings of the court were not backed by evidence or in line with the law. Furthermore, Gardner argued that the ordinance in question is void, impartial, unjust, and contrary to the United States and Nebraska Constitution. Additionally claiming that the City Council of the City of Omaha had no authority to pass such an ordinance.
