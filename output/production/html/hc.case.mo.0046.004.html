The petition of Nelson Lawson respectfully showeth:

That on the 6th day of April last he was committed to the Jail of St. Louis Co. on A mittimus issued by Peter W. Johnston A Justice of the peace of St. Louis Co. Charging him with being A fugitive from labour or A Run-a-way slave. this upon the affidavit of A certain Matthew Monaghen A police officer of this city:

To all of which your petitioner answers, that he is not A slave, and never has been A slave. that he was born free in the year Eighteen hundred and Thirty Seven in the borough of Carlisle and State of Pennsylvania where his parents then resided and where his Mother, Sisters, and Brothers now reside and where he is well known by nearly all the inhabitants of that town, as will he made appear by the accompanying documents recently obtained by his attorney and which it is believed will remove all doubts relative to his being A for now Slave, and therefore he is illegally restrained of his liberty your sd. petitioner complains that his imprisonment is unjust and oppressive, and that his further detention in jail is unjust illegal and oppressive unnecessary and uncalled for and that he has not made application to a court of higher jurisdiction. We therefore pray your Honor to grant him the peoples writ of Habeas Corpus directed to R.F. Logan jailor of keeper of sd. prison, that his case may be inquired into by your Honor and that he may undergo and receive all such things as the court may consider in the premises. And for which he ever prays

Nelson Lawson
x mark

for and in behalf of the sd Lawson

Personally appeared John J. Schuler and made oath the he believes and has a right to believe the statement of facts set forth in the foregoing petition is true.

John J. Schuler Attorney for Petitioner

Duly sworn before me on this June 23 ' June 1858
