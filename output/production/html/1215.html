  • Summary of Proceedings: Arrested and jailed on the charge of "by means of certain bogus checks and worthless checks... to confidence a certain man," Charles Burtt-alias Charles DeFrance-petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against Chief of Police Hoagland. Judge Cornish granted the petition. In his answer, Hoagland claims that Burtt is "well known to the police officers of the City and other cities of the West, as a professional confidence man." After reviewing the evidence, Judge Cornish allowed the writ and ordered that Burtt be released from custody.
