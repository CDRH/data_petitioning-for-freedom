In the District Court holding terms at Seattle King County ss

In the matter of the application of Qui (a Chinaman) for a writ of habeas corpus

To the Hon. Roger S Greene Judge of said court

The petition of Qui a Chinaman, respectfully shows

1. That he is unlawfully imprisoned, detained, confined and restrained of his liberty by John H McGraw Sheriff of King County, Washington Territory.

2. That said imprisonment, detention confinement and restraint are illegal; and that said illegality consists in this to wit:

That in the 17th day of November 1884 at Port Madison in Kitsap County before Henry C Hale a Justice of the Peace in and for Port Madison Precinct in Kitsap County a sworn complaint was filed made before said Justice, by some   person to your petitioner unknown whereby your petitioner was charged with the alleged offense of smoking and inhaling opium on said County on the _ day of November 1884

That upon said complaint a warrant was issued by said Justice upon which warrant your petitioner was arrested and brought before said Justice; that then after such proceedings were had in said complaint that on the said 17 day of November your petitioner was tried before said Justice for said alleged offense and after such trial your petitioner was found guilty of said alleged offense and was by the Judgment of said Court sentenced to imprisonment, for the period of sixteen days

That thereupon said Justice Court issued its warrant of commitment, a true copy of which is annexed to this petition marked Exhibit "A" and intending to act under such warrant of Commitment, the Sheriff of Kitsap County brought your petitioner to the County Jail of King County and then on the said 17th day of November 1884 delivered him unto the custody of the said John H McGraw Sheriff of King County, who now detain him in   custody and restrains him from his liberty, having an other authority or pretext san[?] and expecting said writ of Commitment

That said trial and commitment were had by virtue of Section 2073 of the Code of Washington; that said Section is unconstitutional and void and that said arrest commitment and trial are without authority of law; that the said complaint prepared against your petitioner charges no offense; and said Justice had no power or jurisdiction to render said judgment of imprisonment

Wherefor your petitioner prays, that a writ of habeas corpus may issue directed to said John H McGraw Sheriff of King County, commanding him to have the body of your petitioner before your Honor at a time and place therein to be specified, to do and receive what shall then and there be ordered concerning him, together with the time and cause of his detention and said writ and that your petitioner may be restored of his liberty

Dated Nov 18, 1884 Qui his signature Steve Harris + M Micken Attys for petitioner
