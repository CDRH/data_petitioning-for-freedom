  • Summary of Proceedings: George Harlow was arrested for vagrancy and sentenced to 90 days in jail, but the term was suspended. Depite that suspension, Harlow was imprisoned anyways under orders from the Police Court. Harlow argued he was wrongfully imprisoned and his writ was granted. In fact, the judge in the cases ordered the county to assume the costs for his release. This case is also connected to George Harris, as Harris seems to have been imprisoned under similar circumstances.
