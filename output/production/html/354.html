  • Summary of Proceedings: Frank Smith petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in an attempt to retrieve Mollie Calhoun, a fourteen-year-old girl, from the custody of W. E. Miller and his wife, which was granted. Frank maintained that Mollie's father, Joseph Calhoun, had left Mollie in his charge when he left Oregon for the Indian Territory, now Oklahoma. Although Frank admitted that he had allowed Mollie to live with the Millers in the hope that she receive an education, he asserted that instead they were "making a slave out of her." During the hearing, Frank withdrew his petition. An entry on page 34 of the Marion County Probate Index suggests that Mollie remained with the Millers.
