  • Summary of Proceedings: Louis M. Cohn petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus arguing that Sheriff McClay was depriving him of his liberty. Weeks previous, Cohn had successfully petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, was remanded to custody, and filed an appeal in the Supreme Court. While that petition was pending, Cohn successfully petitioned for a writ after he had been remanded into custody again. Judge Tibbetts ruled that because Cohn had been committed a second time for the same offense, Cohn was deprived of his liberty. Tibbetts ordered that Cohn be released.
