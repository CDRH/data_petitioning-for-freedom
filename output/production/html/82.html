  • Summary of Proceedings: In June 1918, George Harrison was indicted for assault with a deadly weapon in the Multnomah County Circuit Court and committed to the Oregon State Penitentiary in Salem. The following spring, Harrison's attorney, Thomas Garland, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of his client, which was granted. Garland claimed that the indictment against Harrison had been dismissed, rendering his detention illegitimate. After hearing the evidence, however, the writ was dismissed and Harrison was remanded into the custody of the warden.
