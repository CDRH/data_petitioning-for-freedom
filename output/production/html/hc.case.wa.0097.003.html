July 3rd, 1889.

In the matter of the application of George Kai for a writ of Habeas Corpus. No. 6958.

This matter coming on to be heard before the Judge in Chambers on this 3rd day of July, 1889, the Court having considered the same it is hereby ordered that the application of said petitioner for a discharge be, and the same is hereby denied. And it is further ordered by the Court that said petitioner be, and he is hereby, remanded to the custody of the Sheriff of King County.

The Territory of Washington, -vs- George Kai. No. 6849. Recognizance.

Now on this 3rd day of July 1889, comes the said defendant, George Kai before the Judge in Chambers, and acknowledges himself to owe the Territory of Washington the sum of Three Hundred Dollars and for the payment thereof binds himself, his heirs, executors and administrators; and consents that the same be levied of his lands, goods, chattels and effects. Conditioned that if he the said George Kai now before the Court, who has been held to await the action of the Jrand Jury at the May term, 1889, of this Court for the crime of an assault with a deadly weapon, shall appear to answer said charge before this Court on the 26th day of August, 1889, at ten o'clock A.M., and from day to day, and term to term thereafter until he shall be discharged, and abide by and obey all orders of the Court, and without not depart without leave, this obligation shall be void: Otherwise the same to be of full force and virtue.
