  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 5, 1924, Merle Arlington was discharged from the Douglas County jail after serving a 60-day sentence, and while waiting for transportation to return to her home in Casper, Wyoming, she was compelled to appear as a witness in another case. Upon voluntarily appearing in court as a witness, Arlington was sentenced to serve 60 days in the county jail with no complaint issued against her. After being sentenced, Arlington was then charged with vagrancy and prostitution. On October 15, 1925, Arlington's attorney filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on her behalf. The outcome of the petition and Merle Arlington's fate are unknown.
