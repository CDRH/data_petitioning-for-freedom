  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 18, 1909, Kelso was released from jail on an alleged charge of vagrancy where he had been held for fifteen days. When he was released he was immediately rearrested illegally and unlawfully without warrant. The respondent, Donahue, argued that he was lawfully rearrested because he was a fugitive from justice. His petition was granted but there are no results or outcomes in the case files.
