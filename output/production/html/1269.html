  • Summary of Proceedings: Charlie (or Charles) Mitchell, Edith Brown, and Alice Ennis operated and managed a residence that allegedly sold alcohol. On July 3, 1924, Charlie Mitchell was sentenced to five six-month sentences (30 months) for the unlawful sale of intoxicating liquor, a year sentence for maintaining a common nuisance, and a $500 fine for the unlawful possession of intoxicating liquor. On December 23, 1924, Charlie petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. Judge McGee ordered the respondents to release Charlie from custody upon the payment of a bond and the respondents appealed this decision. On January 30, 1926, the decision of the lower court was reversed and Charlie was remanded to the custody of the respondents.
