  • Summary of Proceedings: James Griffin, William Beck, and William O'Brien filed for a writ of Habeas Corpus which was allowed by Judge Cunningham R. Scott In January of 1895. They were detained for their alleged act of larceny for the total of $2,150.00 of property from Eugene Sauders. After the writ was allowed, the case had a placed a continuance on it, and then was stricken from the docket in March of 1895.
