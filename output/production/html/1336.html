  • Summary of Proceedings: In January 1919, Pete Jolett filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on the ground he was being unlawfully deprived of his liberty by Michael L. Clark, Sheriff of Douglas County. Jelott was charged with having possession of intoxicating liquor, found guilty, and sentenced to pay a fine of $100 and the costs of prosecution. The petition alleged the complaint does not charge Jelott with an offense illegal under the laws of the State of Nebraska. Judge Arthur C. Wakeley allowed the petition but denied the writ. Jelott's attorney filed a motion for a new trial on the grounds the decision of the court was not sustained by sufficient evidence, contrary to law, and the court erred in refusing to discharge Jelott as the complaint did not state an offense cognizable under the laws of the State of Nebraska. It is likely this motion was denied, as the appearance docket states Jelott was later released on recognizance in sum of $200.
