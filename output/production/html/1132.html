  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 14, 1918, J. B. Murray made a complaint in the Omaha Police Court against Frank Diblasco. Diblasco was charged with the possession of intoxicating liquor, his second offense, found guilty, and sentenced to 90 days in jail. On November 1, 1918, Diblasco filed for habeas, arguing that such a charge was not under Nebraska law, that a police judge could not charge second offense, that his first conviction was not complete, and that the judge also had not authority to issue a sentence of 90 days. A writ was issued and later sustained on November 8, 1918, and Diblasco was released from custody.
