  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 16, 1901 Omaha Ordinance H859, that outlawed stores being open on Sundays, was passed. On March 4, 1901 there was a complaint filed against David Brown saying he violated Omaha Ordinance. Warrant issued. David Brown was temporarily imprisoned by B. F. Thomas, jailor. On March 7, 1901 there was a trial in the police court. David Brown was found guilty and fined $10 and costs of prosecution. Petitioner, S. Brown, argued that the ordinance was unconstitutional and illegal and void and David Brown was deprived of his liberty. The writ was allowed and David Brown was discharged.
