In the District Court 3rd Judicial District of Washington Territory holding terms at Port Townsend

In the matter of the application of Ah Laing, Ah Chinq Ah Won, Ah Fan, Ah Fat and Ah Jim for a writ of Habeas Corpus } Order granting wit

Upon reading and filing the petition of Ah Lang, Ah Chinq, Ah Won, Ah Fan, Ah Fat and Ah Jim, duly signed and verified by J.J. Calhoun their attorney on their behalf, whereby it appears that the petitioners are illegally imprisoned and restrained of their liberty by one A L Blake Deputy Collector for Puget Sound District, at the City of Port Townsend, Jefferson County Washington Territory, and stating wherein the illegality consists from which it appears here[?] that a writ of Habeas Corpus ought to issue:

It is ordered, that a writ of Habeas Corpus issue out of and under the seal of the District Court of the Third   Judicial District of Washington Territory, directed to the said A L Blake Deputy Collector aforesaid commanding him to have the bodies of the said petitioners befor me in the court room of the said Court on the 24th day of February AD 1885 at 10 O'Clock AM of that day, to do and receive what shall then and ther be considered concuring the said petitioners, together with the time and cause of this detention, and that he have then and there this said writ.

Dated Feb. 24th AD 1885

Eaton Greeneg[?]
