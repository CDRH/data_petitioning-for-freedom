Capt. Welch, a witness called on behalf of the respondent, after being duly sworn, testified as follows:

Q. [Gilman] You reside in Seattle? A. Yes sir.

Q. What has been your occupation during the past six or eight years? A. Policeman.

Q. Of the City of Seattle? A. Yes sir.

Q. Have you during that period known the petitioner in this case Alice McKay.

A. Yes sir.

Q. What has she been doing during that period. A. Well, I never knew her to do anything here. She has been off and on here between here and Tacoma.

Q. Has she ever lived here during that period A. She lived a short time here.

Q. Where? A. Well, she lived down on the beach at the foot of Pike street. Lived there a while.

Q. Do you know, Captain Welch, what her reputation at the present time is as a law abiding woman and as a woman of sober and temparate habits?

A. Well, I could not really say as to her character, I cannot saw anything against her except what I heard.

Q. What was her reputation? A. It was not good.

Q. Did she have the reputation of being a drunken woman?

Ronald: We object.

The COURT: Objection overruled and exception allowed.

A. Yes sir.

Q. Was she not one of those persons that you police were watching?

RONALD: We object.

The COURT: You may answer. Exception noted.

A. I dont know that we watched her very close, we could have got her if we had wanted her, at any time.

Q. Was her husband McKan with her at that time? A. No sir.

Q. Did you know McKay? A. Ys sir.

Q. What kind of a looking man is he? A. He is rather a good looking man.

Q. Is he a white man? A. Yes sir.
