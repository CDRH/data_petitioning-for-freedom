  • Summary of Proceedings: Gaston Akoun, Madroche Zitoune, Abraham L. Reed, and S. B. Wadley worked for the Oriental Exhibition Company and were in a separate court with the Company. Akoun et al. were unlawfully detained after a violation of a pretended order of injunction in the original case against the Trans-Missisippi and International and Oriental Exhibition Company. They alleged there was no process according and that they were denied the right to be separately tried for the alleged violation of the injunction. After that trial ended, they were arrested because they had not paid their fines. After filing for the writ of habeas corpus, the writ was allowed, the case was heard, and it was determined they were being unlawfully held so they were released from custody.
