  • Summary of Proceedings: John Brown was arrested on August 4th, 1917 by Omaha police for the crime of vagrancy. Shortly after, a complaint was filed against him and he was tried and convicted by the court. His sentence was a fine of $100, however, Brown would be imprisoned for not being able to pay the fine. Because the maximum fine for vagrancy was $50, Brown petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus citing the illegality of his sentence. The judge ruled in his favor, and ordered his immediate release from captivity.
