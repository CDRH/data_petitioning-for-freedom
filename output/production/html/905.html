  • Summary of Proceedings: George W. Lunkin petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against Captain W. H. Matthews, alleging that he was unlawfully restrained of his liberty in the military at or near Lewiston. Lunkin claimed that he had enlisted in Oregon on February 23, 1861, but that he was only seventeen years old and had enlisted without the permission of his parents. He also stated that he had been promised $31 a month but that he had not been paid. Judge E. P. Oliphant allowed the writ, but it is unclear if allowing the writ meant that the writ was actually granted. Lunkin's fate is unknown.
