  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 12, 1862, William Smith was found guilty of grand larceny, specifically, horse theft, by justice of the peace A. Dagget. He was put in jail in Walla Walla awaiting trial. The next day, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against Walla Walla county sheriff James Buckley, claiming that there was no evidence to prove he commited the crime. He subpoenaed multiple witnesses on August 14, 1862. Buckley returned the writ of habeas corpus on August 15, stating that he produced Smith in front of judge E.P. Oliphant. The state also subpoenaed several witnesses on August 16, 1862. The outcome of the case is unknown.
