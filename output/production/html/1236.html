  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 23, 1918, Genevieve Shoemaker filed for habeas against health commissioner, Ernest T. Manning, and chief of police, Michael Dempsey, claiming that she was being held under no complaint and sleeping on the floor in an inhumane manner of detention. The petition was granted, and Shoemaker was temporarily released from the detention home prior to the return of the writ. During the return, Manning explained that Shoemaker was being detained under evidence of a venereal virus during physical exam and had been quarantined in the detention house on 22nd and St. Mary's Avenue according to Ordinance #9852. In 1920, it was dismissed for want of prosecution; fate of Shoemaker is unknown.
