  • Summary of Proceedings: Casper Krallman was arrested and charged with unlawful possession and transport of intoxicating liquor in police court on September 16, 1918, following a complaint made by John M. Burger. Krallman was found guilty and charged $200 and costs of prosecution. On September 24, 1918, Krallman filed for habeas with attorney Daniel Hoorigan, charging that police magistrate has no jurisdiction to try and fine on such charges. Krallman's petition was granted and the writ was allowed on September 30, 1918; Krallman was ordered to be released from custody.
