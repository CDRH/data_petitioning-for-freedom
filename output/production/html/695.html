  • Summary of Proceedings: On 20 January 1914, Marcus Glassman was charged with being an inebriate. On 14 January 1914, petitioner was arrested on a charge of being insane and was placed in the County Jail, he has been detained there since that date. He was not granted a hearing until 28 January 1914. At that hearing, no person testified to any facts that would prove that he was either insane or an inebriate. The petition states that the “insanity board of Douglas County, Nebraska” has not made a finding as to whether he was an inebriate or insane, but has continued to confine the petitioner. Glassman filed a petition for a writ of habeas corpus on 3 February 1914 asserting that he was being unlawfully detained. Judge James P. English ordered that the writ be issued and directed to Sheriff McShane. Sheriff McShane received the writ on 3 February 1914.
