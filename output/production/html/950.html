  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 14, 1918, a complaint was filed against Curley Stenson in the Police Court of Omaha. Stenson was charged with vagrancy and sentenced to 15v days in jail. On June 21, 1918, Stenson received another charge for vagrancy and sentenced to 30 days in jail. On July 1, 1918, Stenson filed for Habeas charging that the second charge was unlawful and void. Stenson's petition was granted, a writ was ordered and sustained. Judge Redick ordered Stenson's release on July 2, 1918.
