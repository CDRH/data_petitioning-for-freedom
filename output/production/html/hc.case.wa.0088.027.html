KATIE McKAY, a witness called on behalf of the Petitioner, after being duly sworn, testified as follows:

Q. [Ronald] Where do you live? A. Tacoma.

Q. You are the daughter of Mrs. McKay A. Yes sir.

Q. Do you know this little child here? A. Yes sir

Q. How long have you lived t at Tacoma? A. About eighteen years

Q. Living with your mother there? A. Yes sir.

Q. How long sinci your father and mother have been living together? A. I guess about four years.

Q. Has yur mother been keeping saloon since your father went away? A. No sir.

Q. What has your mother been doing for a livelihood since your father went away?

A. Washing and ironing, sewing andworking.

Q. Does she work all the time? A. Yes sir.

Q. You have been helping her any? A. Yes sir.

Q. How does your mother treat you and your little sister? A. Kindly.

Q. Do you get plenty to eat? A. Yes sir.

Q. Who buys your clothing? A. Mama.

Q. Do you go to school? A. Yes sir.

GILMAN: I dont know how that cuts any figure here?

The COURT: I will hear all sides of the case.

Q. How does she treat you and your sister? A. Kindly.

Q. Does your mother get drunk? A. No sir.

Q. Have you ever seen her drunk? A. No sir I never saw her drunk.

Q. Does your mother have any man sleeping in the house at all? A. No sir.

Q. Who sleeps with your mother every night? A. Me and my sister.

Q. Every yight? A. Yes sir.

Q. Does your mother ever work out any? A. Yes sir.

Q. She comes home night dont she? A. About five o'clock in the evening.

Q. Who stays in the meantime in the house with your sister? A. Me.
