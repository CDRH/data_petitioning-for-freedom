  • Summary of Proceedings: On April 12th, 1916, Robert Morrison was arrested and charged with vagrancy by the Omaha Police Department. He was originally sentenced to be held in county jail for thirty days, however, after his thirty days expired, the Sheriff refused to release him. On May 15th, Morrison filed a Habeas Corpus petition with the hopes of being released. Judge Willis G. Sears heard his case, and allowed Morrison's writ. Soon following, Morrison was granted freedom from his incarceration.
