  • Summary of Proceedings: In December 1916, Irene Steward filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of Charles Bird and Josephine Bird, also known as Jack Lewis. The petition was filed on the basis of Charles and Josephine being unlawfully deprived of their liberty by being confined in the City Jail of Omaha by Henry W. Dunn, Chief of Police of Omaha, and Michael F. Dempsey, Captain of the Police of Omaha. Judge Willis G. Sears allowed the writ of Habeas Corpus and discharged the bound parties.
