IN THE UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT, WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON, NORTHERN DIVISION.

IN THE MATTER OF THE APPLICATION OF MRS. GLADYS FREDRIKA FUSON, FOR WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS.}

NO. 3451 PETITION.

Comes now Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson, by her next friend- Lona McMasters, and petitions this Honorable Court for a Writ of Habeas Corpus herein, and alleges as follows:-

That heretofore, on Friday, October 20th, 1916, the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson, was a resident of Port Townsend, in the County of Jefferson, in the State of Washington, and in the employ of the affiant herein.

That on said date one M. Ruth Dahnken, claiming to be an Officer of the Seattle Police Department, appeared in Port Townsend and arrested the above named Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson and trasported her to Seattle against her will and without her consent, alleging as cause therefor that one King Dykeman had verbally instructed her so to do.

That the said King Dykeman and Blanche H. Mason and Harold W. Fuson and Doctor Lilburn Merrill have confined the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson in a prison or jail at Broadway and Spruce Streets, in the City of Seattle.

That said arrest, imprisonment and restrained were and are illegal and contrary to the course of common law and of the guarantees of the Constitution of the United States concerning life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness.

That it was not alleged that the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson had committed any offense against the laws at any time or any place.

That no warrant or information was issued

That, as affiant belives, that said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson is not being held by any sentence of any competent court.

 

That she has not been served with any kind of process and that when affiant asked for the reasons she could obtain no satisfaction from the said persons, or any of them.

Affiant further says that the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson is a married woman who has been abandoned by her husband, the above named Harold W. Fuson; and that the said Harold W. Fuson has commenced suit for divorce against the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson, to which she has filed an answer and cross complaint in the Superior Court of King County, Washington.

That affiant belives that said proceeding was begun for the express purpose of preventing said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson from making her defense to said action and for the purpose of holding her in involuntary imprisonment for the advantage of the said Harold W. Fuson in said action; and affiant believes and states the fact to be that the said Dykeman, Fuson, Mason and Merill have illegally conspired to deprive the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson of her rights of liberty guaranteed to all citizens of the United States. Affiant further says that the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson is a native born citizen of the United States and entitled to all of the privileges and immunities of such,

That the affiant and the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson are both poor persons, and ask to be permitted to prosecute this action as such.

STATE OF WASHINGTON COUNTY OF KING, } SS:

LONA Mc MASTERS, being first fully sworn, deposes and says: that she is the person named in the foregoing Petition as the next friend of the said Mrs. Gladys Fredika Fuson; that she has read the forgong Petition for Habeas Corpus, knows the contents thereof and that the same is true; that the reason that affidavit of verification is made by affiant and not by the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson is that the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson is in   duress and unale to sign the same,

Mrs. Lona Mc Master

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 25th day of October 1916.

M E Sheldon
Notarary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle
