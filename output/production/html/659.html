  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 28, 1919, Mack Sterns was found guilty of vagrancy by police magistrate Charles E. Foster. He was fined $100 and confined to the county jail until he could pay the money. However, Paragraph 8865 of Section 290 of the Revised Statues of Nebraska for 1913 stated that one could not order someone charged with vagrancy to pay more than a $50 fine or jail them for more than three months. Therefore, on August 29, Sterns and his attorney, George A. Lee filed a writ of habeus corpus against sheriff Michael L. Clark, which was allowed by judge Lee S. Estelle. On August 30, 1919, judge William A. Redick found in the favor of the petitioners, but Sterns still had to pay a $50 fine and the costs of his stay in jail. On September 4, 1919, the writ of habeus corpus and return was filed.
