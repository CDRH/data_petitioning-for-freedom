  • Summary of Proceedings: In June 1919, a South Dakota tailor named Bertram Mulcahy was fined $10 for violating a Bayard, Nebraska village ordinance against selling samples and taking orders without a license. When he did not pay, he was confined to the Morrill County jail. Mulcahy petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in federal court, arguing that his right to conduct interstate commerce had been violated. His fate is unknown.
