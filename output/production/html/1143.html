  • Summary of Proceedings: In July 1924, Jack Moyer petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus on the grounds he was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty. Prior, Moyer was charged with passing a check with insufficient funds, found guilty, and subsequently sentenced thirty days in County Jail. The petition alleges Moyer was not given a fair trial and the sentence was beyond the jurisdiction of the Court. Judge James M. Fitzgerald authorized the petition, yet the outcome of the writ and the fate of Moyer is unknown.
