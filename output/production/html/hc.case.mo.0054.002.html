In the matter of Geo. W. Turley

"By the Habeas Corpus Act"

In the Law Commissioners Court of St. Louis County St. Louis County Set

George W. Turley humbly complaining would respectfully represent unto your honourable Court that he is now committed, detained, confined and restrained of his liberty in the State of Missouri in the County Jail of the County of St. Louis by James Castello the jailor of said County.

That he was arrested as a fugitive slave, by one Hugh Longe and John Garman without any warrant or authority on the 25th day of May 1853 and taken before Peter W. Johnstone a Justice of the Peace in and for said County and by the said Justice committed to the said County Jail without bail or main- prize[?] as a fugitive slave, and without hearing any testimony on the said 25th day of May 1853 - where he has been confined ever since and is still confined which said warrant of commitment is herewith attached marked A. and made a part of this petition

That his imprisonment is illegal in this to wit

1st That he was arrested without any a warrant or any other legal process whatsoever,

2nd That he was committed to the said jail as a runaway slave, without any proof of the same and upon mere suspicion

3rd There is little or no evidence presented as is required by law

4th The evidence is insufficient to warrant a commitment by the parties

5th That the commitment is unfound, illegal and void

6th That he is a free man and is now prepared to establish his freedom

Wherefore he prays the state writ of Habeas Corpus, directed to the said James Castello Jailor as aforesaid commanding him to bring the body of the said petitioner before this Honourable Court - Then and there to abide its decisions and decrees in the premises and he will ever pray &c

Geo. W Turley being duly sworn on his oath states that he believes the foregoing petition and matters therein as stated and true

George Turley

Sworn to & subscribed before me June 16th 1853

Charles W Hicks

Clerk Court Com Pleas

St Louis Co Mo.
