  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 9, 1918, F. B. Murray made complaint in the Omaha Police Court against Henry Hoffman on the charge of unlawful possession of intoxicating liquor. Hoffman was arrested and tried on court June 10, and fined 100 in costs. On June 13, Hoffman filed for habeas charging that such complaint had not be properly signed, that such complaint did not have sufficient facts to be prosecuted, that liquor in private apartment does not break Nebraska laws, and that he did not have proper access to council before and during trial. The petition was granted, but final outcome and fate of Hoffman unknown.
