IN THE UNITED STATE DISTRICT COURT, WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON, NORTHERN DIVISION.

IN THE MATTER OF THE APPLICATION OF MRS. GLADYS FREDRIKA FUSON, FOR WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS.}

NO. 3451 ORDER GRANTING WRIT

Upon reading and filing the Petition of Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson, made by her next friend, Lona McMasters, duly signed and verified, whereby it appears that the applicant, Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson was illegaly arrested, without a warrant or legal process of any kind, and illegally trasported to Seattle and illegally confined in a prison or place of detention in Seattle, King County, Washington, by King Dykeman, Blanche H. Mason, Harold W. Fuson and Doctor Lilburn Merrill, against her will and without her consent; that she is a native born citizen of the United States and that said imprisonment is in violation of her rights as such, and that Writ of Habeas Corpus ought to issue herein.

IT IS ORDERED, That a Writ of Habeas Corpus issue out of and under seal of this Court, directed to the said King Dykeman, Blanche H. Mason, Harold W. Fuson and Doctor Lilburn Merrill, to have body of the said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson before me in the Court Room of this Court at Seattle, Washington, on the 27th day of October, 1916, at 2:00 o'clock P.M. of that day, to do and receive orderes which shall then and there be considered concerning the said Petitioner, together with the time and cause of her detention

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, That the said King Dykeman, or the Clerk of his said Juvenile Court certify fully to this Court all records, proceedings and process of his said Court, if any there are, authorizing the arrest of said Mrs. Gladys Fredrika Fuson, at the City of Port Townsend, on the 20th day of October, 1916, her transportation to Seattle against her will and without her consent, or produce the originals in Court upon retun hereto; and to show cause, together with the persons named, Why she should not be discharged and show cause why the said Mrs. Gladys Fredricka Fuson should not be discharged   from custody and detention and forthwith returned to the City of Port Townsend, her alleged place of residence.

Done in Open Court, this 26th day of October, 1916

BY THE COURT,
Edward E Cushman
Judge.
