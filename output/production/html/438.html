  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 5th, 1902, James Binyaman filed a complaint that S. D. Becker was peddling without a license, violating Omaha city Ordinance 954. James H. Van Dusen, Becker’s attorney, argued that Becker was employed to C. J. Adams Company, a corporation formed in New Jersey. Van Dusen argued that Becker is a citizen and resident of New Jersey and is here on business, selling rugs and general household furnishings. He was delivering rugs to Mrs. A. J. Kuhn when he was arrested by a policeman because of a complaint against him. The appearance docket said that on May 17th, the petition was dismissed at the cost of the petitioner.
