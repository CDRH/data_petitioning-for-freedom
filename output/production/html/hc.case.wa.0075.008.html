In the District Court of the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding terms at Seattle

In the matter of the application of Lee Kee for a writ of habeas corpus } Petition

To the Honorable Roger S Greene sole presiding Judge of said Court

The petition of Wo Lin respectfully represents

I. That Lee Kee is unlawfully imprisoned confined and restrained of his liberty by James H Woolery chief of police and keeper of the jail of the city of Seattle at the city jail of said City in King County Washington Territory

II. That the cause or pretense of his aforesaid confinement and restraint according to your petitioners best knowledge and belief is by virtue of an alleged committment, a copy of which is hereto annexed marked Exhibit "A" and made part hereof.

III. That your petitioner is advised and verily believes that the imprisonment of the said Lee Kee is illegal and that said illegality consists in

First That the offense charged in the said alleged commitment of itself constitutes no crime for which the said Lee Kee should be restrained of his liberty

Second That the court from which said alleged commitment issued had no jurisdiction of said pretended offense

Third That the Court from which said alleged commitment   issued had no authority to issue the same.

Fourth: That the said pretended commitment is void upon its face

Fifth That the ordinance of the city of Seattle, for a violation of which is specifically named in said commitment as cause therefor is without authority of law and is void.

Sixth And that by reason of for each of said causes the prisoner is deprived of his liberty without due process of law.

Wherefor your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted directed to the said James H Woolery Chief of Police and Jailer of said city of Seattle commanding him to have the body of the said Lee Kee before your Honor at a time and place therein to be specified, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered before your honor together with the time and cause of the said Lee Kee's detention and said writ. and that the said Lee Kee may be restored to his liberty.

Dated at Seattle May 25th 1885

Wo Lin his mark On behalf of Lee Kee

Territory of Washington County of King } ss.

Wo Lin being duly sworn on oath says that he is the subscriber to the above & foregoing petition, that he makes the same on behalf of the said Lee Kee : that he has heard read the alleged said petition, and believes the statements and allegations therin contained to be true

Wo Lin his mark

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 29 day of May 1885 Harold Preston Notary Public
