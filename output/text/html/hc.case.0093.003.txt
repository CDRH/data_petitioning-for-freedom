









In the District Court in and for of the 3rd Judicial District in and for the County
of King Territory of Washington.

In the matter of the application of Frank OBrien for a writ of Habeas Cropus}

The petitioner by his Attorney John E. Costello respectfully shows that Frank OBrien
is unlawfully imprisoned confined and restrained of his liberty by William Cochran
sheriff of said King County in said Territory.

That said imprisonment confinement and restraint are illegal and that the illegality
thereof consists in this to wit:-

That said OBrien was committed by byby Justice of the Peace H. F. Jones on or about
the 1st day of January 1889 to await the action of the Grand Jury next to commence
on the 1st Monday of February 1889 that said grand Jury has so commenced but that
the said OBrien has not been indicted that the papers and drawings in said case have
not



been transmitted by said Justice to said District Court, that nothing in the records
of said Court appears whereby said Court has or can have jurisdiction of said or over
the body of the said OBrien

Wherefore your petitioner prays that a Writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted directed
to the said Cochrane commanding him to have the body of Frank OBrien before you Honor
at a time and place therein to be specified to do and receive what shall then and
there by considered by your Honor concerning him together with the time and  cause
of his detention and said writ and that he may be restored to his liberty

Dated on the 28th day of February 1889

Frank OBrien
by his Atty
John E. Costello

And now comes the said John E Costello on oath and says that the above statements
subscribed to by him are trueof his best knowledge and



belief. The said Costello further says that he makes said petition for the reason
that said defendant is confined in the County Jail in said King County and therefore
unable to make personal 

Subscribed &amp; sworn to before me this 28th day of Feby 1889

WE Ledgerwood Clerk
