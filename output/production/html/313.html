  • Summary of Proceedings: Thomas Cornwell filed an application for a writ of habeas corpus on June 3, 1924. Naming Peter H. Dillon (Chief of Omaha Police) and Charles H. Van Deusen (Omaha Chief of Detectives) as respondents, Cornwell stated that he had been detained in the Omaha City Jail without any information or "other charges" being filed against him since May 20, 1924. Judge James M. Fitzgerald granted the writ and commanded that Vandeusen and Dillon appear in court and show cause for Cornwell's detention. The Appearance Docket states that Cornwell was discharged on June 3, 1924, which suggests that Cornwell's writ may have been allowed.
