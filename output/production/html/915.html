  • Summary of Proceedings: On January 25, 1919, Stella Williams was detained by police officers of the city of Omaha, and on January 27th, she was charged with being an inmate of an ill-governed house. Williams was taken to trial on that day, found guilty, and sentenced to serve 20 days in the Douglas County jail. She immediately provided the appeal bond necessary to file an appeal, which was approved by the Police Magistrate, who then notified the keeper of the Omaha city jail to release Williams. The keeper of the jail refused to release Stella Williams due to an order issued by Omaha Health Commissioner E. T. Manning, who ordered Williams to be quarantined and isolated in the jail due to her infection with a communicable venereal virus. On October 31, 1919, the application was dismissed at the cost of the county. Williams' fate is unknown.
