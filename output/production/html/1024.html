  • Summary of Proceedings: In April 1901, the Canton Bridge Company, an Ohio corporation, sued Thomas Ratcliff and several co-defendants for money owed in the U.S. Circuit Court for the District of Nebraska. Ratcliff was subpoenaed to give at a deposition, but refused to cooperate with the notary public, Thomas Maddison, who had Ratcliff jailed for contempt. Ratcliff petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that while Nebraska notaries could exercise this authority, Maddison had been acting under the color of federal law and had no such authority. After hearing the evidence, the judge agreed and Ratcliff was released from custody.
