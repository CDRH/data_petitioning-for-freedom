  • Summary of Proceedings: On November 6, 1880, Elmery Seelling complained against Henry A. Carleton to Gage County justice of the peace Peter Shafer, accusing him of bastardy. He was found guilty, with bail set at $500. Carleton could not pay this, and so he was committed to the county jail. Therefore, with the help of the law firm Bush and Richards, Carleton filed a writ of habeas corpus on December 30, 1880 against Gage County sheriff Nathaniel Herron, arguing that he was being unlawfully detained. However, the writ was not issued and served until January 10, 1881. At the hearing on January 11, court officials argued about whether the testimony from the previous hearing in front of the justice of the peace should be allowed, with it ultimately being struck from the record. Judge J.A. Coffey ultimately ruled against Carleton, committing him to jail and ordering him to pay court costs.
