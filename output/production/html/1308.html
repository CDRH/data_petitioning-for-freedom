  • Summary of Proceedings: Shortly after her husband's death, Lizzie Branson of Lincoln, Nebraska, was approached by J.A. Murray, an agent of the Nebraska Children's Home Finding Society, who, the petitioner alleges, "unlawfully secured the possession and custody" of her three children, George Craig, Olie Williamson, and Mabel Williamson by "force, intimidation, and threats" during her time of grief. After Branson signed a waiver, the children were taken from Lancaster County to the Nebraska Children's Home in Omaha, Nebraska. On February 7, 1916, the children's brother-in-law, G.W. Beard, filed a writ of habeus corpus for the children served to the superintendent of the Nebraska Children's Home, Reverend E.P. Quivey, and J.A. Murray. F.J. McShane, the Douglas County sheriff, served the writ to Quivey, but could not find Murray. A return to writ was filed on February 14, but nothing more is known about the fate of the children.
