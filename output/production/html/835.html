  • Summary of Proceedings: Sherman Hall, age 6, was being held at St. James Orphanage after his father, Louis Hall, placed him in their care in October, 1913. Petitioner, Minnie Hall Riley, is Sherman’s mother. Petitioner stated that Louis was a man of immoral character, a habitual drunkard, and unfit to care for a child. At the time the petition was filed, Louis had not contacted petitioner or the child for a month. Minnie attempted to retrieve Sherman from the orphanage but they refused her request. She submitted this petition for a writ of habeas corpus on 23 November 1914, asking that Sherman Hall be discharged from the unlawful imprisonment. Judge James P. English ordered that the writ be allowed, returnable on 24 November 1914. Judge English ordered that custody of Sherman Hall be granted to Minnie Hall Riley.
