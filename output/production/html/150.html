  • Summary of Proceedings: Samuel Slaughter was taken into custody in St. Louis on 4 November 1854 and was accused of being a runaway slave and the city jailer is ordered in the Commitment document to hold him until his owner (a James King it appears) returns to collect him. It is claimed in the petition that he was, in fact, a freeman and was being imprisoned without just cause. The petition was granted on the 23rd of November. No more information on whether the case proceeded further.
