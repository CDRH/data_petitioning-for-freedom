  • Summary of Proceedings: James Hallahan was brought into custody on 21 September 1913 on the complaint and information of E. R. Leigh who charged him with the crime of disturbing the peace. He was confined until 23 September 1913, at which point he was brought before Police Judge James Callahan and sentenced to 30 days in County Jail. This petition for a Writ of Habeas Corpus argues that the imprisonment is wrongful because the Court doesn’t have jurisdiction as Hallahan is a 17 year old minor. As such, the case should be in the Juvenile Court of Douglas County. Judge Lee Estelle allowed the writ on 24 September 1913.
