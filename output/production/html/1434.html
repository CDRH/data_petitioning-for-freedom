  • Summary of Proceedings: J. J. Blevins petitioned for writs of habeas corpus in the county and circuit courts to retrieve his three young sons, Henry, John, and Thomas, from his father-in-law, Thomas Small, both of which were granted. Smalls claimed that his grandchildren refused to return to their father, saying that they "cried Piteously" at the thought of leaving. Smalls added that Blevins was an unfit parent, and that for the past three years Smalls and his wife, Elizabeth, had been supporting Blevins and the children. After hearing the evidence, the district court judge remanded the three boys to the custody of their father. Blevins and Smalls spent the next two years litigating a property dispute in civil court.
