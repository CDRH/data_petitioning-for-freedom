  • Summary of Proceedings: On September 11, 1918, J. B. Murray made complaint against Ike Payne. On September 12, Payne was charged and tried in the Omaha Police Court for the unlawful possession and transport of intoxicating liquor. Found guilty, on note of second offense, Payne was fined 300 and to be placed in jail until fine be paid. On September 27, Payne filed for habeas charged that such a fine exceeds the jurisdiction of a police magistrate. Payne's petition was granted, but case was dismissed for want of prosecution on January 30, 1920. Note: Payne filed new Habeas petition a week later in 1918.
