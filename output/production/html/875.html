  • Summary of Proceedings: On June 14, 1864, C.M. Bradshaw petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of Nicholas Adams and Thomas Gould. Bradshaw stated that Nicholas and Thomas were being held captive by an armed group of over thirty people that referred to themselves as the “Vigilance Committee”. The group allegedly threatened to expel Nicholas and Thomas from the territory by force and execute them by mob law before the writ could be issued. Bradshaw also asked for a warrant to be issued for the group’s apprehension. The judge accepted the application for the writ and issued the warrant. Adams’ and Gould’s fates are unknown.
