  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 1, 1922, Cloid J. Wilson, with attorneys, John N. Baldwin and James H. Walker, filed for habeas against sheriff, Michael L. Clark. In the petition, Wilson argued that he is being held on a complaint made in Idaho under an offense which he was not in Idaho during, was arrested without warrant, and has been a resident of Nebraska for more than two years. A writ was issued, but the case was dismissed for want of prosecution on January 21, 1924; the fate of Wilson is unknown.
