To the Honorable Judge Dusenbury

Judge of the Law Commissioners Court of S. Louis County:

The Petition of Elizabeth Vincent, alias Elizabeth Allen, respectfully represents — that she is now held in active confinement & restraint of her liberty by Mr. Boardman, Jailer of S. Louis County, On a charge of being a fugitive from service or runaway slave: that she was born free, and has been free all her life; that she was arrested in the State of Illinois & brought over to this City and confined in Jail ever since: that she has been in said Jail about two weeks: that she has not applied for releif, nor been refused, by any court, officer superior to the one to whom this petition is presented; that she has applied for a copy of the papers accompanying or concerning her said confinement and the same has been refused. she therefore prays that your Honor will part[?] to her a writ of Habeas Corpus, to be denoted[?] to the said Roderman commanding him to bring the body of the said Elizabeth Vincent, alias Allen, before you & require him to certify the cause of her detention:

Elizabeth Vincent, alias Elizabeth Allen, by Carroll her attorney

Personally appeared Joseph Brooks who being duly sworn in his oath says that he believes the facts stated in the above petition to be true:

x Joseph Brooks

Subscribed & Sworn before me on this 13th day of May 1859

H Dusenbury

Law Commr
