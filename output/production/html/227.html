  • Summary of Proceedings: In May 1923, Robert Ross petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus on the grounds he was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty by Michael L. Endres and Joseph Sherry, Sheriff and Deputy Sheriff of Douglas County, Omaha, respectively. Ross was charged with disorderly conduct and sentenced to serve ninety days in county jail. The petition asserted the ordinances of the city of Omaha only authorizes the municipal court or police court, to sentence a person to thirty days for such an offense. Judge Charles A. Goss allowed the writ and ordered the petitioner be released from custody.
