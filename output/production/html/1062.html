  • Summary of Proceedings: In January 1896, Spafford Woodhull was arrested and charged in federal court with having murdered Amos La Prentis on the Omaha Indian Reservation the previous September. Woodhull, who was "born a member of the Omaha tribe of Indians," petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, arguing that he was a US citizen living apart from the Omaha, that the alleged murder occurred in Thurston County, Nebraska, and that the federal government therefore lacked the jurisdiction to charge him. His fate is unknown.
