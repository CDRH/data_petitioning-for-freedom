In the District Court of Olympia

In re Habeas Corpus, Gus Bendjus Et Al

To Hon. Frank Allyn Judge,

The petitions herein make answer to the return of Wm Billings, Sheriff of Thurston County to the Writ of Habeas Corpus hereafter issued as follows:

1. They deny the authority of W.H. Bush to have petitioners confined in the jail of Thurston County or placed in the hands of Wm Billings aforesaid for safe keeping or at all.

2. They deny of the sufficientcy of the warrants mentioned in the petitions and deny that they were duly issued.

3. They deny the validity of the indictments attached to the return by certified copy.

And for a further answer they alleged:

1. That the indictments are insufficient and void.

  Page 2

2. That in the causes of #135 and 136 no offense or crime is alleged known to our statutes.

3. That the each fail to state any offense against petetioners.

4. That each of said indicments were found by a body of men assuming the findings of the Grand Jury.

5. That this Grand Jury in acting was not selected, drawn, summoned, empannelled or sworn as prescribed by law.

6. That none of said indictments are signed by the foreman of the Grand Jury.

7. That the names of all the witnesses examined before this, alleged Grand Jury are not enclosed in the indictments or any of them.

8. That names of the indictments were prosecuted or marked filed as prescribed by the Code.

9. That certain persons other than the Grand Jurors were present before the Grand Juy denied the investigation of the charge or charges   upon which these indictments are based.

10. That each of the indictments herein being causes #135, 136 and 137, does not conform to the indictments of the code.

11. That all and each of the Petitions have been indicted, arrested, and are deprived of liberty without due process of law.

Bignold & Robinson
Atty for Petitioners
