  • Summary of Proceedings: On December 9, 1876, nine mariners were arrested and charged with desertion after refusing to take the American Ship "War Hawk" to San Francisco. The petitioners claimed they were wrongfully detained for this crime by a deputy US marshal. Instead of proceeding to the ordered location, the mariners landed at the port of discovery in Washington and were arrested there. The nine men petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, claiming neglect was the cause for not proceeding to their ordered location. Their attorneys argued that while the "War Hawk" was able to provide the plaintiffs with suitable food, it neglected to do so and was an "unseaworthy" ship. Marshall Wyckoff responded that the said marines absented themselves without permission from the master or another officer of the said "War Hawk" and before the end of said voyage contract. John R. Lewis, a King County judge, ruled that the said plaintiffs were "illegally restrained and imprisoned of their liberty" and granted their petition.
