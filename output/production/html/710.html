  • Summary of Proceedings: Sixteen-year-old Virgie La Grand was arrested in Portland, Oregon on November 6, 1914, accused of being a "common prostitute" and charged with vagrancy, and sent to the State Industrial School for Girls in Salem. The following April, her husband, James La Grand, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. James argued that the Portland municipal court that had sentenced his wife lacked the jurisdiction to do so, but was unsuccessful. After hearing the evidence, Virgie was remanded back into the custody of the school superintendent.
