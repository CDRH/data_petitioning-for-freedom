  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 10, 1884, Sid Howard was charged with smoking and inhaling opium and fined $25. When he failed to pay his fine, a justice of the peace had him committed to jail. Howard petitioned for habeas corpus, which was granted, arguing that there was no proper authority to pass judgement and that his arrest was unlawful. After hearing the evidence, Howard was remanded into the custody of his jailer.
