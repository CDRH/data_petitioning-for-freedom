  • Summary of Proceedings: On 21 April 1913, Ethel Allen and Nellie Roberts were arrested and and charged with prostitution. They were convicted of said charge and committed by the Judge of the Police Court of the City of Omaha to the County Jail. The complaint, submitted by A. M. Wirt, stated that the two women engaged in prostitution on 21 April 1913 “contrary to the ordinances of the City of Omaha, aforesaid, and statutes of said State in that behalf provided and against the peace and dignity of the people of the State of Nebraska.” This complaint was submitted to Charles E. Foster, Police Magistrate for the City of Omaha. On 22 April 1913, Roberts and Allen appeared in court and pled guilty. Costs were assessed at $95.00 each. The State had two witnesses, one whose name is illegible and the other by the last name Holden. On 30 April 1913, Allen and Roberts along with their attorney, F. W. Driscoll, submitted a petition for a writ of habeas corpus which stated that the charge was not a state offense and “not contrary to the form of the statutes of the State of Nebraska.” The Appearance Docket noted that their application was denied on 1 May 1913.
