UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT, WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON, NORTHERN DIVISION.

In the Matter of the Application of Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, for a Writ of Habeas Corpus. ) No. 2780. ASSIGNMENT OF ERRORS.

Iron

Comes now Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, Washington, for the United States Government, the respondent and appelant in the above entitled cause, by Clay Allen, United States Attorney for the Western District of Washington, and George P. Fishburne, Assistant United States Attorney for said District, and says that in the record and proceedings in this cause and in the order and judgment of discharge, dated the 20th day of July, 1914, and filed and entered in the Clerk's office July 20, 1914, and in the decision of the Court made and filed the 17th day of July, 1914, there is manifest error in this, to-wit:

I.

That the Court erred in issuing the writ of habeas corpus.

II.

That the Court erred in assuming jurisdiction of the case.

III.

That the Court erred in holding that he had jurisdiction and power to discharge the petitioner Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, when the records showed that she had previously had a due and legal hearing before C. A. -1-   Turner, an immigration inspector, and that on testimony and proof taken before said investigator, W. D. Wilson, Secretary of Labor, had ordered the petitioner to be deported on the ground that she was a prostitute and had been found practicing prostitution subsequent to her entry into the United States.

IV.

That the Court erred in finding that the petitioner was legally married.

V.

That the Court erred in finding that by marriage the petitioner was made a citizen of the United States, when it appeared by the record that she could not be lawfully naturalized.

VI.

That the Court erred in finding that petitioner was not subject to deportation after the Secretary of Labor had found that she should be deported on the ground that the petitioner was a prostitute and had been found practicing prostitution subsequent to her entry into the United States.

VII.

That the Court erred in finding marriage conferred citizenship upon the petitioner, and prevented her deportation, when the records showed that she was not a person of good moral character, nor in any other way qualified to be naturalized according to the laws of the United States.

VIII.

That the Court erred in not finding that the question of deportation of the petitioner was exclusively within the province of the Secretary of Labor and the immigration -2-   authorities.

IX.

That the Court erred in setting aside a ruling of the immigration authorities that the marriage of the petitioner was void so far as the Immigration Department was concerned.

X.

That the Court erred in finding that he had a right to set aside a ruling of the Immigration Department as to the effect of marriage as shown in the case at bar on the citizenship of the petitioner, and in not holding that it was a question exclusively within the province of the Immigration Department.

XI.

That the Court erred in setting aside the ruling of the Secretary of Labor that the said alien should be deported on the ground that she was a prostitute and had been found practicing prostitution subsequent to her entry into the United States, because the conclusion and order of the said Secretary of Labor as to whether a woman were married or not as marriage affects the right of the Immigration Department to deport an immigrant.

XII.

That the Court erred in holding that the marriage of the petitioner could interfere with the immigration authorities to deport her on the ground that neither the common law as to the effect of marriage nor the naturalization statutes were ever intended or allowed to interfere -3-   with the immigration laws of the United States.

WHEREFORE, Henry M. White, Commissioner of Immigration at Seattle, Washington, respondent and appellant herein, prays that his assignment of errors be entered upon the record in this cause, and that upon the hearing of this appeal it may be adjudged by the United States Circuit Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit that the order and judgment, dated July 20, 1914, and entered July 20, 1914, and the decision, made and filed July 17, 1914, be in all things reversed, set aside and held for naught, and that it be adjudged and decreed that the applicant, Marie Robina Grayson, alias Marie Robina, be remanded to the custody of the respondent herein to be further dealt with according to law.

Dated at Seattle, Washington, August 20th, 1914.

By G. P. Fishburne Clay Allen
