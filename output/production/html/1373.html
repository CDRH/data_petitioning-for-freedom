  • Summary of Proceedings: L. J. Urann petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against King County Sheriff L. C. Smith and Massachusetts agent Morris Wolf on July 1, 1905. He claimed that he was imprisoned without due process or warrant as a fugitive from justice because he was falsely accused of larceny under Massachusetts law. He claimed that a witness had falsely accused him in order to extort money from him to pay a civil debt. Smith responded that the detention of Urann was legal because Washington State governor Albert Mead had ordered the detention so that Urann could be returned to Massachusetts. Judge Cornelius H. Hanford ordered that Smith and Wolf show cause for Urann's detention. Hanford denied Urann's petition and remanded him to Smith's custody. Urann petitioned for appeal, and his ultimate fate is unknown.
