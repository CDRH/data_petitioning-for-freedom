  • Summary of Proceedings: In June 1907, Hyman Stein, a salesman for the Rockford, Illinois-based firm, Chandler Brothers, secured an order for some "lace curtains and rugs" in Schuyler, Nebraska. He was charged in the local police court with peddling without a license, a municipal violation, and fined $25. When he refused to pay, he was charged with contempt of court and placed in the Colfax County jail. Stein petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, he was released from custody.
