In the District Court of the First Judicial District W. T. on and for Walla Walla County

In the matter of application of John Doe for a writ of Habeas Corpus

To the Hon. James K. Kennedy Judge of said court

The petition of John Doe, an Indian, respectfully shows:

1st. That he The the said John Doe is unlawfully restrained of his liberty – is unlawfully confined imprisoned and detained by one James McAuliff sheriff of Walla Walla County, W. T., and is now imprisoned and held by said McAuliff in the County jail of Walla Walla County, W. T.

2nd. That the said imprisonment, detainment, confinement and restraint is illegal.

 

3rd. That this affiant does not know of the cause of said restraint or illegality or pretense for the same.

4th. That this petitioner has not committed any crimes or violation of the statutes of Wash. Territory, nor has he been arrested or committed by any committing magistrate for the same nor is he now held by the order of any Court or committing magistrate of Washington Territory, wherefore your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted, directed to said James McAuliff, sheriff of aforesaid commanding him to have the body of your petitioner before your honor at a time and place therein to be specified   to do and receive what shall then and there be considered by Your honor concerning your petitioner together with the time and cause of his detention and said writ; and that your petitioner may be returned his liberty.

Dated Oct. 13/69

L. A. Mullan & E. C. Ross attys for Petitioner

Territory of Washington County of Walla Walla } SS

L. A. Mullan being first duly sworn says that he is the atty of the petitioner named in the foregoing petition, and that he knows the content of the foregoing petition and that the same is true as verily believes

L. A Mullan  

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 13th day of Oct. 1869

Chas W Frush
Justice of Peace

Territory of Washington County of Walla Walla } SS

To James McAuliff, Sheriff of Walla Walla County, you are hereby commanded to have the body of the within named, John Doe, before me at the Court house in Walla Walla, at 3 o clock of the afternoon of this, the 13th day of October, 1869, together with the cause of his detention, if any you have.

Witness my hand, the day and year above written.

James K. Kennedy Judge SS.  

In the matter of application of for a writ of Habeas Corpus

In Dis. Court 1 st Jud. Dist. W. T.

Filed Oct. 13th, 69

R. P. Reynolds Petitioner attys.
