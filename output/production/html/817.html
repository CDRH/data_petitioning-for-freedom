  • Summary of Proceedings: Jesus Garcia was accused of carrying a concealed weapon on March 17, 1917, found guilty by police magistrate James M. Fitzgerald, and sentenced to pay a fine of $25 and court costs on March 19. He was to be held in custody until he could produce the money. He filed for a writ of habeus corpus on March 20, 1917, to be served against the sheriff, Michael L. Clark. The sheriff's return to writ was filed on March 26, 1917, but no further information about the outcome of the petition or Garcia's fate is in the case file.
