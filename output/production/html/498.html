  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 29, 1916, Richard Casey committed the alleged crime. On October 31, 1916, the Police Court of the City of Omaha, Douglas County, charged him with “carrying concealed weapons” in violation of statutes of the state of Nebraska. Casey was tried, convicted, and sentenced to pay a fine of twenty-five dollars. However, on the same day, Casey petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on the grounds that he was unlawfully deprived of his liberty. He claimed that the police had no jurisdiction to try and sentence him under a charge of “carrying concealed weapons.” Thus, all the proceedings were unlawful and void, and the police unlawfully deprived him of his liberty. The outcome of this proceeding is unknown.
