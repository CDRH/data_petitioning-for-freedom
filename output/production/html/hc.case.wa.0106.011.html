1708

In the District Court of the 2nd Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding terms at Olympia for the Counties of Mason, Lewis, Chehalis and Thurston

In the matter of the application of Sarah E. Seibert for a writ of Habeas Corpus } Petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus

To the Honorable Roger S. Greene Judge of the above named Court.

The petition of Sarah Elizabeth Seibert, respectfully shows:

That she is unlawfully imprisoned, detained, confined and restrained of her liberty by William Billings Esqr Sheriff of Thurston County Washington Territory, in the County Jail, at Olympia, in Thurston County, Washington Territory.

That the cause or pretense of her restraint, according to the   best of her knowledge, is a commitment issued out of the District Court of the 2nd Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding terms at Olympia upon a pretended, or assumed conviction judgment and sentence, for the crime of polygamy, under Section 128, of the Criminal practice act of Washington Territory approved Nov. 10th 1873.

That the said confinement, detention, and imprisonment is illegal, and that the illegality thereof consists in this:

First

That the Grand Jury which found the Indictment, upon which the petitioner was tried, convicted and sentenced, had no legal authority to inquire into the offense charged in said indictment because said offense is within the exclusive Jurisdiction of the District Court of the United States and because the said Grand Jury had no Jurisdiction to enquire of offenses committed against the laws of the United States.

 

Second

Because said Section 128 of the Criminal Practice Act of the Territory of Washington upon which the indictment, conviction, judgment and commitment are based, is unconstitutional and void, because it is inconsistent with Section 5352 of the Revised Statutes of the United States.

Third

Because the District Court of the 2nd Judicial District of Washington Territory holding terms at Olympia, being the Court to which the indictment against petitioner was returned, and in which she was tried, convicted and sentenced and out of which the commitment issued had no Jurisdiction over the offense charged in said Indictment by reason of the provisions of Section 711 of the Revised Statutes of the United States.

Fourth

Because the Grand Jury, which found the indictment, upon which petitioner was convicted, sentenced, and committed. Could only   inquire off and presentment make of offenses committed against the laws of the Territory of Washington and that the said Indictment so found, does not state facts sufficient to constitute any offense against the laws of said Territory.

Wherefore, your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted, directed to the said William Billings, Sheriff as aforesaid commanding him to have the body of your petitioner, before your Honor, at a time and place therein to be specified, to do and receive what shall then and there be considered by your honor, concerning your petitioner together with the time and cause of her detention, and said writ, and that your petitioner may be restored to her liberty.

John P. Judson Attorney for Petitioner
