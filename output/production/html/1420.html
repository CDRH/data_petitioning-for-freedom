  • Summary of Proceedings: On February 2nd, 1887, Nancy O'Brien took her son, Harry O'Brien, away from their home in Fremont, Nebraska, without the knowledge of Samuel O'Brien, Nancy's husband and Harry's father. Nancy and Harry O'Brien travel to the Dakota territory and then to the territory of Washington. On July 9th, 1887, Samuel O'Brien filed a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of his son in the territory of Washington. On July 12th, 1887, Sheriff James Glispin brought Harry O'Brien to court on orders from Judge George Turner. Judge Turner then ruled in favor of Samuel O'Brien and handed custody of Harry O'Brien over to him.
