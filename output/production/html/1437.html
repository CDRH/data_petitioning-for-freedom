  • Summary of Proceedings: Dick Pfeffer caught Hebert N. Humphrey violating Nebraska Game law by having a Black Bass less than 8 inches in his possession. This violation resulted in a complaint against Humphrey, where a justice of the peace sentenced him to 5 days in county jail. A relative of his, Roy M. Humphrey, filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus on H. Humphrey's behalf. On August 29th, 1924, the day after his arrest, H. Humphrey posted bail and went to court to plea for a writ of Habeas Corpus. Though the judge agreed to hear his petition, the case's outcome is unclear.
