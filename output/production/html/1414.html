  • Summary of Proceedings: On January 6, 1922, Joseph Buckley filed for habeas against Chief of Police, Michael Dempsey, and Chief of Detectives, Charles H. Van Dusen. In the petition, Buckley argued that he was, since January 3, being held without any charges, complaint or commitment. On the return of the issued writ, the respondents explained that a warrant was issued by Police Magistrate W. F. Wappich on January 6, 1922, for the charge of fugitive from justice. The case was later dismissed for want of prosecution on January 21, 1924; the fate of Buckley is unknown.
