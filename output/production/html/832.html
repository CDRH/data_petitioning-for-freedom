  • Summary of Proceedings: On August 24, 1920, Earl Thomas Beni petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. In his petition, he claimed that on August 12th, the police arrested him and charged him with impersonating an officer and disorderly conduct. The next day, the police magistrate, Charles E. Foster, tried his case and sentenced him to 30 days in the County jail. However, Beni argued Foster lacked jurisdiction, and the court’s judgment was void because his charge does not exist in the state of Nebraska statutes. He does not specify which charge he referred to. The same day, the judge accepted and ordered a writ of habeas corpus. However, the outcome of this proceeding is unknown.
