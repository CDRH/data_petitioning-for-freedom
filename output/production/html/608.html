  • Summary of Proceedings: A. F. Ryan was arrested on June 2nd, 1919, by two detectives who took him to the Omaha Police Station for "investigation." Ryan's attorney showed, however, that no complaint was ever filed against him. In light of this argument, an application for a writ of Habeas Corpus was filed by Ryan and his attorney and granted by Judge William A. Redick. The judge ordered the bound and holding parties to reappear in court; however, this case does not indicate that Ryan was set free.
