  • Summary of Proceedings: Napoleon Johnson was arrested and jailed. He petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted, asserting that he did not know any reason why he should be detained. His jailer responded that Johnson was being detained because a ship captain had accused him of "stealing a watch & pistol," but that the only witness, an unidentified Black man, could not testify because he was away on a boat. After hearing the evidence, Johnson was released.
