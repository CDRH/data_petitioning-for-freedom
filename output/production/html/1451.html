  • Summary of Proceedings: On May 9, 1924, Herbert G. Flood applied for a writ of habeas corpus against Peter Dillon (Omaha Chief of Police) and Charles H. Van Deusen (Omaha Chief of Detectives). Flood alleged that he had been arrested by Omaha police on May 6 and charged with being a fugitive from justice. He asserted that no complaint had been filed against him, which made his detention illegal. Judge James M. Fitzgerald commanded that Van Deusen and Dillon come to the Court and show cause for Flood's detention. Van Deusen and Dillon returned the writ with a document from John W. Yeager (the Chief Deputy County Attorney) stating that they were holding Flood on behalf of B. S. Grannis, the Commonwealth Attorney for the 19th Judicial District of the State of Kentucky. Grannis had telegraphed them with a bench warrant from Bracken County, Kentucky, alleging that Flood committed the crime of bigamy. After a hearing, Flood's application was granted, and the court docket states that he was discharged.
