  • Summary of Proceedings: Hugh G. Durham petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of his daughter, Sylvia May Durham, claiming that Frank and Corey Drury illegally deprived Sylvia of her freedom. When Silvia was a baby, her mother died, and Hugh delivered her into the custody of Cory Drewery, Hugh's sister. He filed the writ of habeus corpus because he had remarried and wished to start a family. The holding party produced the body of Sylvia, but there is no record of testimony. Months later, the petitioner moved to dismiss the case, which the unnamed judge granted. This was apparently so that he could start a new action against Sylvia's foster parents. The fate of the bound party is unknown.
