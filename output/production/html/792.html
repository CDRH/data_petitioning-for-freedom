  • Summary of Proceedings: In May 1924, a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus was issued for James Farrar on the basis he was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty by Michael L. Endres, Sheriff of Douglas County. Farrar was sentenced to ninety days in jail under a complaint of vagrancy, and the petition alleged the mittimus was void and of no force as the authority, J.F. Powers, was not a state officer empowered to prosecute on behalf of the state of Nebraska, and that complaint failed to state a cause of action. The appearance docket stated the writ was denied and Farrar was remanded to custody.
