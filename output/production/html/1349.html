  • Summary of Proceedings: In the summer of 1884, Herman Hadlan was arrested and charged with violating a pair of Olympia, Washington city ordinances prohibiting the sale of liquor without a license. He was found guilty, ordered to pay $5 and court costs, and sent to jail until he could pay. Hadlan petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, claiming that he had received permission from the city to keep his beer hall and, in any case, that the relevant city ordinances were void. His fate is unclear.
