  • Summary of Proceedings: Stanely Fox was charged with statutory rape and default of bail on September 15th, 1919. He filed a petition for habeas corpus on September 18, 1919, in which he argued that there was insufficient evidence to hold him. The writ was denied on September 26th, 1919. Stanely Fox motioned for a new trial the next day again arguing that there was insufficient evidence to deny his writ. The motion for a new trial was denied, so he appealed to the Supreme Court of Nebraska. The Supreme Court of Nebraska heard his case in the January term of 1920. The court agreed with the original decision and decided in favor of the defendant. Stanely Fox remained in custody.
