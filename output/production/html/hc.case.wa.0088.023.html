MARY KEEVIN. called on behalf of the respondent. after being duly sworn. testified as follows:

Q. [Gilman] Mary. where do you live? A. Belltown.

Q. With whom do you live? A. With Mrs. Keevin.

Q. This lady right here? A. Yes sir.

Q. How long have you lived there. ever since you can remember? A. Yes sir.

Q. Do you recollect of ever living with any one else? than Mrs. Keevin? A. No sir.

Q. How does Mrs. Keevin treaat you?

Q. RONALD: We object. it is incompetent in this case.

The COURT: She can state. Exception allowed.

A. Good.

Q. Treats you kindly? A. Yes sir.

Q. Furnishes you with plenty to eat and ware? A. Yes sir.

Q. Do you go to school? A. Yes sir.

Q. Where do you go to school? A. Belltown.

Q. Would you like to be taken away from Mrs. Keevin? A. No sir.

RONALD: We object.

The COURT: I would like to know what she thinks about it. herself.

RONALD: We except

The COURT: Exception allowed.

Q. Would you like to be taken away from Mrs. Keevin and go with this woman here?

A. I would not go with her.

Q. Who is that woman, do you know? A. No sir

Q. Do you want to be left with Mrs. Keevin? A. Yes sir.

CROSS-EXAMINATION:

Q. [Ronald] Mrs. Keevin has taught you to like her has she? A. Yes sir.

Q. She dont want you to like this woman here at all? A. She never said anything to me to like another woman.

Q. She dont want you to know but that she is your mama does she? If that other woman was your mother you would like her? Mrs. Keevin has talked to you a good deal about the other woman has she not? A. No sir.

Q. Did she ever let you come in to see the other woman? A. Yes sir.

Q. Never sent you out of the room? A. No sir.

Q. Always let you get on the other woman's lap did she? - I did not get on the other woman's lap.

[Mary Keevin, 20. [] 76--119.  

Q. [Gilman] Why did you not want to get on the other woman's lap?

The COURT: That is not material.
