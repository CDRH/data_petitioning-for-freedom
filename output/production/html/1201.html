  • Summary of Proceedings: In the summer of 1920, Alson Cole was awaiting execution for first-degree murder in the Nebraska State Penitentiary in Lincoln. He petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus in federal court, alleging numerous violations of his rights by the Howard County district court. The state countered that Cole had already been denied habeas relief in federal court. The judge, however, found that the state district court had erred by sentencing Cole to death after he pled guilty to murder without having had a hearing to determine the degree of the guilt--in other words, that Cole had only pled guilty to murder, not murder in the first degree. Cole was remanded to the custody of the Howard County sheriff for re-sentencing.
