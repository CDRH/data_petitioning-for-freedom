  • Summary of Proceedings: In December 1917, the Clatsop County Juvenile Court had thirteen-year-old Gertrude Hansen committed to the State Industrial Training School in Salem as a delinquent minor. In September 1919, Hansen was transferred to the State Institution for the Feeble Minded in the same city. The following summer, William Zook, a man claiming to be Hansen's husband, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on her behalf, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, Hansen was remanded to the custody of the superintendent of the state institution.
