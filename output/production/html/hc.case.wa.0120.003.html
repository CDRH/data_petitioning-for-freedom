#1384

In District Court of Columbia County Washington territory, holding terms at Dayton.

Before Hon. William G. Langford Judge of above named Court

In the matter of the
application of
L. T. Savacool for
Writ of Habeas Corpus

Complaint

by the Hon William G. Langford Judge of the above named Court and associate Justice of the Subpoena Court of the Territory of Washington

The petition of L. T. Savacool the plaintiff respectfully shows:

1 That he is illegally imprisoned, detained confined and restrained of this liberty by J. H. Hosler, Sheriff of Columbia County Washington Territory; he is confined by said Sheriff in the county jail at the City of Dayton in the county and territory aforesaid.

2nd That the cause or pretense of such restrain according to the best of his knowledge and belief of the applicant is this; this applicant was brought before William Newland one of the Justices of the Peace in and for Garfield County, Washington Territory, for examination upon a charge of uttering and publishing as true a deed for property and upon such[?] examination by said Justice of the Peace ordered this said applicant be held to answer said charge before the grand jury of Garfield County, And the said Sheriff J.H. Hosler   claims & should keep, imprison and restrain applicant of this liberty - by writing a commitment issued by said Justice of the Peace authorizing him into his custody and safely keep said applicant.

3= That such restraint is illegal in this, the said instrument of which this applicant is charged with of having uttered and published action, so not[?] a deed for property - or any one of the instruments mentioned in section 854 of the Code of Washington.

That said Sheriff J.H. Hosler has on writ and Committment issued by said Justice of the Peace and all authorizing or empowering him the said Sheriff imprison, detain or restrain said applicant of his liberty - or at all

Wherefor he prays for a writ of Habeas Corpus that the same may be inquired into and he be discharged

L. T. Savacool
Petitioner

Territory of Washington County of Columbia } SS

L.T. Savacool being first duly sworn says he is petitioner and applicant above named this the foregoing petition or complaint is true as he verily believes.

L. T. Savacool

subscribed and sworn to before me this 18 day of February 1886

Geo B. Baker

Notary Public
