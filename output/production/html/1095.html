  • Summary of Proceedings: Tony Butera, brother of Sam Butera, filed this petition on 8 July 1914 which stated that Sam was being held in the jail of the City of Omaha by Henry W. Dunn (Chief of Police), Steve Maloney (Chief of Detectives), and Edward Morris (Fire Warden) without any warrant or legal authority. The petition states that the aforementioned parties would not allow anyone to see Sam and they would not fix a bond amount. Judge Charles Leslie ordered that the writ be issued and directed at the aforementioned parties (Dunn, Maloney, and Morris). Sheriff McShane stated that he delivered the writ to Dunn and Maloney, but was unable to locate Morris. On 8 July 1914, Judge Leslie stated that Sam Butera was being held on the charge of arson. He ordered that Sam be released from imprisonment on a $2,000.00 “hand in sum,” with the promise to appear in Court the following day. Sebastian Salerno was listed as the surety of this bond. NOTE: The Butera case file erroneously contains two documents from an unrelated case that was tried before a Nebraska juvenile court in the late 1930s.
