  • Summary of Proceedings: William P. Keif was arrested for allegedly selling liquor without a license, and was jailed on the order of Police Judge William J. Houston. Keif petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus. He claimed that Marshall Phillip H. Cooper was illegally detaining him and that his right to due process was violated. Judge Samuel M. Chapman remanded him into custody but ruled that Keif could enter into his own recognizance for a sum of $200.
