  • Summary of Proceedings: Salvatore Giampapa was arrested on December 19th, 1923, after fleeing the scene of the murder of a man named Gibilterra. Following a preliminary hearing for the trial of Gibilterra's murder, Giampapa filed a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus for being unlawfully detained by the Douglas County Sheriff, Michael Endres. DeGeorge claimed that he was wrongly being charged with First-degree murder on the basis of insufficient evidence and that the court had failed to follow proper procedure by not setting the price of his bail. Despite the Habeas Corpus petition being granted, his writ was denied as the judge deemed that the warrant for his arrest was valid and he should be held until the outcome of the murder trial. Giampapa remained in the custody of the Douglas Country Sheriff.
