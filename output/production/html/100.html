  • Summary of Proceedings: On October 25th, 1918 , Pete Wolf, along with three co-defendants, were arrested on the charge of being inmates of an ill-governed house. On the 26th, Police Magistrate Ray J. Madden sentenced Wolf to sixty days in the Douglas County Jail for the crime. On November 2nd, a petition for a writ of Habeas Corpus was filed by Wolf and his attorney alleging that the Police Magistrate did not have jurisdiction in the case. Within the same day, Judge Redick issued an order releasing the bound party. It is unclear what the nature of this ill-governed house charge is with respect to a male being charged.
