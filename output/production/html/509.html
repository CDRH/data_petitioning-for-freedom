  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 30, 1921, Charles Saap was arrested and accused of having alchohol in his possesion. On August 1, he was found guilty by Police Magistrate Charles Foster and ordered to pay a fine. He appealed the decision, paid the appeal bond, and was released from custody. On September 26, Judge L. B. Day decided that the appeal bond was defective and dismissed it for want of jurisdiction. Charles was then remanded to the custody of the respondents. On September 29, Charles’ wife, Alvina, petitioned on his behalf for a writ of habeas corpus. The writ was dismissed for want of prosecution and Charles’ fate is unknown.
