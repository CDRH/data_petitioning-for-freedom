  • Summary of Proceedings: The Board of Commissioners for Gosper County, Nebraska found that John Wilken was a dipsomaniac, or alcoholic, and had him admitted to the Nebraska State Hospital for the Insane "until cured but not exceeding three years." Wilken petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. After hearing the evidence, the judge ruled that while the hospital could admit and detain Wilken, the Board of Commissioners had exceeded their authority in sentencing him for a period of time. The writ was denied and Wilken ordered to be returned to Gosper County to appear a second time before the Board of Commissioners.
