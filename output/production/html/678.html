  • Summary of Proceedings: Institutionalized in the Nebraska State Hospital for the Insane, Thomas Walton petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against superintendent James L. Greene. Judge Frost granted the petition. When Walton was judged insane by a commission, they ruled that he was not dangerous and could be released. Walton's assigned guardian, John Taylor, also claimed that Walton had the means to provide for himself if released. After hearing this evidence, Judge Frost denied the writ and ordered that Walton be remanded back into the custody of Greene.
