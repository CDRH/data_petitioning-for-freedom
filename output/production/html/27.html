  • Summary of Proceedings: Cornelius O'Keefe alleged that on August 18, 1895, John McKinnon stole four meat cattle from him, and O'Keefe filed a complaint against McKinnon on August 24 in the County of Yale, British Columbia. McKinnon was arrested that same day, after being found with hides that bore a brand that belonged to Mr. O'Keefe. On December 22, 1896, E.C. Simmons filed another complaint against McKinnon alleging that he committed the aforementioned crime and Arthur H. Kenyon, commissioner of the circuit court of the United States for the District of Washington, issued a warrant for McKinnon's extradition and arrest to face charges for larceny. McKinnon appeared before Kenyon on December 23, and his attorneys alleged that the warrant did not state sufficient facts to constitute a crime, and that Kenyon had no right to authorize McKinnon's extradition or a warrant for his arrest due to a treaty between the United States and Great Britain.
