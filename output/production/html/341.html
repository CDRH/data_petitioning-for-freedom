  • Summary of Proceedings: In September 1920, Harry L. Hunsaker filed a petition for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of his daughter, Genevieve Hunsaker. The petition stated that Harry L. Hunsaker was awarded the custody of Genevieve in 1919 after the divorce to her mother. Harry entered an agreement with Walter W. Hamilton, where Hamilton agreed to keep Harry's three children together in his home. Hamilton violated this agreement and gave Genevive to the Nebraska Children's Home Society, which gave custody to respondent Pearl Beck. The petition alleges Pearl is a woman of bad character and unlawfully restraining Genevieve in her custody. The case was dismissed for want of prosecution in 1922.
