St. Louis Law Commissioners' Court

April Term 1854, No. 92

Wilson, Mary,

In the matter of

Habeas Corpus

Defendant arrested on oath of John J. Schuler as a fugitive slave; defendant was a free person of color who had lived in St. Louis for about 9 years; petition claimed that Schuler had "malicious" motives in having her arrested because defendant refused to pay him the fee for services he claimed she owed; physical description included; defendant supposedly was employed on steamboat "Isabel" as a chambermaid
