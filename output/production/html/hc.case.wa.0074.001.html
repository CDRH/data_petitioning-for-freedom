In the District Court of the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, holding terms at Seattle

In the matter of the application of Lee Kee for a writ of Habeas Corpus

Petition

To the Honorable Roger S Greene sole presiding Judge of said Court.

The petition of Harold Preston respectfully represents I. That the said Lee Kee is unlawfully imprisoned detained confined and restrained of his liberty by James H Wooley Chief of Police of the city of Seattle, and James Bogart jailer of said city. at the city jail in the city of Seattle in King County Washington Territory.

II. That the cause or pretence of the aforesaid confinement and restraint of said Lee Kee according to your petitioners best knowledge and belief is by virtue of an alleged commitment, a copy of which is hereto annexed marked "Exhibit A" and made part hereof.

III. That your petitioner is advised and verily believes that the imprisonment of the said Lee Kee is illegal and that such illegality consists in

I. That the offense charged in said alleged commitment of Lee[?] constitutes no crime for which the said Lee Kee should be restrained of his liberty.

II. That the court from which said alleged commitment issued had no jurisdiction of the said alleged offense

 

III. That the said alleged offense is ^attempts to be made such by an by an ordinance of the city of Seattle viz Ordinance 618 entitled an ordinance to prevent the the smoking or inhaling of opium [?] November 21st 1884. Which said ordinance is without authority of law and is void.

Wherefor your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may be granted directed to the said James H Woolery; chief of the police of the city of Seattle and to James Bogart Jailer of said city commanding them to have the body of the said Lee Kee before your Honor at a time and place herein to be specified to do and receive what shall then and there be considered before your Honor together with the time and cause of the said Lee Kee's detention and said writ[?]: and that the said Lee Kee may be restored to his liberty. Dated at Seattle May 25 of 1885.

Harold Preston. On behalf of said Lee Kee.

Harold Preston & Jas H Lewis Attys for Petitioner

Territory of Washington County of King } ss

Harold Preston, being first duly sworn on his oath says, That he is the subscriber[?] to the above petitioner. That he wrote such petitions on behalf of the said Lee Kee: That he has read the said petition and believes the allegations therein contained to be true.

Harold Preston

Subscribed in my presence and sworn to before me on this 25th day of 1885. G A Hill Notary Public
