State of Missouri County of St Louis Co } To the Hon: the Law Commissioner's Court of St Louis County

The Petition of Adam Riley respectfully sheweth: That your Petitioner is a free man of color - born in Indianapolis state of Indiana - that he has been accustomed to run the river as fireman on steam boats between Cincinnati & St Louis for the last three or four years - that he was arrested in this city on the 5th instant as a fugitive slave, taken before C. C. Simmons Recorder of the City of St Louis & by him committed to the jail of St. Louis County kept by James Castello where your petitioner was confined and is now confined - that he is illegally imprisoned and unjustly restrained of his liberty. That your petitioner demanded a copy of the commitment & the evidence against him (if any) but was refused. Your Petitioner further states that none of his family as far as he can learn were ever held in slavery & that no person claims your petitioner as a slave - that he can prove that he is free-. Your petitioner prays that a writ of Habeas Corpus may issue directed to the said James Castello commanding him to bring the body of your Petitioner before your Honor & on its appearing that he is illegally held that your Petitioner may be set free.

R.S. MacDonald

Atty for Adam Riley.
