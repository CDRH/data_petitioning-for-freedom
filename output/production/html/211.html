  • Summary of Proceedings: Martin D. Reilly and Christy Reilly, the petitioners, adopted Ruth Reilly in October of 1920 with a decree from the Juvenile Divison of the Circuit Court of New Madrid County, Missouri. Ruth Reilly was then unlawfully detained by Mrs. Elizabeth Reilly, Martin's mother, and Mrs. Ellen Dennett, his sister. Martin and Christy Reilly then filed a writ of Habeas Corpus is issued so they could possess sole custody of Ruth. Judge Leslie ordered the writ of Habeas Corpus to be allowed, and Martin and Christy Reilly were issued custody of Ruth in 1922. In 1923 they file the same petition with the same details. One addition is a response from Ellen Dennett stating that she did not know that Martin and Christy Reilly were the legally adopting parents of Ruth and that they had abandoned the child in May of 1921 when she began to care for and provide for her. Prior to Dennett's statement on the 15th of November, Judge Day oversaw the case and approved the writ of Habeas Corpus, ordering Ruth to be returned to the custody of Martin and Christy Reilly on the 6th of November, 1923. In 1924 the case is dismissed for want of prosecution.
