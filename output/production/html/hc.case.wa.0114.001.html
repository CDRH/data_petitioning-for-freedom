2427

Territory of Washington, )
Second Judicial District, )

In the District Court holding terms at Olympia.

Application of the United States Attorney to compel certain persons to give recognizance, with sureties, for their appearance to testify as witnesses in a cause in which the United States is a party.

To Hon. John P. Hoyt, Judge of the above entitled District Court:

Comes now John B. Allen, United States Attorney for Washington Territory, by his Assistant, C.H. Hanford, and respectfully showeth unto your Honor: That William Jameison, Peter Daly and William Winters have been accused on oath of having, at Seattle, in the Third Judicial District of Washington Territory, on or about the 31st day of January, 1885, committed the crime of conspiracy to defraud the United States, and have been held a to appear and answer for said crime at the next regular term of the District Court holding terms at Seattle; and said complaint will be submitted to the Grand Jury at said term of said District Court, to be held at Seattle, aforesaid, commencing on the 1st Monday of April, 1885; That William Jones, William H. Doyle, Fred. Williams and Alfred Wood and each of them, are necessary and competent witnesses for the United States on the trial of said criminal proceeding:

Wherefore the said United States Attorney makes application unto your Honor to issue a warrant and to do whatsoever may be necessary to compel each of the said witnesses to give their recognizances, with sufficient sureties, to appear to testify in said criminal proceeding.

John B Allen

United States Attorney for Washington Territory,

By C.H. Hanford
His Assistant.
