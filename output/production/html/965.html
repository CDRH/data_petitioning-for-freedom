  • Summary of Proceedings: According to the appearance docket, on July 5th, 1902, Milton LaMaster, a seventy-four-year-old man declared insane, petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus against James L. Greene, superintendent of the Nebraska Home for the Insane. Judge Holmes presumably granted the petition because the docket claims that Greene received a copy. On July 15th, Milton moved to dismiss the cause, which Judge Cornish granted. A simultaneous legal action, "In the Matter of the guardianship of Milton F. Lamaster, an insane person," exists in the records.
