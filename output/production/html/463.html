  • Summary of Proceedings: Edna M. Gerould, the mother of Russell G. Watson, had been married to Fred Watson and, shortly after the birth of Russell, put him in the care of his grandmother Susan C. Watson. Later, Edna divorced Fred Watson and married Percy E. Gerould. Edna M. Gerould then filed for a writ of habeas corpus to regain custody of her son, Russell, from Susan. Susan claimed that Edna is a prostitute and of poor character. Several witnesses spoke to the court on the merits of Edna and Percy Gerould's reputation, stating that they were well-equipped to care for children and that Edna was a well-respected lady. Edna M. Gerould was then awarded custody of her child, Russell G. Watson, and it was found that Susan C. Watson was unlawfully in possession of the child. Gerould was then permitted to move said child from the city of Omaha to the city of Hastings.
