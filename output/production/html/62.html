  • Summary of Proceedings: In May 1902, the Umatilla County Circuit Court sentenced R. E. Berry to six years in the Oregon State Penitentiary for "uttering forged evidence of debt." In June 1907, he petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus, which was granted. Berry argued that he had served his time after accounting for a sentencing reduction as stipulated by a state law passed earlier that year. After hearing the evidence, Berry was released.
