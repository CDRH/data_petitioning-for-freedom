  • Summary of Proceedings: On July 10, 1921, Robert McCullough was taken in by Omaha police officers Graham and Franks and confined to the city jail. He was later moved to the Omaha Detention Home, an institution designed to treat those with "social diseases," but which bore many resemblances to a jail. On July 11, 1921, McCullough filed a writ of habeus corpus against the commissioner of police, health, and sanitation Henry W. Dunn, chief of police Michael F. Dempsey, and health officers Dr. Thomas D. Boler and Dr. A.S. Pinto, who worked at the Detention Home. On the 12th, the application was dismissed on the motion of the petitioner.
