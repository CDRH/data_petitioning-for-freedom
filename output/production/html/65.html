  • Summary of Proceedings: On March 30th, 1920, Carl Gunther was arrested in Omaha under the order of the Omaha Chief of Detectives for being a fugitive from the state of Missouri. Gunther argued in his petition for freedom that he had no complaint filed against him and no bond set for his release. The judge agreed to his plea and had Detective Dunn bring him to court. Though the petition outcome is not contained in the case file, the appearance docket shows that the writ was denied; Gunther remained in custody as the Omaha police processed his extradition to Missouri.
