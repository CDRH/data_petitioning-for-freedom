  • Summary of Proceedings: In November 1918, Carl Berg petitioned for a writ of habeas corpus on the grounds he was being unlawfully restrained of his liberty by Henry M. White, U.S. Commissioner of Labor. Berg was a subject of the King of Sweden, and was arrested by an Immigration Inspector, charged with being an alien and having advocated or taught the unlawful destruction of property. The petition alleged Berg demanded a copy of said evidence against him to attach to the petition but was denied by the respondent. Judge Neterer denied the writ, and Berg was remanded to the custody of the Commissioner of Immigration.
