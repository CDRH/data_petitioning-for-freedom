  • Summary of Proceedings: William Johnson was detained on suspicion of having committed a crime on February 24th, 1920. There was a complaint filed against him the next day for statutory rape. A preliminary trial was held and Nettie Vacirca, a 14-year-old girl and alleged victim, testified along with Vosburgh and Hopkins who both work in the police station and heard William Johnson allegedly voluntarily admitted to the crime. Johnson petitioned for habeas corpus on February 27th, 1920, arguing that there was insufficient evidence for a sustained trial and that the alleged confession was extrajudicial and coerced. His petition was denied so he motioned for a new trial on March 4th, 1920. On March 6th, 1920, he filed again for habeas corpus. The second petition was allowed and a return was sent, but the outcome of the second petition is unknown.
