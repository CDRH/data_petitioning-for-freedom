EXHIBIT B

UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT WESTERN DISTRICT OF WASHINGTON NORTHERN DIVISION

In the Matter of the Application of Tonka Bajlo for the Writ of Habeas Corpus )

2459 No. 1328

AFFIDAVIT OF NICK RADOVICH

STATE OF WASHINGTON COUNTY OF KING ) ss.

Nick Radovich being first duly swon on oath deposes and says: That I am a citizen of the United States and a resident of Seattle, an Austrian by birth and have resided here about 28 years; that I raised my family here and am a married man; that I am at present conducting a lunch counter in the Frog Solo Bar, corner of 3rd and Pike Streets; that on monday, April 28th, 1913, Chas. H. Ennis, in company with Jack Bajlo, called upon me at my place of business and requested me to act as interpreter at an interview, and in reply to my questions he Jack Bajlo gave me the following answers:

Q. Why do you say your wife is a dirty old whore? A. Because she will not live with me.

Q. Is that the only reason why you say she is a dirty whore? A. Yes.

Q. Would you call her a dirty, old whore if she would live with you? A. No.

Q. Why did you have her arrested? A. I had her arrested and sent back home because she would not live with me.

Q. Then if she was willing to live you you would not send her back home? A. No. I do same all old country style. Make her fear me. She know I boss.

I asked him who told him that his wife was sleeping with the men where she worked and he said another Austrian, and then I asked him who he was and he said that he did not know his name, and then I asked him where said Austrian could be found and he said that he did not know. When questioned about the men, who they were, and where they could be found he did not seem to have   anyone in mind at all; that he seemed to be lying and not at all sincere about his statements that his wife was a prostitue, or that she had been living with these men, and he said that previous to their marriage he knew her to be a virtuous girl.

N Radovich

Subscribed and sworn to before me this 2nd day of May, 1913.

Chas H Ennis
Notary Public in and for the State of Washington, residing at Seattle.
