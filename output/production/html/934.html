  • Summary of Proceedings: Pearl Weaver was charged in early June 1918 with vagrancy and prostitution by Police Magistrate Roy Madden. As a result, she was remanded to a jail for a period of time specified to be "until cured." On June 15th 1918, a petition was filed by Weaver and her attorney, Keslo A. Morgan. That writ was allowed and it was ordered that she be released from custody. Sheriff Michael R. Clark brought the writ of return to the court on June 22nd 1918.
