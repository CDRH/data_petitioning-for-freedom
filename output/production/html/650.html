  • Summary of Proceedings: Francisco and Carolina Marino petitioned for a writ of Habeas Corpus, claiming that their minor son, Joseph, was being held unlawfully on the U.S. naval vessel "Wisconsin." They were represented by the lawfirm Preston, Carr, and Gilman, but their individual attorney is not named in the documents. The respondent, Lt. Cmdr. H. T. Mayo of the Wisconsin claimed and provided evidence that Joseph claimed to be "eighteen years and one month" and swore an oath to that effect at his enlistment. The respondent also stated that they would return Joseph if it was demonstrated that he was a minor, but that he would then be "liable for punishment by a Naval Court Martial" for fraudulent enlistment. Judge Hanford ruled that Joseph was a minor and canceled his enlistment. It is unknown whether Joseph did face a naval proceeding.
